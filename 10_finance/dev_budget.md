# 10_finance/dev_budget.md

## Purpose

Define Wave 1 development-phase finance assumptions and the initial package-building budget for `WE OUT HERE` before full production greenlight. This document is meant to anchor early spend discipline while creating enough material to raise, partner, cast, and budget the series credibly.

## Canon Assumptions

- Title: `WE OUT HERE`
- Format: `6 x 30 min` season
- Production model: indie-to-lower-mid-budget professional production
- Assumed total season range: `$1.5M-$3M`
- Target production start: `Q3 2026`
- Target delivery: `Q1-Q2 2027`

## Development Budget Objective

Wave 1 should fund the minimum serious package required to:

- lock creative direction and written materials
- establish legal and finance readiness
- produce investor / partner-facing materials
- prepare for early casting conversations
- create a credible visual and digital presentation layer

This is not a prep or production budget. It covers development work through an initial financing/packaging decision gate.

## Recommended Wave 1 Development Range

- Low working case: `$135,000`
- Base working case: `$171,000`
- High working case: `$212,000`

The base case is the recommended planning number. It is high enough to support a professional package, but still disciplined relative to a `$1.5M-$3M` season.

## Recommended Planning Adjustment

The strict base-line item rollup lands at `$186,000`, which is acceptable for a serious indie package. If the team needs a firmer Wave 1 cap closer to the middle of market expectations, reduce scope as follows:

- bring pitch materials from `$14,000` to `$12,000`
- bring lookbook / branding from `$18,000` to `$16,000`
- bring proof-of-concept UI design from `$20,000` to `$18,000`
- hold web presence at `$10,000` instead of `$12,000`
- reduce contingency from `$15,000` to `$10,000`

This yields a practical target Wave 1 cap of **`$171,000`**.

## Budget Assumptions

- Writing spend assumes experienced indie-level writers or writer-producers, not speculative unpaid development.
- Legal assumes competent entertainment counsel and formation support, but not major financing closings.
- Pitch and brand materials assume professional external design support rather than purely internal founder labor.
- UI design is concept-stage only, not software engineering or product build.
- Web presence assumes a polished promotional presence, not a custom platform.
- Casting prep assumes packaging support and materials prep, not formal casting sessions for full series hire.
- Consulting assumes outside review strong enough to avoid amateur investor materials and weak structural assumptions.

## Spend Timing

**Phase A: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)**

- Legal formation and chain-of-title setup
- Writing kickoff and story architecture
- Brand direction start
- Expected spend: `20%-25%`

**Phase B: Package Build (Weeks 5-10)**

- Pilot and season materials progress
- Deck development
- Lookbook build
- UI concept work
- Expected spend: `40%-45%`

**Phase C: Market Readiness (Weeks 11-16)**

- Web presence launch
- Casting prep materials
- Finance/legal review pass
- Packaging revisions
- Expected spend: `25%-30%`

**Reserve Holdback**

- Contingency should remain unspent until a clear packaging gap or timing issue appears.
- Expected uncommitted reserve through midpoint: at least `50%` of contingency.

## Cost-Control Notes

- Do not commission full proof-of-concept production in Wave 1 unless financing conversations clearly require it.
- Keep UI work to frames, flows, and visual concepts; avoid engineering spend.
- Use one lead designer across deck, lookbook, and site where possible to reduce rework.
- Cap external revision rounds in writing and design scopes before work starts.
- Sequence consultant engagement around decisions; do not retain advisors broadly without defined deliverables.
- Delay paid casting outreach until character breakdowns and package positioning are stable.

## Decision Gates

**Gate 1: Development Readiness**

Required before material spend accelerates:

- project entity and rights posture are clean enough to proceed
- pilot premise and season engine are approved internally
- target budget lane remains inside `$1.5M-$3M`

**Gate 2: Package Readiness**

Required before broad external outreach:

- pilot draft and season materials are presentation-ready
- pitch deck and lookbook support the intended market position
- finance assumptions pass advisor sanity check

**Gate 3: Market Engagement / Greenlight to Next Spend**

Required before moving into heavier prep or attachment costs:

- at least one credible financing, platform, distributor, or producing-partner path is active
- casting strategy is aligned to budget reality
- revised top-sheet production budget indicates the series can still be executed in range

## Finance Interpretation

For a series targeting a `$1.5M-$3M` season, a Wave 1 development budget in the `~$150K-$190K` band is credible. It is significant enough to signal professionalism, but still conservative relative to the risk of entering the market with underbuilt materials.

Recommended working number for approvals: **`$171,000` cap`** with release in tranches against the decision gates above.
