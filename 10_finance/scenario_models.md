# 10_finance/scenario_models.md

## Purpose

Stress-test the economics of `WE OUT HERE` across downside, base, and upside release outcomes so the team and prospective investors can evaluate the project on realistic ranges instead of a single optimistic case.

## Content

### Modeling Assumptions

- Control budget: **`$2,250,000`**
- Equity at risk: **`$1,200,000`**
- Investor premium target: **`20%`**
- Modeled term: `5 years` from release
- Figures below are directional and exclude tax consequences at investor level

### Scenario Summary

| Scenario | Release Outcome | Gross Receipts | Net Receipts To Waterfall | Investor Outcome |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Downside | Digital-first / modest niche performance | $1,450,000 | $950,000 | Partial recoup only |
| Base | Festival launch -> mid-level streamer/distributor acquisition | $3,350,000 | $2,350,000 | Principal plus premium, modest backend |
| Upside | Strong festival response -> premium acquisition plus ancillary tail | $5,400,000 | $4,000,000 | Strong recoupment and meaningful backend |

### Net Receipt Bridge

The difference between gross and net assumes a standard drag from:

- distribution fees
- delivery and release expenses
- sales expenses
- senior bridge repayment and financing costs where applicable

### Scenario 1: Downside

#### Commercial shape

- No meaningful streamer acquisition.
- Release occurs through an indie distributor, aggregator, or self-managed digital-first path.
- Revenue builds slowly through TVOD, AVOD, small licensing opportunities, and long-tail audience conversion.

#### Downside model

| Line | Amount |
| --- | ---: |
| Gross receipts | $1,450,000 |
| Less distribution fees and expenses | ($350,000) |
| Less debt/bridge repayment and finance costs | ($150,000) |
| **Net receipts to equity waterfall** | **$950,000** |

#### Result

- Investors do not fully recoup principal within the modeled term.
- Equity recovery: about `79%` of principal.
- Producer backend: effectively none.

Interpretation:

This is the honest failure-to-medium case. The project survives commercially, but it does not clear the full investor hurdle inside the modeled window.

### Scenario 2: Base

#### Commercial shape

- Festival exposure creates validation and discoverability.
- A streamer, digital distributor, or premium AVOD/SVOD buyer acquires the series at a respectable indie price.
- Additional tail comes from secondary territories and library exploitation.

#### Base model

| Line | Amount |
| --- | ---: |
| Domestic or primary acquisition | $2,700,000 |
| Secondary / international / ancillary | $650,000 |
| **Gross receipts** | **$3,350,000** |
| Less distribution fees and expenses | ($650,000) |
| Less debt/bridge repayment and finance costs | ($350,000) |
| **Net receipts to equity waterfall** | **$2,350,000** |

#### Result under recommended waterfall

- Investor principal returned: `$1,200,000`
- Investor premium returned: `$240,000`
- Remaining receipts after preferred return: `$910,000`
- Under a 50/50 corridor and shared backend approach, investors receive an additional meaningful profit share.

Estimated investor total return: **approximately `$1.70M-$1.90M`**, or **`1.4x-1.6x`** on invested equity.

Interpretation:

This is the target business case. It does not assume a breakout sale. It assumes a credible indie-series acquisition and enough discipline that the backend still matters.

### Scenario 3: Upside

#### Commercial shape

- Strong festival response or strong buyer competition.
- Premium acquisition price relative to budget.
- Better ancillary performance because the show's cultural angle travels through comedy, tech, and internet-native press.

#### Upside model

| Line | Amount |
| --- | ---: |
| Premium or competitive acquisition | $4,500,000 |
| Ancillary and secondary revenue | $900,000 |
| **Gross receipts** | **$5,400,000** |
| Less distribution fees and expenses | ($900,000) |
| Less debt/bridge repayment and finance costs | ($500,000) |
| **Net receipts to equity waterfall** | **$4,000,000** |

#### Result under recommended waterfall

- Investor principal and premium are fully paid.
- Large remaining pool flows through the corridor and shared backend.
- Producer side meaningfully participates without undermining investor economics.

Estimated investor total return: **approximately `$2.40M-$2.90M`**, or **`2.0x-2.4x`** on invested equity.

Interpretation:

This is a successful indie outcome, not a unicorn case. It represents strong reception and disciplined execution in a lane where budget scale remains manageable.

### Sensitivity Levers

The variables that most affect returns are:

- final equity amount actually at risk
- whether an MG closes before production
- realized tax incentive value
- distribution fee and expense leakage
- whether the project lands a genuine festival-to-buyer handoff

### Decision Use

This model is most useful for three decisions:

1. Whether to hold the budget at `$2.25M` or push lower.
2. Whether investor terms remain fair relative to downside risk.
3. Whether digital-first fallback is economically survivable.

### Working Conclusion

At the recommended budget, `WE OUT HERE` is not risk-free, but it is economically coherent. The downside is survivable, the base case is attractive enough for rational indie investors, and the upside is meaningful without requiring fantasy-level revenue assumptions.
