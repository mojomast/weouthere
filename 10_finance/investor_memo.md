### Executive Summary

`WE OUT HERE` is a 6 x 30 min dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy about three burned-out technologists who launch a chaotic AI consultancy from `Backchannel` and bootstrap temporary legitimacy through manic hustle, pseudo-legal improvisation, and internet-native performance.

The proposed first season is designed as a **contained, desktop-heavy hybrid production** with real cost efficiencies from recurring interiors, screenlife storytelling, and a tight ensemble. The recommended budget is **`$2.25M`**, with a target release path of **festival premiere -> streamer acquisition** and a credible fallback of **digital-first rollout**.

This memo frames the project as a disciplined indie-media investment: creatively distinctive, budget-aware, and built for multiple revenue outcomes rather than one speculative exit.

### Investment Thesis

| Thesis Point | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| Distinctive concept in an active cultural lane | AI, labor anxiety, and tech satire remain commercially legible topics |
| Contained production design | Higher production value per dollar than a location-heavy ensemble comedy |
| Short order season | Lower financing burden and faster path to market than traditional 8-10 episode orders |
| Buyer-friendly format | Half-hour episodes are adaptable for streamers, festivals, and digital platforms |
| More than one exit path | Project is not dependent on a single streamer greenlight scenario |

### Why the Budget Is Rational

The budget is not low because the team is pretending production is free. It is controlled because the concept supports:

- a limited core cast
- recurring contained spaces
- a large proportion of screen-based storytelling
- efficient block shooting
- stylized but finite VFX/UI needs rather than large-scale asset creation

The budget still protects the categories that matter most on this show:

- polished UI and screenlife execution
- strong editorial and sound finishing
- professional cast and crew compensation
- contingency against indie-series volatility

### Return Logic

Investor economics are expected to flow through a standard controlled waterfall:

1. Gross receipts collected through a collection account or equivalent controlled process.
2. Distribution fees and approved distribution expenses deducted.
3. Senior debt or bridge repayment, if any.
4. Equity investors recoup principal.
5. Equity investors receive a negotiated premium or preferred return.
6. Remaining net profits split between investors and producer/talent participations.

The exact waterfall is set out in `10_finance/recoupment_waterfall.md`.

### Revenue Paths

Primary case:

- festival launch creates buyer validation
- streamer or distributor acquires domestic rights
- ancillary revenue comes from international, AVOD/TVOD tail, and library exploitation

Fallback case:

- distributor or aggregator-supported digital release
- project monetizes through TVOD, AVOD, small licenses, and long-tail audience build
- investors may take longer to recoup, but the project retains more control and optionality

### Key Risks

| Risk | Management Response |
| --- | --- |
| Financing gap before production | Majority-equity close targeted before prep; budget can scale down if necessary |
| Cash timing mismatch | Bridge facility modeled in advance; tax incentive timing not ignored |
| No streamer acquisition | Digital-first scenario modeled as a real fallback, not a hand-wave |
| VFX/UI scope creep | UI and surreal work kept in controlled envelope with design lock before principal |
| Market contraction | Budget kept in a range where moderate outcomes still matter |

### What Makes This Investor-Friendly

- Honest downside modeling rather than hype-based assumptions.
- Budget discipline aligned with the format.
- Multiple monetization scenarios.
- A recoupment structure that can be understood quickly.
- Clear legal/entity assumptions designed to support rights hygiene and clean chain of title.

### Investor Fit

Best fit investors are:

- media angels
- indie film/TV investors comfortable with development and market risk
- strategic investors who understand comedy, internet-native audiences, or digital distribution

Poor fit investors are:

- control-seeking investors expecting majority governance for minority capital
- investors who require premium-streamer economics to justify the check
- investors uncomfortable with independent-market timing and uncertainty

### Disclaimer

This memo is a working project summary only. It is not a securities offering document, not legal advice, and not a guarantee of return. Any formal fundraising must be structured and reviewed by qualified securities, entertainment, and tax counsel.
