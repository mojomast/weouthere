## Content

### Finance Objective

Finance the first season of `WE OUT HERE` at a level that preserves the show's hybrid visual identity, protects labor and post quality, and keeps the total negative cost low enough to remain sellable and recoupable.

Recommended control budget: **`$2,250,000`**.

### Why This Project Is Financeable

- The season is only `180 finished minutes`, not a long-order network bet.
- The concept is marketable in both comedy and tech-culture lanes.
- The contained-location and screenlife components create real cost efficiencies.
- The budget sits in an indie lane where a credible acquisition or digital rollout can still matter economically.
- The format supports a proof-of-voice and buyer-ready package without requiring blockbuster scale.

### Preferred Financing Stack

| Source | Target Amount | % of Budget | Status Logic |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Private equity investors | $1,200,000 | 53.3% | Primary risk capital |
| Transferable or refundable state tax incentive, net of discount | $360,000 | 16.0% | Assumes production in a competitive incentive jurisdiction |
| Soft money, grants, brand support, or cultural funds | $190,000 | 8.4% | Non-dilutive or lightly encumbered capital |
| Distribution MG / pre-license / sales advance | $400,000 | 17.8% | Preferably triggered by package strength, pilot, or festival path |
| Producer deferrals or internal fee deferrals | $100,000 | 4.4% | Limited, disciplined, and documented |
| **Total** | **$2,250,000** | **100.0%** |  |

### Source Notes

#### Private equity investors - target `$1.2M`

- Core financing source.
- Best structured as a small investor group rather than one controlling investor.
- Should sit behind collection-account management and a transparent recoupment waterfall.

#### State tax incentive - target net `$360K`

- Assumes a jurisdiction capable of delivering a meaningful transferable or refundable incentive.
- Gross credit may be higher, but the budget should model the monetized net after broker discount, legal, and audit costs.
- Should not be treated as optional upside if the production plan depends on it.

#### Soft money / grants / brand support - target `$190K`

- Includes arts/media grants, comedy or episodic labs, cultural funds, in-kind offsets converted to hard savings, and carefully structured brand or service support.
- Must not compromise creative control or distribution eligibility.

#### Distribution MG / pre-license - target `$400K`

- Best case: close part of this against a platform, distributor, or sales agent before or near production.
- If not available pre-shoot, replace with additional equity plus bridge, then treat MG as deleveraging revenue later.

#### Producer deferrals - cap at `$100K`

- Use sparingly.
- Appropriate for selected producer fees, a portion of creator overhead, or backend-convertible compensation.
- Should never hide true cash needs in payroll, insurance, or critical vendor lines.

### Financing Sequence

| Stage | Objective | Minimum Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stage 1 | Close package, entity, legal readiness, and investor materials | Investable package and clean rights posture |
| Stage 2 | Secure anchor equity commitments | At least `$750K` soft-circled or committed |
| Stage 3 | Lock incentive jurisdiction and line budget | Tax-credit lane validated and financeable |
| Stage 4 | Close remaining equity and soft money | Minimum `70%-80%` of budget committed before principal |
| Stage 5 | Layer in MG, advance, or bridge | Cover timing gap and reduce investor exposure |

### Investor Positioning

The pitch to investors is not that `WE OUT HERE` is a guaranteed hit. The pitch is that:

- the project has a distinctive commercial identity
- the budget is controlled
- the team is deliberately building more than one exit path
- the economics are sized for rational indie risk, not prestige-series fantasy

### Capital Structure Principles

- Keep approvals centralized in the production entity.
- Avoid over-fragmented investor classes.
- Use one clear waterfall for all equity participants in the same class unless there is a negotiated senior tranche.
- Cap backend leakage before investor recoupment.
- Route receipts through a collection account or equivalent controlled process.

### Financing Fallbacks

If the preferred stack does not close on schedule, use the following order of fallback:

1. Reduce the budget into the `$1.8M-$2.0M` lane without breaking the format.
2. Replace speculative MG assumptions with additional equity.
3. Move to a pilot-plus-finance strategy instead of full-season greenlight.
4. Shift to a digital-first release model and underwrite economics accordingly.

Do not rely on:

- unpaid labor
- vague "we will make it up in backend" promises
- uncertain crowdfunding as core production capital
- a hope that festival awards will finance completion

### Financeability Tests Before Greenlight

The project is finance-ready only if all of the following are true:

- top-sheet budget and cash flow model reconcile
- entity and chain-of-title posture are clean enough for investors and insurers
- at least one incentive jurisdiction is validated in writing
- investor memo and scenario models show downside honestly
- producer deferrals are documented and limited
- contingency remains real and not already spent on wish-list scope

### Recommended Working Position

`WE OUT HERE` should be financed as a **controlled-risk indie series**, not as an undercapitalized passion project and not as a faux-premium streamer play. The optimal structure is a majority-equity plan supported by incentive value, soft money, and a moderate MG or advance, with a bridge facility sized to manage timing rather than rescue the budget.
