## Content

### Canon Budget Frame

- Title: `WE OUT HERE`
- Format: `6 x 30 min`
- Production model: indie-to-lower-mid-budget professional production
- Total season planning range: `$1.5M-$3.0M`
- Release path: `festival -> streamer acquisition` or `digital-first rollout`
- Production design premise: desktop-heavy hybrid production with contained locations and screenlife efficiencies

### Budget Positioning

Recommended working case for financing and packaging: **`$2,250,000` total season budget**.

Why this number works:

- It sits in the middle of the approved planning range.
- It funds a credible professional production without pretending the show can be made at premium-cable spend levels.
- It preserves the formal ambition of the hybrid format while forcing contained-location discipline.
- It is low enough to support multiple exits: acquisition, MG-backed digital release, or controlled self-distribution.

### Base Case Cost Logic

#### Above the line

- Assumes one lead director for most or all episodes, with modest episodic director fees if not fully consolidated.
- Assumes a 3-lead ensemble with disciplined indie-series rates rather than celebrity pricing.
- Assumes writing expense is partially front-loaded in development and partially capitalized into production.

#### Production staff and crew

- Assumes a lean but fully professional crew.
- Uses selective department compression on desktop-capture days.
- Protects critical roles that directly affect execution quality: 1st AD, DP, production designer, sound mixer, editor, and UPM/line producer.

#### Locations and sets

- Built around 2-3 controllable recurring interiors and a small number of practical swing locations.
- Assumes location strategy favors one metro area, minimal company moves, and block shooting.
- Uses contained locations as the main savings engine rather than underpaying crew.

#### Post and VFX/UI

- Higher than a pure live-action comedy because the show's hybrid form depends on polished chat UI, desktop realism, and surreal insert execution.
- Still materially lower than a conventional VFX-heavy series because effects are interface-driven, stylized, and short-duration rather than asset-heavy world building.

### Cost Per Finished Minute

Base case:

- Total finished runtime: `180 minutes`
- Cost per finished minute: **`$12,500`**
- Cost per episode: **`$375,000 average`**

That is realistic for a formally distinctive indie comedy series that is not trying to compete with premium-platform spend.

### Budget Guardrails

- Do not let ATL expand beyond `27%` of the total unless a specific attachment materially increases sales probability.
- Do not let VFX/UI exceed `10%` of total without cutting scope elsewhere.
- Keep contingency unallocated until prep is substantially complete.
- Protect post sound, color, and UI execution; they are not cosmetic on this show.
- If financing lands below base case, cut shoot complexity before cutting key prep or post functions.

### Go/No-Go Interpretation

| Budget Lane | Interpretation | Recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `$1.5M-$1.8M` | Achievable only with tighter schedule, fewer practical locations, lighter ATL, and reduced surreal/VFX ambition | Use only if financing window forces fast greenlight |
| `$2.0M-$2.35M` | Best balance of ambition, control, and recoupment potential | Recommended lane |
| `$2.5M-$3.0M` | Allows more cast flexibility, schedule resilience, and polish but raises recoupment threshold | Pursue only with stronger distribution confidence |

### Working Conclusion

The recommended packaging number for `WE OUT HERE` is **`$2.25M`**. It is large enough to look and feel professional to buyers and talent, but still lean enough to support realistic investor recoupment under a festival-to-acquisition or digital-first model.
