## Content

### Waterfall Objective

Use a simple, credible waterfall that:

- protects investor confidence
- avoids hidden backend leakage
- preserves enough producer participation to keep the project financeable and motivated
- remains legible to counsel, accountants, and collection-account managers

### Working Definitions

| Term | Working Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Gross Receipts | All monies actually received from exploitation of the series, including license fees, advances, prizes allocable to the series, and ancillary income |
| Distribution Fees | Contractually approved fees paid to distributor, sales agent, aggregator, or platform intermediary |
| Distribution Expenses | Approved, recoupable third-party delivery, marketing, and sales expenses, subject to caps where negotiated |
| Senior Debt | Bridge loans, tax-credit loans, or other senior secured production financing plus approved interest and fees |
| Equity Investors | Cash investors funding the production on agreed equity terms |
| Net Profits | Receipts remaining after the waterfall steps defined below |

### Recommended Waterfall Order

1. **Collection account fees and direct receipt handling costs**
2. **Approved distribution fees**
3. **Approved recoupable distribution and delivery expenses**
4. **Guild residuals, participations payable off the top if contractually unavoidable, and required withholding obligations**
5. **Repayment of senior debt, bridge facilities, and tax-credit financing, including approved interest and fees**
6. **Return of 100% of equity investor principal, pro rata by invested capital**
7. **Payment of investor premium / preferred return of 20% on principal**
8. **50/50 corridor split of remaining receipts between equity investors as a class and producer pool until investors reach 125% total return**
9. **Thereafter, 50% to equity investors and 50% to defined net-profit participants, unless final documents set a different long-tail split**

### Why This Structure Is Recommended

- Step 6 gives investors straightforward principal-first protection.
- Step 7 gives a meaningful but not predatory preferred return.
- Step 8 lets producers and core participants begin sharing once investors are substantially protected.
- The structure is simple enough to explain in one meeting.

### Producer Pool Guidance

Producer-side participation should be carved from the producer half of backend, not from pre-recoupment investor recovery.

Suggested producer-side pool uses:

- showrunner / creator participation
- producing entity participation
- limited talent backend for critical attachments
- any approved employee or advisor bonus pool

Do not promise:

- uncapped producer expenses as recoupable above investors
- informal oral backend commitments
- multiple overlapping waterfalls for different investors absent a clear senior/junior structure

### Distribution Expense Controls

Because distribution leakage is often where indie economics break, the working position should be:

- distribution fees capped to negotiated market norms
- P&A or release spending approved in writing above agreed thresholds
- no affiliate markups without disclosure
- regular reporting and audit rights

### Sample Simplified Formula

For investor conversations, the practical summary is:

1. Money comes in.
2. Sales and distribution costs come out.
3. Debt gets repaid.
4. Investors get principal back.
5. Investors get a 20% premium.
6. Remaining profits split with producers.

That is usually the most useful version in a room.

### Interaction With Financing Stack

This waterfall assumes:

- equity is the primary risk capital
- tax-credit monetization may sit in senior debt position
- any MG or sales advance enters the receipt stream and is handled through the same controlled waterfall
- producer deferrals are either subordinated to investor recoupment or clearly documented elsewhere

### Negotiation Variables

The following terms may move in dealmaking:

- investor premium: `10%-25%`
- corridor threshold: `120%-130%`
- long-tail split after corridor
- treatment of producer deferrals
- approval thresholds for distribution spend

The default recommendation remains conservative and investor-readable: **100% principal back, 20% preferred return, then shared profits**.

### Legal Note

This document is a term-model, not contract language. The enforceable version must be reflected in subscription documents, operating agreement terms, investor side letters if any, distribution agreements, and collection-account instructions.
