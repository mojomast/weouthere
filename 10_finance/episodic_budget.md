### Base Season Assumption

- Total season budget: **`$2,250,000`**
- Episode count: **`6`**
- Average per episode: **`$375,000`**

The show should not be budgeted as six identical half-hours. Certain episodes naturally carry more cost because of initiation spend, heavier practical production, or larger post/UI burden.

### Episode-Level Production Logic

#### S01E01 `Don't Send That` - $410,000

- Carries pilot burden: world introduction, initial asset build, Backchannel interface rules, and recurring production grammar.
- Includes up-front spend on UI templates, screen-capture pipeline, hero wardrobe/set dressing, and higher edit iteration.
- Should shoot efficiently, but it still bears the cost of building the series language.

#### S01E02 `Terms of Abuse` - $360,000

- Benefits from assets already built in Episode 1.
- Can lean on contained interiors, chat sequences, and one or two legal/compliance-oriented practicals.
- Requires careful writing discipline more than expensive physical production.

#### S01E03 `The Manic Defense` - $370,000

- Presentation and client-facing sequences create performance and editorial demands.
- Budget assumes a modest increase in staging and coverage, not a major scale jump.
- Moderate post pressure because the episode depends on the team's ability to sell instability as expertise.

#### S01E04 `Contain Me Daddy` - $395,000

- Likely the most location-sensitive non-pilot episode.
- Advisor, investor, consultant, or governance-facing material may require cleaner environments and more day-player support.
- Carries more schedule risk because mismanaging the containment figure will read flat if coverage is compromised.

#### S01E05 `The Superego Collapse` - $345,000

- Best candidate for controlled spend.
- Emotional and comic collapse can be staged in recurring environments and strongly written dialogue scenes.
- Uses established show language instead of introducing many new production variables.

#### S01E06 `Let Him Cook()` - $370,000

- Finale should feel sharper, not necessarily bigger.
- Funds editorial, sound, graphics finishing, and delivery polish to make the consequences legible and satisfying.
- Assumes limited but meaningful scale-up in pressurized public-facing or exposure-driven material.

The final row includes season-level costs allocated across episodes for management reporting, including insurance, payroll burden, accounting, delivery prep, and the held reserve. It is not a literal per-episode invoice bucket, but it keeps episode reporting tied to the real control budget.

### Scheduling Implications

- Plan `5` shoot days for S01E01 and S01E04.
- Plan `4` shoot days for S01E02, S01E03, S01E05, and S01E06, with one floating recovery/buffer day in the overall block.
- Separate low-headcount desktop-capture and insert pickups from full-company live-action days wherever possible.
- Finish UI asset lock before Episode 1 principal photography to avoid expensive post redesign.

### Management Use

This file should be used to challenge scope drift at the script and schedule stage. If an episode starts demanding more practical locations, more cast days, or more VFX than its lane supports, something else must contract. The point is not evenness. The point is control.
