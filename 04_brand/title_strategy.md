## Canonical Title System

### Locked Series Title
- Approved title: `WE OUT HERE`
- Always render in all caps.
- Never add punctuation.
- Never add a subtitle.
- Never attach a tagline directly to the title as if it is part of the official name.

Correct:
- `WE OUT HERE`

Incorrect:
- `We Out Here`
- `WE OUT HERE:`
- `WE OUT HERE - A Dark Sitcom`
- `WE OUT HERE: Cataclysm Consulting`

### Title Positioning Statement
`WE OUT HERE` should read as an indie prestige title with internet-native fluency, not as a spoof title, meme title, or startup product name. The title has bluntness and group-chat immediacy; the surrounding design system must supply the premium finish.

### Working Interpretation For Creative Teams
- `WE` signals a damaged collective.
- `OUT HERE` signals exposure, improvisation, and social overvisibility.
- The title should feel both declarative and faintly defensive, like something posted before the consequences arrive.

## Canonical Logline
Three burned-out technologists launch a chaotic AI consultancy from `Backchannel` and try to bootstrap legitimacy through manic hustle, irony, pseudo-legal improvisation, and memes. Their friendship becomes the failed holding environment that keeps the company alive while making everyone less stable.

Use this exact wording in canon-facing materials, partner one-sheets, press kits, and any high-trust metadata source.

## Title Hierarchy

### Primary Mark
- Use the plain wordmark `WE OUT HERE` as the default mark.
- Favor typographic confidence over illustrative logos.
- The mark should feel authored and controlled, not overdesigned.

### Secondary Lockup
- Secondary lockup may pair the wordmark with one campaign line, release line, or format descriptor.
- The secondary line must be visually subordinate.
- The secondary line is removable in all contexts.

Approved secondary examples:
- `A dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy`
- `From the world of Cataclysm Consulting`
- `Six episodes of startup rot and failed containment`

Not approved as permanent lockups:
- `WE OUT HERE: startup rot edition`
- `WE OUT HERE by Cataclysm Consulting`
- any construction that makes a tagline look canonical

## Wordmark Direction

### Formal Brief
The wordmark should feel like premium desktop culture under pressure: editorial, exact, slightly overcompressed, psychologically loaded. It should not feel like:
- a venture-backed SaaS logo
- a hacker parody logo
- an ironic Y2K revival for its own sake
- a synthetic AI gradient wordmark

### Recommended Formal Traits
- Tight or slightly tense tracking
- Clean uppercase geometry with one humanizing irregularity
- Strong silhouette at thumbnail scale
- Comfortable living inside dark UI frames, desktop captures, and poster compositions
- Able to hold on black, off-black, pale gray, and muted system-white backgrounds

### Recommended Variants
- Primary horizontal wordmark
- Compact stacked wordmark for posters and social crops
- Micro wordmark for desktop UI insert use
- Reversed version for dark backgrounds

## Typography Relationship

### Title Typeface Criteria
Choose a display face that can carry:
- seriousness
- compression
- institutional confidence under strain
- legibility in stills, trailers, posters, and website headers

Best-fit categories:
- neo-grotesk with tension
- editorial grotesk
- rigid sans with subtle ugliness

Avoid:
- overly friendly geometric sans
- luxury serif systems that make the show look period or literary
- glitch fonts
- faux-terminal display fonts
- generic cinematic condensed all-caps faces unless meaningfully customized

### Supporting Type Relationship
The title should sit against supporting typography that feels like:
- internal decks
- legal footers
- issue trackers
- annotated screenshots
- editorial dossier captions

The contrast between premium title typography and utilitarian support typography is core to the brand.

## Usage Rules By Context

### Posters And Key Art
- Treat `WE OUT HERE` as the main visual anchor.
- Keep it large enough to read from distance.
- Do not bury it in collage noise or interface clutter.
- Let campaign lines live elsewhere in the composition.

### Trailer Main Title Card
- Main title card should arrive late, after behavior and tone are established.
- Prefer stark presentation over flashy reveal animation.
- A subtle system flicker, cursor behavior, or UI snap is acceptable; a full glitch blowout is not.

### Website
- Homepage hero should use the wordmark cleanly, with supporting metadata nearby.
- Avoid putting the mark inside a generic rounded card or startup hero shell.
- The mark can coexist with desktop artifacts, but should not look trapped inside them.

### Social
- Maintain all caps in all official account graphics.
- When typed in caption copy, `WE OUT HERE` remains preferred.
- Avoid shortening to `WOH` in audience-facing creative.

### Press And Metadata
- Platform titles, festival listings, and press materials should always list `WE OUT HERE` exactly.
- If a CMS or distributor tool defaults to title case, override it manually where possible.

## Title + Company Relationship
`Cataclysm Consulting` is a major in-world brand, but it is not the series title. Marketing should use the company name as a dramatic lure, proof artifact, or sub-brand layer.

Recommended uses:
- fake client deck excerpts
- consultancy artifacts inside teaser materials
- poster microcopy
- website worldbuilding surfaces

Avoid:
- presenting `Cataclysm Consulting` as a subtitle
- letting consultancy branding overpower the series title

## File Naming And Delivery Conventions
- Final deliverables should preserve `WE OUT HERE` visually, but filenames may use `we-out-here` for technical consistency.
- Export folders should separate `title_wordmark`, `title_lockups`, and `title_safe_area_guides`.
- Provide SVG, PDF, transparent PNG, and outlined print package versions.

## Approval Checklist
- Is the title rendered exactly as `WE OUT HERE`?
- Is the title more prominent than any tagline or in-world company mark?
- Does the typography feel premium, tense, and internet-literate without reading as generic AI branding?
- Can the wordmark survive poster scale, phone scale, and trailer card scale?
- Is the surrounding composition helping the title feel authored rather than decorative?
