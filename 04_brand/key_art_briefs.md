## Purpose
Provide practical key art routes for `WE OUT HERE` so designers, photographers, retouchers, and campaign leads can develop poster systems, social crops, and web hero imagery from one coherent creative brief.

## Campaign Goal
Key art must sell the show as premium, specific, and behaviorally dangerous. The audience should understand immediately that this is not generic startup satire and not generic prestige gloom. The visual target is sharp social comedy with real consequence.

## Canon Anchors To Preserve
- Title is always `WE OUT HERE`.
- There are exactly three leads: `twinkle.puss`, `bl0ng`, `ariane.emory`.
- The working company name is `Cataclysm Consulting`.
- The show combines desktop realism, live action, and surreal inserts.
- The emotional engine is friendship as failed containment.

## Primary Campaign Recommendation
Lead with Route 1 for flagship key art. Support with Route 2 for digital campaign rollouts and Route 3 for character expansion. Route 4 is optional for festival and critic-facing collateral.

## Route 1: Audit Trail

### Core Idea
The trio appear in one sharply composed live-action frame while evidence of their working life accumulates around them: comments, timestamps, deck fragments, doc marks, status labels, and notification residue.

### Why It Works
- Balances people and desktop culture immediately.
- Sells tone and form in one image.
- Feels premium and specific.
- Works well across one-sheet, website hero, and streaming key art.

### Visual Build
- One hero still with the trio in a room that can plausibly function as office, bunker, and social crash site.
- Keep posture and eye-lines slightly disunified so the group reads as a broken system, not a united team.
- Layer in restrained interface artifacts around edges or across negative space.
- Use one danger accent only, ideally red or amber.

### Character Direction
- `twinkle.puss`: slightly forward, activated, trying to force momentum
- `bl0ng`: composed but withholding, as if already drafting the explanation
- `ariane.emory`: holding the room together and resenting it in real time

### Recommended Taglines
- `The joke became a company before anyone could stop it.`
- `Every shortcut leaves a paper trail.`
- `They can help. That is the problem.`

### Best Uses
- primary poster
- homepage hero
- streaming tile master art
- sales deck cover

## Route 2: Desktop Evidence

### Core Idea
The campaign behaves like recovered business evidence: layered windows, comment threads, faux deck pages, cropped faces, and archived language that slowly reveals the trio and the company's instability.

### Why It Works
- Leans hardest into the show's formal identity.
- Distinguishes the project from standard cast portrait campaigns.
- Strong for digital-first marketing, motion posters, and social rollouts.

### Visual Build
- Use a dark field with stacked windows and controlled overlap.
- Title remains clean and dominant over the window stack.
- Treat text fragments as readable enough to feel real, but not so dense that the design becomes homework.
- Show traces of collaboration and conflict: comments, edits, approvals, warnings, draft labels.

### Recommended Taglines
- `Built in chat. Discoverable in court.`
- `All communication may be used against them.`
- `Every message is a decision now.`

### Best Uses
- digital OOH
- animated social assets
- teaser poster series
- website section art

## Route 3: Failed Containment

### Core Idea
Three character portraits built around the function each person serves in the broken regulatory system. The portraits feel premium but destabilized by small system intrusions or surreal pressure.

### Why It Works
- Gives marketing a character-led expansion path.
- Supports cast-focused rollout.
- Makes the psychology legible without flattening the show into archetype posters.

### Visual Build
- Separate portrait sessions with coherent framing and palette.
- Each character gets one dominant mode of disruption.
- Keep wardrobe precise and lived-in.
- Use minimal but revealing copy.

### Character Copy Pairings
- `twinkle.puss` - `He turns shame into momentum.`
- `bl0ng` - `He can explain anything except why he helped.`
- `ariane.emory` - `She can contain everything except the cost.`

### Best Uses
- character poster triptych
- cast reveals
- social avatars and vertical video backgrounds
- press gallery stills

## Route 4: Cataclysm Consulting Sell Sheet

### Core Idea
An in-world consultancy poster or deck cover that almost sells `Cataclysm Consulting` as real, until the details expose fraudulence, exhaustion, and escalating discoverability.

### Why It Works
- Excellent for critics, festivals, web easter eggs, and social engagement.
- Deepens the world without replacing the main campaign.
- Lets the series market the company while also satirizing it.

### Visual Build
- Clean consultancy brand shell
- suspiciously overconfident service language
- premium formatting with ominous microcopy
- subtle evidence of collapse in the margins

### Recommended Taglines
- `AI adoption, brand voice, and premium unmanaged risk.`
- `Now onboarding panic at enterprise scale.`

### Best Uses
- alt poster
- press-kit appendix art
- website worldbuilding asset
- social stunt creative

## Shoot Guidance

### Environments
- apartment-office hybrids
- conference room with too much glass
- coworking corner that feels overlit and impersonal
- workspace with real fatigue residue, not set-dressed clutter for its own sake

### Props
- laptop stacks
- chargers and adapters
- legal pads and marked-up printouts
- takeaway containers and neglected water glasses
- decks, intake forms, post-its, policy printouts

### Performance Notes
- no broad smiling ensemble energy
- tension between collaboration and blame
- each person should appear mid-process, not posed as a celebrity portrait only
- humor should come from relational geometry and composure failure

## Crop And Deliverable Requirements
- 27x40 theatrical one-sheet
- 24x36 festival poster
- 16:9 hero still
- 9:16 vertical teaser crop
- 4:5 social poster crop
- 1:1 platform tile crop

Each route should be tested at thumbnail scale before approval.

## Retouching Guidance
- preserve skin texture and fatigue
- avoid over-polished luxury grading
- keep screens believable
- use overlays and interface marks with restraint
- let the image feel expensive because it is precise, not because it is glossy

## Approval Checklist
- Can a viewer tell this is about three people, not one founder-genius?
- Does the image communicate desktop-native culture without generic tech cliches?
- Is the title still clean and dominant?
- Does the art suggest danger, evidence, and failed containment?
- Would this still feel specific with all copy removed?
