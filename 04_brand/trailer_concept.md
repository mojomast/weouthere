## Trailer Mission
Sell the show as a prestige indie dark sitcom with premium desktop culture, startup rot, and psychologically precise ensemble comedy. The trailer should make viewers feel they are watching a company get willed into existence by the wrong people.

## Format Recommendation

### Primary Launch Trailer
- Length: 75 to 95 seconds
- Use: website hero, social pin, partner deck, festival submissions, outreach

### Teaser Cutdown
- Length: 30 to 45 seconds
- Use: announcement, social rollout, vertical adaptations

## Core Promise To Communicate
- three specific leads
- a chaotic AI consultancy called `Cataclysm Consulting`
- desktop realism as real storytelling grammar
- humor from defense mechanisms, not broad jokes
- escalation from manic possibility to visible consequence

## Editorial Spine
The trailer should move through the season's logic in miniature:

`mania -> legitimacy theater -> ethical drift -> containment failure -> exposure`

Do not try to summarize plot episode by episode. Sell the ratchet.

## Recommended Structure

### Beat 1: Ignition
Open on a desktop action with consequence energy: typing, unsending, hovering over send, or a message appearing at exactly the wrong time. Start small and specific.

Goal:
- establish desktop realism immediately
- tell the audience this show understands online work behavior from the inside

Possible opening card:
- `A joke company.`

### Beat 2: The Trio Snap Into Focus
Cut between the three leads in their distinct modes:
- `twinkle.puss` generating momentum
- `bl0ng` explaining the bad idea elegantly
- `ariane.emory` naming risk while still helping

Goal:
- make the trio legible as a broken system
- keep comedy character-driven

### Beat 3: Cataclysm Becomes Real
Introduce client-facing language, faux authority, decks, calls, or doc approvals. The joke hardens into workflow.

Possible card:
- `A real client.`

Goal:
- show why the premise is current and dangerous
- let the audience feel the thrill before the damage

### Beat 4: Velocity And Delusion
Increase density. Layer overlapping calls, cutaways, desktop evidence, and short surreal flashes. Let the consultancy appear briefly competent.

Possible card:
- `A little success.`

Goal:
- make success feel intoxicating
- imply that legitimacy itself is the trap

### Beat 5: Containment Failure
Rhythm breaks. Silence or negative space enters. A warning, screenshot, contradiction, or interpersonal rupture lands.

Possible cards:
- `No usable adults.`
- `Everything is discoverable.`

Goal:
- pivot from funny confidence to exposed instability
- show stakes without turning solemn

### Beat 6: Title And Final Sting
Land the main title late. Follow with one sharp button that is funny and damning rather than explosive.

Suggested sequence:
1. `WE OUT HERE`
2. short format descriptor or release line
3. final sting of someone trying to keep the machine alive one beat too long

## Sound Design And Music

### Music Direction
- start controlled, minimal, and tense
- build through percussion, pulse, or clipped synth structure
- avoid trailer-braam prestige sludge
- avoid quirky comedy scoring that undermines danger

### Sound Motifs
- keyboard taps
- notification hits
- message send whoosh or error state
- room tone cutting out before a bad decision
- subtle UI sounds used rhythmically, not cartoonishly

### Dialogue Mix Rule
Dialogue should feel fast and intelligent, but not buried under music. Prioritize lines that reveal psychodynamics rather than premise exposition.

## Visual Grammar

### Must Include
- live-action performance beats
- readable desktop moments
- evidence of `Cataclysm Consulting`
- at least one glimpse of each lead's comic engine
- one tonal break where consequence becomes visible

### Use Sparingly
- surreal inserts
- rapid-fire montage density
- title cards

### Avoid
- generic app montage
- empty city shots
- drone mood footage
- overuse of glitch transitions
- dialogue that only explains what the show is

## Sample Card Set
Use only a few of these in one cut.

- `A joke company.`
- `A real client.`
- `A terrible idea to keep going.`
- `Still technically operational.`
- `Everything is discoverable.`

## Line Selection Guidance
Pick lines that do one of three jobs:
- convert psychology into comedy
- convert workflow into danger
- expose the friendship as infrastructure

Best line types:
- tactical self-justification
- pseudo-legal reframing
- exhausted containment
- accidental confession inside a joke

Avoid line types:
- generic AI exposition
- lines that sound like a thesis statement
- lines that make the characters seem merely silly

## Trailer End-Copy Options
- `The joke became a company before anyone could stop it.`
- `They can help. That is the problem.`
- `Every shortcut leaves a paper trail.`

## Deliverables
- 90 sec landscape master
- 45 sec cutdown
- 30 sec cutdown
- 15 sec title sting
- 9:16 vertical adaptation
- caption-safe social versions

## Editorial Approval Checklist
- Does the trailer feel like premium desktop culture rather than generic screenlife?
- Are the three leads clearly distinct?
- Is the comedy rooted in pressure and defense mechanisms?
- Does the cut escalate rather than just pile up moments?
- Does the title land with authority?
- Would a viewer mistake this for broad startup satire or AI gimmick comedy? If yes, recut.
