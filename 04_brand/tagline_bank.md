## Usage Rules
- Taglines are campaign language, not canon.
- Do not attach any tagline permanently to `WE OUT HERE` as though it is part of the official title.
- Favor lines that imply behavior, pressure, and consequence.
- Avoid generic prestige-TV vagueness, generic AI satire copy, and empty disruption language.
- Keep the show's intelligence intact; the copy should sound like the world understands decks, terms, screenshots, and collapse from the inside.

## Core Positioning Line
Use when one line must do the most work:

`Three burned-out technologists turn shared burnout into a business model.`

## Hero Taglines
Best for one-sheets, website hero areas, teaser cards, and festival-facing campaign art.

- `The future is being handled by the least contained people alive.`
- `They built a company out of panic, language, and vibes.`
- `Three founders. One consultancy. Zero internal governance.`
- `Professional legitimacy was always a little theatrical.`
- `The joke became a company before anyone could stop it.`
- `They can help. That is the problem.`

## Prestige-Dark Taglines
Best for premium key art and slower campaign materials.

- `Friendship was never meant to function as governance.`
- `Every shortcut leaves a paper trail.`
- `They mistake momentum for truth.`
- `The company survives. The people don't, exactly.`
- `A dark sitcom about work, panic, and the market value of sounding certain.`
- `Success arrived before accountability.`

## Desktop-Culture Taglines
Best for digital ads, motion cards, social promos, and interface-led art.

- `All communication may be used against them.`
- `Sent from Backchannel with catastrophic confidence.`
- `The deck is clean. The people are not.`
- `Every message is a decision now.`
- `Bad boundaries. Good typography.`
- `Built in chat. Discoverable in court.`

## Startup-Rot Taglines
Best for campaign moments leaning into business comedy and consultancy absurdity.

- `Cataclysm Consulting is now accepting clients and generating liability.`
- `AI adoption, brand voice, and premium unmanaged risk.`
- `The consultancy for leaders who need answers before they have questions.`
- `Strategy. Workflow. Disclaimers. Collapse.`
- `A premium service business held together by shared delusion.`
- `Now onboarding panic at enterprise scale.`

## Character-Pressure Taglines
Best for character posters, cast reveals, and social assets.

- `He turns shame into momentum.`
- `He can explain anything except why he helped.`
- `She can contain everything except the cost.`
- `Together, they form a broken regulatory system.`
- `Nobody here can stop the machine they built.`

## Trailer Card Lines
Short enough for title cards and beat pivots.

- `A joke company.`
- `A real client.`
- `A terrible idea to keep going.`
- `A little success.`
- `A lot of documentation.`
- `No usable adults.`
- `Still technically operational.`
- `Everything is discoverable.`

## Poster Topline Options
Best for main art above or below title.

- `A dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy`
- `An indie prestige comedy about startup rot and failed containment`
- `Three burned-out technologists launch an AI consultancy and become just successful enough to destroy themselves`

## Social And Announcement Copy Hooks
Short lines for launch posts, teaser posts, and quote cards.

- `A consultancy built on speed, decks, disclaimers, and dread.`
- `This is what happens when the group chat gets incorporated.`
- `They were never supposed to become a company.`
- `Prestige desktop culture meets psychic mismanagement.`
- `The archive remembers everything.`
- `Nobody wants to admit they don't know what they're buying.`

## Lines To Retire Unless Needed For A Specific Joke
These are usable only in highly contextual social spots, not as core campaign language.

- `Let him cook()`
- `Thought leadership but evil`
- `We're so back office`
- `Synergy for the clinically overextended`

Reason: these tilt too meme-forward if overused and can flatten the prestige positioning.

## Copy Angles By Audience

### Press And Festivals
- `A dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy about three burned-out technologists who launch a chaotic AI consultancy from Backchannel.`
- `A psychologically sharp workplace comedy about startup rot, friendship as governance, and the market value of sounding certain.`

### Talent And Creative Partners
- `A premium ensemble comedy where every joke is carrying labor anxiety, failed intimacy, and consequence.`
- `A formally distinctive live-action series combining desktop realism, performance comedy, and surreal inserts.`

### General Audience
- `They started an AI consultancy as a joke. Then clients showed up.`
- `Three internet-native founders turn burnout into a business model.`

## Selection Guidance
- If the art is visually dense, use a simple tagline.
- If the art is stark, use a more behaviorally specific line.
- If `Cataclysm Consulting` appears prominently, avoid taglines that repeat `consultancy`, `company`, or `startup` too heavily.
- For premium launch moments, favor dread, consequence, and language-as-infrastructure over overt meme signaling.
