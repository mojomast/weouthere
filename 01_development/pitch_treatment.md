## Series Definition
- Title: `WE OUT HERE`
- Format: `6 x 30 min single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy`
- Style: `desktop realism + live action + surreal inserts`
- Leads: `twinkle.puss`, `bl0ng`, `ariane.emory`
- Company: `Cataclysm Consulting`

## Logline
Three burned-out technologists launch a chaotic AI consultancy from Backchannel and ride a manic wave of sudden success into ethical drift, containment failure, and public collapse.

## Pitch Statement
`WE OUT HERE` is a comedy about contemporary intelligence under contemporary pressure. It lives in the gap between what people know, what they say they know, and what the market rewards them for pretending to know. The series takes the current AI boom seriously enough to be specific and lightly enough to be funny. It understands that the funniest thing in the world right now may be a person using therapeutic language, legal disclaimers, and product vocabulary to avoid admitting they are spiraling.

At its core, this is a show about three friends whose shared fluency becomes a liability. They are talented enough to sound credible, damaged enough to overidentify with every opportunity, and bonded enough to confuse support with permission. When they start `Cataclysm Consulting`, they do not merely build a company. They build an apparatus that lets each of them remain uncontained a little longer.

## Why This Show
The AI economy has created a specific comic condition: leaders feel pressure to act, workers feel pressure to adapt, and almost nobody wants to confess uncertainty. That makes room for consultants, deck-makers, prompt whisperers, and workflow shamans who can translate panic into language. `WE OUT HERE` is interested in the people on both sides of that transaction.

What makes the series feel current is not jargon density but behavioral truth. These characters do not act absurdly because they are caricatures; they act absurdly because the environment already rewards dissociation, speed, pseudo-expertise, and permanent responsiveness. The show is about a culture where evidence exists in chat logs, where work and identity fuse, and where every private joke can become a line item on an invoice.

## The World
The setting is a near-present tech and media ecosystem with no speculative leap required. AI tools are normal enough to be integrated into routine work and unstable enough to generate panic, overpromising, and institutional confusion. The founders organize from Backchannel, whose channels, DMs, links, screenshots, and pinned messages become both plot engine and evidence archive.

`Cataclysm Consulting` thrives because it offers exactly what anxious institutions want: the feeling that someone competent is already handling the future. The founders sell strategy, prompt systems, synthetic brand voice, process design, and crisis messaging under an innovation banner. In practice, they are often selling framing, acceleration, and permission.

## The Characters

### twinkle.puss
twinkle.puss is the company's spark plug and greatest threat. He is charismatic, hilarious, improvisational, and capable of turning a passing idea into a room-changing event. He experiences activation as truth. His gift is momentum. His danger is that momentum can feel morally self-justifying once other people applaud.

### bl0ng
bl0ng gives Cataclysm its architecture, or at least the appearance of architecture. He is good with systems, documents, workflows, and implementation language. He often spots problems first, then builds just enough process to help everyone keep going anyway. He is not a coward, exactly; he is a technician of avoidance.

### ariane.emory
ariane.emory is the emotional sharpest knife in the room. She can identify manipulation, drift, and interpersonal fracture long before the others will name it. Her tragedy is that understanding does not free her from attachment. She becomes the show's most credible witness and one of its deepest accomplices.

## The Engine
Every episode combines three elements:
- a concrete consulting problem with real external stakes
- an internal destabilization in one or more leads
- a digital escalation that transforms a bad idea into a decision before anyone can metabolize it

This structure lets the show move fluidly between workplace mechanics and psychological fallout. The company keeps surviving just long enough to validate the wrong lesson. That is the engine of both comedy and tragedy.

## The Form
The single-camera live-action scenes ground the series in bodies, rooms, and social performance. Desktop realism captures the actual velocity of modern work: channels, docs, DMs, decks, pop-ups, terms, calendar invites, voice notes, and the unbearable permanence of sending. Surreal inserts allow the audience to experience manic certainty, dissociation, fantasy authority, and moral self-exoneration from inside the characters' nervous systems.

The formal rule is clarity first. Screens are readable. Live action carries consequence. Surreal material never replaces plot; it sharpens subjectivity and comic pressure.

## Season 1 Trajectory
The season follows a locked progression:
- `Mania`
- `Fragile success`
- `Ethical drift`
- `Containment failure`
- `Ironic collapse`
- `Exposure to consequences`

Episode path:
1. `Don't Send That`: a joke consultancy becomes real when the wrong message gets the right response.
2. `Terms of Abuse`: documentation, contracts, and platform language become tools for making risk feel governable.
3. `The Manic Defense`: failure is reframed as disruptive genius and the group starts protecting the company from reality.
4. `Contain Me Daddy`: the team seeks outside containment but only if it will affirm their exceptionalism.
5. `The Superego Collapse`: the internal moral voice gives way under commercial pressure, resentment, and accumulated justification.
6. `Let Him Cook()`: archived behavior, unstable product logic, and real-world harm converge, forcing exposure to consequences no meme can metabolize.

## The Comedy
The humor is fast, exact, and rooted in recognizable contemporary behavior: overqualification, disclaimers, false clarity, deck-speak, accidental honesty, strategic softness, oversharing, and the panic response of making a framework. The show is dark because the consequences are real, but it remains a sitcom because people keep trying to recover social control in increasingly ridiculous ways.

## The Emotional Core
The audience should feel that these three people genuinely love one another, even when they are enabling each other's worst instincts. Their friendship is not secretly healthy beneath the damage. It is the source of warmth, momentum, witness, and catastrophe. The series works because the collapse is not just professional. It is relational.

## What Buyers and Partners Are Getting
- A sharply contemporary comedy with a clear point of view on work, language, and AI culture.
- A formally distinctive but production-manageable approach that integrates screen storytelling without becoming a gimmick piece.
- Three lead roles with precise comic and dramatic range.
- A six-episode season with a strong escalation spine and meaningful rewatch value.
