## FAQ

### What is `WE OUT HERE` in one sentence?
It is a 6 x 30 minute single-camera dark sitcom and tech psychocomedy about three burned-out technologists who start an AI consultancy from Backchannel and become just successful enough to destroy themselves.

### What makes the show different from generic startup satire?
The series is not interested in broad founder archetypes or easy anti-tech jokes. Its specificity comes from digital workflow realism, psychologically accurate self-justification, and a friendship engine that turns professional drift into emotional catastrophe.

### Is the show pro-AI or anti-AI?
Neither. The series treats AI as a real social and economic force that exposes existing human weaknesses: panic, opportunism, magical thinking, status anxiety, and the desire to automate uncertainty. The target is not the tool alone but the culture around it.

### Why now?
The market is saturated with AI messaging while public understanding remains uneven. That gap is comic, dramatic, and highly legible. The show captures a moment when expertise has become theatrical and language itself has market value.

### How does the format work on screen?
The series combines live action, readable desktop storytelling, and controlled surreal inserts. Desktop realism carries decisions and evidence. Live action carries bodies, performance, and fallout. Surreal inserts visualize manic certainty, dissociation, and fantasy authority without breaking the grounded world.

### Is the show expensive or formally difficult to produce?
It is formally distinctive but production-manageable. The screen language is intentional rather than effects-heavy, and the world is built from contemporary offices, homes, meetings, digital spaces, and a contained set of recurring environments.

### Who is the core audience?
Digitally fluent adults 25-44, especially viewers who recognize contemporary work culture, platform logic, burnout, and the gap between professional performance and actual stability.

### Does a non-tech audience need specialized knowledge?
No. Viewers do not need technical expertise to follow the series. The emotional and comic stakes are friendship, status, ambition, self-delusion, and consequence. Tech specificity adds texture and authority, not a barrier to entry.

### Who are the leads?
`twinkle.puss` is the catalytic force, `bl0ng` is the systems rationalizer, and `ariane.emory` is the conscience under attachment pressure. Their combined fluency is the company's asset and its fatal weakness.

### What is the season arc?
The season moves through `mania`, `fragile success`, `ethical drift`, `containment failure`, `ironic collapse`, and `exposure to consequences`, across six episodes: `Don't Send That`, `Terms of Abuse`, `The Manic Defense`, `Contain Me Daddy`, `The Superego Collapse`, and `Let Him Cook()`.

### Is the company name locked?
`Cataclysm Consulting` is currently a soft lock and should be treated as the working company name across development materials unless legal or branding review requires an update.

### How dark is the comedy?
The show is dark in consequence, not in tone management. It stays funny through specificity, speed, and behavioral accuracy even as the characters move toward moral and relational collapse.

### Is there franchise or future-season potential?
Yes. The consultancy structure creates a scalable episode engine, while the leads' psychology and evolving public exposure provide durable season-to-season escalation. Season 1, however, is designed to feel complete and sharply shaped on its own.

### What should partners understand immediately?
The series' biggest asset is point of view. It knows exactly how contemporary language, work, and instability interact, and it dramatizes that knowledge in a form that is accessible, funny, and commercially legible.

## Suggested Talking Points for Meetings
- The show understands AI culture without becoming an "issue" series.
- The friendship dynamic is the emotional engine, not a side element.
- Desktop realism is part of the storytelling grammar, not a gimmick.
- The season has a strong rise-and-collapse shape across six episodes.
