## Tonal Thesis
`WE OUT HERE` should feel intelligent, unstable, very funny, and quietly dangerous. The audience should recognize the language, interfaces, self-mythologizing, and panic-management behaviors from their own lives even when the characters push them to absurd extremes.

## Primary Comp Mix

### `Industry`
Use for: workplace specificity, status games, strategic language, and the comedy of people making meaning under institutional pressure.

Carry forward:
- precision about professional behavior
- comedy rooted in power and rhetoric
- characters trying to maintain selfhood inside a system

Do not borrow:
- detached coolness as the dominant tone

### `Search Party`
Use for: millennial self-delusion, escalating bad decisions, and the way irony curdles into danger.

Carry forward:
- spiral logic that still tracks character truth
- comedy from self-protective language
- ensemble complicity

Do not borrow:
- mystery-thriller architecture as the primary engine

### `Severance`
Use for: visual discipline around systems, interfaces, and institutional dread.

Carry forward:
- rigorous formal control
- comedy generated by procedural structures
- unease created through ordinary workflow

Do not borrow:
- high-concept speculative mythology

### `Succession`
Use for: verbal aggression, status volatility, and how people weaponize fluency to avoid sincerity.

Carry forward:
- language as combat
- humiliation as a scene engine
- comedy inside cruelty and self-protection

Do not borrow:
- dynastic scale or elite-family framing

### `Atlanta`
Use for: permission to move between realism and surreal inserts without announcing a genre change.

Carry forward:
- tonal elasticity
- subjective surrealism with clear authorial confidence
- precision about subculture and world texture

Do not borrow:
- anthology looseness or dream logic that overtakes plot

## Secondary Comp Phrases
- `The Office` for the document trail, but with contemporary platform anxiety and no mockumentary grammar.
- `Black Mirror` energy at the level of social diagnosis, but comedic and near-present rather than cautionary sci-fi.
- `Girls` or `Fleabag` in the sense of self-awareness failing to produce better behavior.

## Tonal Guardrails
- The show must never become a broad tech parody.
- The characters should feel observed from within, not mocked from above.
- AI language should be specific enough to feel lived-in and sparse enough to stay legible.
- Surreal beats must intensify character subjectivity, not decorate it.
- Emotional damage should land, even when scenes are joke-dense.

## Tonal Blend Statement
The target blend is:
- 40% workplace and systems comedy
- 30% friendship damage and psychological instability
- 20% language-driven status play
- 10% surreal subjective rupture

## Buyer-Friendly One-Liners
- `Industry` meets `Search Party` in an AI consultancy born inside Backchannel.
- A dark single-camera tech psychocomedy with the workplace precision of `Industry`, the spiral energy of `Search Party`, and flashes of surreal subjectivity.
- A contemporary ensemble comedy about AI panic, platform-shaped work, and the friendship system that makes collapse possible.

## Red Flags in Development
If any draft starts feeling like the items below, the tone has drifted:
- generic startup satire
- anti-tech sermonizing
- random meme sketching
- sleek prestige coldness with no comic mess
- broad "crazy friend" behavior without psychological logic
