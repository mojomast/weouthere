## Series Snapshot
- Title: `WE OUT HERE`
- Format: `6 x 30 min single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy`
- Style: `desktop realism + live action + surreal inserts`
- Company: `Cataclysm Consulting`

## Logline
Three burned-out technologists launch a chaotic AI consultancy from Backchannel and ride a manic wave of sudden success into ethical drift, containment failure, and public collapse.

## The Show
`WE OUT HERE` is a dark workplace comedy about what happens when smart, unstable, internet-native adults turn shared burnout into a business model. The series follows twinkle.puss, bl0ng, and ariane.emory as they transform a semi-ironic Backchannel idea into `Cataclysm Consulting`, an AI consultancy built on speed, vibes, decks, disclaimers, and the market value of saying "we can help" before anyone asks for proof.

The world is near-present, not sci-fi. AI tools are accessible, overvalued, poorly understood, and socially compulsory. Executives want to look current. Nobody wants to admit confusion. Cataclysm steps into that gap and monetizes it. What begins as a joke becomes a real company because the culture is already irrational enough to support it.

At the center of the show is a friendship system that functions as management, therapy, sales, and mutual destruction. The trio are brilliant at reading language, perception, and panic. They are much worse at boundaries, accountability, and basic reality testing. Every win makes them feel chosen. Every success increases the cost of stopping.

## The Leads

### twinkle.puss
The catalyst. Magnetic, fast, funny, and frighteningly persuasive. He turns humiliation into momentum and treats activation like revelation.

### bl0ng
The systems brain. He builds frameworks, documents, and workflows that make chaos look operational, even when they mostly function as decorative legitimacy.

### ariane.emory
The conscience under pressure. She sees the ethical and emotional truth fastest, but insight does not spare her from complicity.

## Tonal Identity
The comedy is precise, psychologically literate, and behaviorally specific. The show understands online language, tech culture, and therapeutic self-narration from the inside. It is not "about weird internet people." It is about contemporary professional life mutating under platform logic, hype cycles, and unprocessed need.

The form combines:
- desktop realism for decision-making, miscommunication, and evidence trails
- live action for embodied fallout, meetings, performances, and interpersonal damage
- surreal inserts for manic logic, dissociation, fantasy authority, and private psychic distortion

## Season 1 Arc
The season moves through a clear escalation:
- `Mania`: the company is born in a burst of shame-flipping grandiosity
- `Fragile success`: early wins make the bit feel ordained
- `Ethical drift`: language and process become tools for manufacturing permission
- `Containment failure`: attempts to import structure collapse under projection and sabotage
- `Ironic collapse`: the company keeps surviving past the point where it should stop
- `Exposure to consequences`: public visibility turns archived behavior into real stakes

Episodes:
- `Don't Send That`
- `Terms of Abuse`
- `The Manic Defense`
- `Contain Me Daddy`
- `The Superego Collapse`
- `Let Him Cook()`

## Why It Works Now
- AI has made expertise newly theatrical.
- Professional legitimacy increasingly depends on language, interfaces, and confidence theater.
- Burnout, self-diagnosis, and performative fluency are mainstream conditions, not niche subculture traits.
- Audiences are ready for a comedy that understands the psychic cost of living through platform-shaped work life.
