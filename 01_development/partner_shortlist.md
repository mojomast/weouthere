## Shortlist Summary
`WE OUT HERE` is a `6 x 30 min` single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy built around a sharply current premise and a production-manageable formal approach.

Three burned-out technologists launch a chaotic AI consultancy from a Discord-like group chat and try to bootstrap legitimacy through manic hustle, irony, pseudo-legal improvisation, and memes. Their friendship becomes the failed holding environment that keeps the company alive while making everyone less stable.

The series combines desktop realism, live action, and surreal inserts to tell a story about AI panic, contemporary work performance, burnout, and the social systems people build to avoid admitting they are spiraling. It is specific about internet language and professional behavior without becoming niche, didactic, or generic startup satire.

At the center are `twinkle.puss`, `bl0ng`, and `ariane.emory`: three leads with distinct comic and psychological functions whose shared fluency makes the company possible and the collapse inevitable. Season 1 escalates across six episodes from mania to fragile success, ethical drift, containment failure, ironic collapse, and exposure to consequences.

The project is designed to be formally distinctive but practical to produce: contained settings, a tight ensemble, readable screen storytelling, and a controlled surreal grammar that sharpens subjectivity rather than inflating scope. That makes `WE OUT HERE` legible as both a creative calling card and a disciplined indie series opportunity.

For external readers, the value proposition is clear:

- culturally current subject matter with durable relevance beyond trend-chasing
- a strong tonal identity rooted in psychologically precise dark comedy
- a compact six-episode shape with escalation, consequence, and rewatch value
- clear appeal to digitally fluent adults, prestige-comedy viewers, tastemakers, and industry-adjacent audiences
