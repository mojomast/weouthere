# WE OUT HERE - Multi-Season Arc Document
**Seasons 2, 3, and 4 Bible**

## Context & Baseline
Cataclysm Consulting is a shell game. twinkle.puss (the manic, terminally-online face), bl0ng (the detached, sociopathic tech architecture), and ariane.emory (the desperate, caffeine-addicted ops/superego) barely survived their disastrous Season 1 AI demo. Now, with no product, no morals, and the threat of litigation breathing down their necks, they do the only logical thing: pivot to web3. 

---

## Season 2: The Web3 Grift (The Pivot)

**Core Theme / Containment Failure:** Decentralized Delusion / Ponzi Economics. The lie moves from a closed corporate loop into the wild, decentralized internet where the bagholders are entirely unhinged. 

**Season Summary:**
Fleeing the ashes of their failed AI demo, the trio rebrands Cataclysm as a Web3/Crypto powerhouse. They launch a fundamentally useless utility token and accidentally cultivate a rabid cult of bagholders on Discord. As the money actually starts rolling in (in the form of highly volatile shitcoins), the internal pressure mounts to orchestrate the perfect "rug pull" before the SEC, their own community mods, or a 14-year-old hacker from Estonia dox them.

**Episodes:**
1. **"Minting Catastrophe"** - The crew scrambles to draft a whitepaper composed entirely of scraped Reddit comments to distract investors from their AI failure.
2. **"Apeing In"** - twinkle.puss accidentally promises a highly-anticipated metaverse integration during a Twitter Space, forcing bl0ng to host it on a rusty Raspberry Pi.
3. **"Discord Moderation"** - ariane.emory spends 48 sleepless hours trying to quell a mutiny in their token's Discord by pretending to be six different community managers.
4. **"Rug Pull Dynamics"** - When their token unexpectedly pumps to a $50M market cap, the team viciously argues over whether to crash the project intentionally or ride it to a bigger fraud.
5. **"Smart Contract, Dumb People"** - bl0ng's hastily copy-pasted smart contract gets exploited, forcing the team to negotiate a ransom with a teenage hacker who only accepts payment in Robux.
6. **"To The Moon (Cratering)"** - With the FBI asking questions about unregistered securities, the team fakes a catastrophic "hack" to drain the liquidity pool and pivot to the Metaverse.

## Season 3: The Metaverse Wasteland (The Void)

**Core Theme / Containment Failure:** Virtual Solipsism / Digital Decay. The reality distortion field becomes literal. The characters are trapped in the consequences of their crypto collapse, retreating into digital spaces as their physical lives rot.

**Season Summary:**
Having narrowly escaped prison by claiming they were victims of a crypto hack, Cataclysm wins a bizarre government contract to build an enterprise Metaverse platform for bureaucratic onboarding. They end up creating a deeply depressing digital purgatory for middle managers. Losing their grip on reality, the trio spends 18 hours a day in VR headsets, avoiding their expiring office lease, unpaid utility bills, and the creeping realization that they are haunting their own fake company.

**Episodes:**
1. **"Low Poly Hell"** - Cataclysm secures their first enterprise client, but the Unity asset pack bl0ng stole makes all the virtual avatars look like PS1 sleep paralysis demons.
2. **"Haptic Feedback"** - twinkle.puss buys a full-body sensory suit on the dying company card and refuses to take it off during a critical VC pitch over Zoom.
3. **"Virtual Real Estate"** - ariane.emory attempts to artificially inflate the value of digital land next to a virtual Wendy's to pay off real-world loan sharks who found their coworking space.
4. **"Digital Twin"** - bl0ng creates an AI clone of himself to handle all client meetings, but the clone starts giving better technical advice and attempts a hostile takeover of the Slack workspace.
5. **"The Infinite Scroll"** - Trapped in the server closet because the electronic door lock failed, the trio must navigate their own buggy metaverse to manually trigger an office reboot from the inside.
6. **"Headset Removal"** - The physical office lease expires, forcing them to live entirely out of a rented, freezing server farm while claiming to Forbes that they are "fully remote visionaries."

## Season 4: The Hardware Pivot (Physical Consequences)

**Core Theme / Containment Failure:** Supply Chain Rot / Flesh Meets Silicon. Software scams are too easily traceable; hardware requires physical proof. The lie manifests in the real world, and it is heavy, expensive, and potentially explosive.

**Season Summary:**
Realizing that the Metaverse is dead and software scams leave too much of a digital footprint, Cataclysm pivots to "vaporware hardware." They promise a revolutionary biometric wearable device on Kickstarter. But instead of just faking code, they are suddenly drowning in shipping manifests, Chinese manufacturing disputes, and the terrifying reality of having to produce a physical object that doesn't immediately catch fire on a user's wrist.

**Episodes:**
1. **"Kickstarter Physics"** - The team launches a wildly successful crowdfunding campaign for a "neural-sync" wearable that is actually just a cheap pedometer taped to a Bluetooth speaker.
2. **"Shenzhen Nightmares"** - ariane.emory has a complete mental breakdown while trying to negotiate tooling costs with a sketchy manufacturing plant via Google Translate at 4 AM.
3. **"Supply Chain Issues"** - A shipping container full of their fake wearables gets stuck in the Suez Canal, briefly making them heroes to their investors who love an "act of God" excuse for delays.
4. **"The Prototype"** - To everyone's horror, bl0ng accidentally builds a functional prototype, but it emits a high-frequency noise that causes aggressive, localized paranoia in the office.
5. **"Unboxing Video"** - twinkle.puss pays desperate tech influencers to fake excitement while unboxing entirely empty, weighted packages, leading to a massive Reddit sleuth investigation.
6. **"Planned Obsolescence"** - Cornered by furious backers, federal regulators, and a mountain of e-waste, the trio decides the only way out is to rebrand back to an AI consultancy, perfectly completing the grift cycle.
