## Content

### Season Overview
Season 1 of `WE OUT HERE` tracks the rise of `Cataclysm Consulting`, an improvised AI consultancy launched on `Backchannel` by `twinkle.puss`, `bl0ng`, and `ariane.emory`. Across six half-hour episodes, a joke company becomes a real business, and every step toward legitimacy deepens the trio's instability, intimacy, and exposure.

### Episode Guide

#### `S01E01` - `"Don't Send That"`
After a humiliating professional moment, the trio turn a group-chat bit into a reckless outreach move that accidentally gets traction. What should have remained private becomes the founding event of a company no one was actually ready to run.

#### `S01E02` - `"Terms of Abuse"`
With their first real opportunity on the line, the founders confront contracts, liability, and the difference between sounding legitimate and being protected. Documentation becomes both shield and threat.

#### `S01E03` - `"The Manic Defense"`
As delivery pressure mounts, Cataclysm starts reframing instability as innovation. The founders discover how easily confidence, jargon, and shared exhaustion can become a working moral system.

#### `S01E04` - `"Contain Me Daddy"`
Knowing they need help, the trio goes looking for structure, oversight, or adult supervision. Instead, they expose how badly they want containment on terms that do not actually limit them.

#### `S01E05` - `"The Superego Collapse"`
Internal jokes, external promises, and public-facing language stop lining up. As screenshots, contradictions, and resentment accumulate, the company's self-image begins to fracture in full view.

#### `S01E06` - `"Let Him Cook()"`
Facing reputational fallout, the founders attempt one final act of narrative control. What they call vision looks increasingly like consequence arriving on time.

### Season Promise
The series progresses through `mania`, `fragile success`, `ethical drift`, `containment failure`, `ironic collapse`, and `exposure to consequences`. Each episode compounds pressure rather than resetting it.
