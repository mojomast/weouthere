## Content

### What is `WE OUT HERE`?
`WE OUT HERE` is a `6 x 30 min single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy` about three burned-out technologists who launch the AI consultancy `Cataclysm Consulting` from the fictional platform `Backchannel` and become just successful enough to reveal how unstable the whole operation really is.

### What makes the show distinct?
The series combines psychological precision, workplace comedy, and digital realism in a way that feels contemporary without chasing trend language. It is less interested in broad startup parody than in the way smart, articulate adults talk themselves into risk, drift, and complicity.

### Is the show satirizing AI?
The show treats AI less as a target than as a pressure system. It is about the social world around AI: hype, labor anxiety, expertise theater, improvisational ethics, and the performance of certainty.

### Do viewers need technical knowledge to follow it?
No. The series is built for general audiences as well as industry-literate viewers. The core stakes are friendship, ambition, shame, self-delusion, and consequence.

### Why set the story on `Backchannel`?
`Backchannel` allows the series to dramatize how work, intimacy, and crisis now blur together in digital spaces. It is office, social life, archive, and evidence trail all at once.

### Why does the project use desktop realism?
Because that is where many contemporary decisions actually happen. Screen language in `WE OUT HERE` is not ornamental. It is the grammar of how these characters misread, escalate, document, and expose themselves.

### How dark is the comedy?
Dark in consequence, but still recognizably a sitcom in rhythm and pressure. The humor comes from specificity, speed, and self-protective behavior rather than from cruelty for its own sake.

### Who are the three leads?
`twinkle.puss` is the charismatic accelerant, `bl0ng` is the anxious systems rationalizer, and `ariane.emory` is the overfunctioning witness who keeps trying to contain what the others keep unleashing.

### What is the emotional core of the show?
The friendship. These three people genuinely matter to one another. Their care is real, but it is tangled up with dependency, collusion, and shared professional desperation.

### Is the show mocking online culture?
No. It is written from inside that fluency. The aim is not to condescend to online-native behavior, but to dramatize how internet-shaped speech and work patterns have become ordinary social reality.

### What audiences is the project aimed at?
Digitally fluent adults, prestige-comedy viewers, and anyone who recognizes contemporary work culture as a mix of performance, panic, and highly managed instability.

### What are the episode titles?
`"Don't Send That"`, `"Terms of Abuse"`, `"The Manic Defense"`, `"Contain Me Daddy"`, `"The Superego Collapse"`, and `"Let Him Cook()"`.

### How should the show be described in short form?
Use: `6 x 30 min single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy`.

### What should interviewers avoid mischaracterizing?
- Do not frame the show as speculative sci-fi.
- Do not flatten it into generic startup satire.
- Do not describe the platform as a real-world brand.
- Do not imply the series is anti-technology in a simplistic sense.

### Approved short pull quotes
- "A dark comedy about the AI era that understands work as performance and friendship as infrastructure."
- "An internet-literate half-hour where fluency becomes liability."
- "Three brilliant, burned-out operators build a company that should not exist and then have to live inside it."
