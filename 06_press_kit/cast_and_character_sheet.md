### Cast Status

### Principal Roles

#### `twinkle.puss`
- Role type: lead
- Age range: late 20s to mid 30s
- Function in series: catalyst, closer, myth-maker
- Public-facing description: Charismatic, fast, and frighteningly persuasive, `twinkle.puss` is the engine that turns a joke consultancy into a real company. He experiences activation as clarity and treats momentum like proof. His brilliance is real. So is his inability to tolerate limits.
- Performance notes: Requires an actor who can move between razor-sharp comic language, volatility, glamour, and exposed vulnerability without losing authority.

#### `bl0ng`
- Role type: lead
- Age range: late 20s to late 30s
- Function in series: architect, rationalizer, systems brain
- Public-facing description: `bl0ng` gives `Cataclysm Consulting` the appearance of structure. He turns anxiety into frameworks, ethics into argument, and bad ideas into workflows that almost function. He sees the danger clearly, but usually too elegantly to stop it.
- Performance notes: Requires dry comic precision, technical credibility, and the ability to make over-articulate avoidance feel both funny and painful.

#### `ariane.emory`
- Role type: lead
- Age range: late 20s to late 30s
- Function in series: operator, witness, unstable conscience
- Public-facing description: `ariane.emory` is the closest thing the trio has to a functioning adult. She can identify manipulation, drift, and fallout before anyone else, but her insight does not free her from complicity. She keeps the company legible long enough for it to become worse.
- Performance notes: Requires stillness, authority, dry wit, and the ability to show intense internal pressure beneath control.

### Ensemble Dynamic
The three leads form a broken regulatory system:
- `twinkle.puss` supplies acceleration
- `bl0ng` supplies justification
- `ariane.emory` supplies containment

The series works because no pair can replace the absent third. Their friendship is the show's warmth, comic rhythm, and central liability.

### Public Casting Positioning
For talent, `WE OUT HERE` offers:
- three lead roles with unusually specific contemporary language
- a strong balance of comedy and psychological realism
- an ensemble dynamic built on pressure, intelligence, and dependency
- a compact season structure with meaningful arc progression across all six episodes
