# 07_website/website_strategy.md

## Purpose

Define the website strategy for **WE OUT HERE**, an indie prestige TV project positioned as a 6 x 30 min dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy. This document sets the role of the site, the audience pathways it must support, the content and conversion priorities, and the aesthetic/technical principles needed to make the project feel credible to both culture-facing and industry-facing visitors.

## Owner/Subagent
web_agent

## Status
Draft v1 - strategy baseline for site planning and build

## Dependencies

- Final or near-final series logline, synopsis, tone statement, and creator bios
- Brand system: typography, color, key art direction, logo lockups
- Core stills, mood frames, teaser footage, and behind-the-scenes assets
- Press materials: project summary, credits, bios, contact details
- Legal review for public-facing claims, investor-facing language, and casting/job postings
- Analytics and email tooling decisions
- Domain, hosting, CMS/workflow, and contact inbox ownership

## Goals

The website should function as both a cultural object and a production interface.

- Establish WE OUT HERE as a serious premium indie TV project with distinct taste and a coherent world
- Convert first-time discovery into deeper engagement, credibility, and contact
- Give press, festivals, collaborators, and industry stakeholders a fast path to authoritative information
- Provide a polished home for updates, materials, and recruitment needs over time
- Support legitimacy signals for investors, partners, cast, crew, and community members
- Capture email interest without making the experience feel like a generic startup funnel

## Audiences

Primary audiences:

- Curious public visitors discovering the project through social, press, festivals, or word of mouth
- Press and cultural writers looking for a clear angle, assets, and factual project details
- Festival programmers, industry gatekeepers, producers, reps, and development executives assessing seriousness and fit
- Talent considering creative alignment, tone, and production quality
- Crew and collaborators evaluating whether the project is real, ambitious, and well-organized
- Investors and partners looking for competent packaging and a clear contact path

Secondary audiences:

- Fans and early community members who want to follow the project and feel “inside” the world
- Friends-of-project traffic who need an easy canonical link to share
- Future casting applicants and job candidates

## Positioning

WE OUT HERE should read as:

- A premium indie series with sharp creative authorship
- Internet-native but not disposable, ironic, or trend-chasing
- Fluent in startup, creative tech, and online social realism without feeling like brand satire for its own sake
- Built with enough seriousness to attract press, festivals, talent, and partners

The site should feel like the project belongs in a triangle between prestige TV microsite, art-forward production dossier, and founder-era product launch. The balance matters: too corporate makes it fake, too DIY makes it small, too “cool” makes it unserious.

## Site Architecture Principles

- Every page should answer two questions quickly: “What is this?” and “Why should I care?”
- The information hierarchy should separate public-facing cultural storytelling from practical industry utility, while keeping both in one coherent system
- The site should scale from teaser-stage project presence to active production communications hub
- Pages should feel editorial, not template-driven
- Navigation labels should be plain enough for press and industry, while page composition can still express the project’s voice
- The user should always have an obvious next move: read more, view assets, inquire, apply, join, or subscribe
- Important facts should be repeated strategically across pages so key visitors do not need to hunt

## Visual And Experience Logic

Preserve a **premium indie-TV meets startup-rot** visual logic.

- Use desktop realism, interface framing, live-action stills, faux system surfaces, and occasional surreal inserts as part of the storytelling language
- Favor modular editorial compositions, screen-like containers, annotations, logs, dossiers, and production artifacts over generic hero-banner-plus-three-cards layouts
- Let the site feel internet-literate and contemporary, but not like a SaaS landing page, design agency clone, or “coming soon” splash
- Motion, transitions, and interactive reveals should feel deliberate and slightly uncanny, not slick for its own sake
- Avoid stock motifs: oversized gradient blobs, empty manifesto copy, generic founder-style value props, and vague cinematic buzzwords

## Recommended Page Roles

### Home

- High-impact introduction to the series
- Establish tone, format, legitimacy, and current status quickly
- Route different visitor types to the right deeper page
- Primary CTAs: explore series, get updates, contact, press kit

### About the Series

- Canonical overview: premise, format, tone, world, themes, creative intent
- Suitable for public readers and industry skim

### Episodes

- Season overview and episode-level loglines or placeholders
- Can evolve from conceptual structure to full episode pages later

### Characters

- Introduce principal cast of the fictional world with sharp, useful summaries
- Support tone communication and audience attachment

### Press Kit

- One-stop page for logline, synopsis, credits, bios, stills, downloadable assets, contact
- Built for speed and trust

### News / Updates

- Living proof of project motion
- Production milestones, festival selections, casting announcements, release of teaser assets, partnerships, development updates

### Casting

- Public casting notices only when active
- Clear boundaries, process notes, and legitimacy signals

### Jobs / Join the Production

- Crew, collaborators, internships, or volunteer postings where appropriate
- Should communicate standards and project seriousness

### For Investors / Partners

- Private-feeling but publicly legible overview for aligned backers and partners
- Framed carefully to avoid over-disclosing while signaling readiness for conversation

### Contact

- Clear routing for general, press, partnership, casting, and production inquiries

### FAQ

- Reduces repetitive inbound questions
- Good place for status, submissions policy, casting boundaries, and community expectations

### Community / Discord

- Onboarding surface for fans and supporters
- Should feel selective and intentional, not like a growth hack

## CTA Strategy

The site needs multiple CTA tracks because the project has multiple real-world use cases.

Primary CTA families:

- Discover: read about the series, meet the characters, explore episodes
- Follow: join email list, join community, follow updates
- Evaluate: view press kit, review project facts, access materials
- Contact: press inquiry, talent inquiry, crew inquiry, partner/investor inquiry
- Apply: casting submission, job interest, collaboration interest

CTA principles:

- Match CTA intensity to visitor intent; not every page should push the same action
- Keep the most public CTA lightweight: email updates
- Keep high-friction CTAs targeted and context-specific: investor, casting, jobs
- Use credibility-supporting microcopy around forms and submissions so the project feels organized and safe
- Avoid cluttering every viewport with repeated buttons

## Conversion Goals

Core conversions:

- Email signups
- Contact form submissions by qualified press, talent, crew, and partners
- Press kit downloads or asset accesses
- Click-throughs to key credibility pages from Home
- Community joins when active

Secondary conversions:

- Time spent on About / Episodes / Characters pages
- Return visits to News / Updates
- Shares of canonical pages
- Replies to outreach after visitors review the site

## Content Governance

- Maintain one canonical version of key facts: logline, synopsis, format, creator credits, status, contact details
- Press Kit, About, and Home should share a common source of truth to prevent drift
- Define who can update public-facing copy, asset libraries, and status information
- Date-stamp News / Updates posts and archive stale notices for casting/jobs
- Any investor/partner language should be reviewed for compliance and positioning risk before publication
- Casting and job pages must clearly state timeline, geographic constraints, compensation status where applicable, and submission rules
- Use a lightweight editorial workflow with approval for high-visibility pages and quick publish capability for updates

## Technical Assumptions

- Static-first or hybrid website architecture is preferable for performance, reliability, and simple deployment
- CMS may be useful for News / Updates, FAQ, casting notices, and job postings, but should not force generic layouts onto core brand pages
- Email capture should integrate with a mailing platform that supports tagging by intent if possible
- Forms should route by inquiry type to distinct inboxes or filtered workflows
- Asset delivery for press materials should support direct download without friction
- The site should be mobile-capable from day one, without sacrificing the desktop-realism aesthetic on larger screens
- Basic privacy, cookie, and form-consent requirements should be handled from launch

## Accessibility

- Treat accessibility as part of legitimacy, not a compliance afterthought
- Ensure strong color contrast even if the palette is dark, glossy, or low-key
- Provide semantic page structure, keyboard navigation, clear focus states, and accessible forms
- Motion and surreal interface effects should respect reduced-motion preferences
- Images, stills, and interactive visual treatments need meaningful alt text or equivalent labeling where appropriate
- Avoid critical information existing only inside stylized faux-UI graphics

## SEO Strategy

SEO should be disciplined and factual, not volume-driven.

- Optimize for branded search: `WE OUT HERE`, creator names, series title + format terms
- Create indexable, information-rich pages for About, Episodes, Characters, Press Kit, and News
- Use metadata that clearly states the project is a TV series / web series / indie television project as appropriate to final positioning
- Maintain clean titles, descriptions, social share cards, and image previews
- Use structured data where relevant for organization, articles/news posts, and media project entities
- Publish updates periodically so the site demonstrates activity and remains crawl-worthy
- Avoid thin placeholder pages that weaken overall search quality signals

## Analytics Recommendations

- Track page-level traffic by audience-intent page clusters: public, press, recruitment, investor, community
- Track CTA clicks separately by type: email signup, press kit access, contact, casting, jobs, investor inquiry, Discord/community join
- Measure referrers from press, social, festivals, newsletters, and direct shares
- Track form completion rates and drop-off points
- Review device splits to ensure the desktop realism concept does not impair mobile use
- Set up lightweight event tracking and privacy-conscious analytics from launch

Suggested key metrics:

- Email conversion rate
- Press kit engagement rate
- Qualified inquiry volume by channel
- Repeat visitor rate
- Update-post traffic over time
- Community join conversion from Home and News pages

## Asset Requirements

- Final logo and wordmark variants
- Brand typography guidance and color palette
- Hero stills / key art with both landscape and portrait crops
- Character stills or portraits
- Short teaser or loopable motion assets if available
- Creator bios and headshots
- Credits list and project metadata
- Downloadable press kit assets in web-friendly and high-res formats
- Optional worldbuilding artifacts: chat screenshots, faux dashboards, notes, system overlays, production ephemera

## Rollout Phases

### Phase 1: Credibility Launch

- Publish Home, About the Series, Press Kit, Contact
- Add email capture and basic analytics
- Establish visual system and canonical messaging
- Goal: legitimacy, discoverability, shareability

### Phase 2: Story World Expansion

- Add Episodes, Characters, FAQ, News / Updates
- Start public editorial rhythm with milestone posts
- Goal: deepen engagement and improve press/festival usability

### Phase 3: Recruitment And Community

- Add Casting, Jobs / Join the Production, Community / Discord
- Introduce audience-specific forms and submission workflows
- Goal: support operational needs without breaking brand coherence

### Phase 4: Partner Readiness

- Add or refine For Investors / Partners
- Tighten materials, data points, and contact routing for serious conversations
- Goal: make the project easy to evaluate for aligned backers and collaborators

## Risks To Avoid

- Looking like a placeholder campaign instead of a real project
- Over-indexing on aesthetics at the expense of factual clarity
- Making press or industry visitors dig through “lore” to get basic information
- Letting recruitment or investor messaging feel scam-adjacent, vague, or legally sloppy
- Building a generic landing page that flattens the project’s tone and intelligence
- Creating too many empty future-facing pages before there is enough content to support them

## Success Criteria

- A first-time visitor understands the premise, format, tone, and seriousness of the project within seconds
- Press and industry users can retrieve core facts and assets without friction
- The site creates a strong enough impression to support outreach, submissions, and referral sharing
- Email capture and contact flows produce qualified interest across multiple audience types
- The website feels like a coherent extension of the series world rather than a marketing wrapper around it
