## Content

## Page Goal

Provide a legitimate, safe, and clearly scoped public surface for casting notices without inviting chaos, spam, or ambiguity.

## Page States

### State A. No Active Casting Calls

Required content:

- Clear inactive message
- Guidance to join updates
- Link to Contact only if general casting inquiries are accepted
- Optional note on future opportunities

### State B. Active Casting Calls

Required content:

- Page intro
- List of active roles
- Submission process rules
- Deadline information
- Eligibility / geography / union status if relevant
- Contact or submission form

## Required Sections

### 1. Page Hero

- H1: `Casting`
- Active or inactive status line
- Short intro
- CTA: `Get Updates` or `Submit Materials`

### 2. Casting Process Overview

Explain:

- Whether calls are public, invite-only, or mixed
- Whether self-tapes are accepted
- How submissions are reviewed
- Expected response boundaries

### 3. Active Roles List

Per role fields:

- Role title
- Character summary
- Age range if needed and legally appropriate
- Location
- Shoot timing window
- Compensation status
- Submission deadline
- Submission instructions

### 4. Submission Rules

Required rules:

- Follow the listed brief exactly
- Do not send unrelated materials
- Do not submit if you do not match stated role criteria where legally permitted
- Only one submission per role unless updated materials are requested
- Response does not imply audition or callback

### 5. Legitimacy Signals

Contents:

- Link to About the Series
- Link to Press Kit
- Named production contact or official submissions route
- Privacy note for submitted materials

### 6. FAQ Snippets

Possible questions:

- Are unsolicited submissions accepted?
- Will everyone receive a response?
- Are travel or housing covered?
- Can agents submit on behalf of talent?

## Functional Requirements

- Ability to switch between inactive and active states quickly.
- Role listings should be structured, not hardcoded longform only.
- Deadlines should expire cleanly.
- Submission forms should support links and uploads if required.
- Spam protection is required.

## Tone Requirements

- Professional and calm
- Never vague or hypey
- Specific enough to feel safe and credible

## Risks To Avoid

- Looking like an open casting scam
- Missing compensation or location details when they are required
- Burying deadlines or submission rules
- Leaving stale active calls live after close

## Analytics

- Active role clicks
- Submission starts
- Submission completions
- Update-signup conversions from inactive state

## Success Criteria

- Applicants understand whether casting is active within seconds.
- Real candidates know exactly what to do.
- The page reduces off-process outreach.
