## Content

## Core Direction

The site should feel like a prestige series dossier built by people who actually understand startup language, online status performance, and contemporary creative labor from the inside. It should not feel like a startup landing page, a studio corporate site, or an AI-generated entertainment template.

Required tone stack:

- Premium
- Internet-literate
- Indie prestige
- Startup rot
- Psychologically sharp

## Creative Reference Logic

Think in the overlap between:

- Editorial culture site
- Prestige television microsite
- Founder-era product launch page after the soul has started leaking out
- Digital dossier / archive / operator dashboard

The project should feel authored, not optimized.

## What To Avoid

- Generic SaaS gradients and glassmorphism
- Empty futurist branding language
- Huge anonymous hero banners with floating cards
- Safe studio-black layouts with nothing specific happening
- Overbuilt “cinematic” moodiness that hides information
- Fake startup polish that makes the project look scam-adjacent

## Visual Language

### Layout

- Use modular editorial compositions with strong horizontal rhythm.
- Favor asymmetry that still scans clearly.
- Let sections feel like screens, records, notes, dossiers, or fragments of a larger operating system.
- Break out of the standard centered-marketing-column pattern.

### Surface Treatment

- Dark base with precise contrast, not muddy low-contrast black-on-black.
- Use contained panels, ruled lines, metadata labels, timestamps, and system framing sparingly.
- Panels can feel slightly invasive or surveillant, as if the interface is watching the visitor back.

### Imagery

- Use stills, portraits, faux desktop states, cropped conversations, notes, overlays, and production ephemera.
- Imagery should imply social pressure and interior fracture, not just look glossy.
- Prefer frames with human tension over empty atmospheric photography.

### Motion

- Motion should feel intentional and faintly uncanny.
- Use subtle hover reveals, panel shifts, signal flickers, or timestamp transitions.
- Avoid over-slick product-demo motion.

## Typography Direction

Use a pairing that creates tension between editorial seriousness and contemporary interface readability.

Recommended system shape:

- Display face: sharp, high-contrast serif or eccentric neo-grotesk with cultural weight
- Text / UI face: modern sans with excellent readability and neutral precision

Typography behavior:

- Headlines should feel authored, not ad-like.
- Metadata, labels, and system text can run smaller and tighter.
- Use uppercase labels selectively for dossier feel.
- Preserve readable line lengths on mobile and desktop.

## Color Direction

Recommended palette behavior:

- Dominant near-black or deep charcoal base
- Bone, smoke, and muted paper tones for text and surfaces
- One acidic or alert accent for interaction and status moments
- One bruised or emotional accent for depth

Suggested palette mood, not final hex:

- Base: graphite, tar, smoked navy, dead-screen black
- Text: chalk, off-white, cool gray
- Accent 1: toxic chartreuse or electric citron used sparingly
- Accent 2: bruised plum, oxidized blue, or dirty red-violet

## UI Motifs

- Metadata chips
- Status rows
- Framed image windows
- Annotation callouts
- Time-based logs
- Filing labels
- Selective cursor-like prompts

Use these as texture, not gimmick.

## Homepage Art Direction

- Open with a strong textual statement and a high-tension visual frame.
- Hero should feel like entering a live system, not seeing a film-poster billboard.
- The first screen should communicate authorship, genre, and seriousness in under ten seconds.
- Build the page as a sequence of revelations, not stacked marketing modules.

## Page-Specific Notes

### Press Kit

- More structured and factual than Home.
- High clarity, low ambiguity.
- Still within brand language, but optimized for retrieval and trust.

### Casting And Jobs

- Clean, sober, and credible.
- Avoid over-stylizing application flows.
- Make deadlines, requirements, and contact rules impossible to miss.

### Investors / Partners

- Private-feeling, restrained, competent.
- Use confidence and clarity instead of hype.

### Community

- More porous, slightly warmer, but still selective.
- Avoid gamified growth language.

## Mobile Experience

- Mobile should keep the dossier logic without collapsing into generic stacked cards.
- Prioritize sharp typography, clear hierarchy, and cropped surfaces.
- Make navigation and CTA access effortless.
- Ensure visual frames scale cleanly and do not hide core facts.

## Accessibility Notes

- Contrast must remain strong even in dark mode-heavy compositions.
- Motion effects must support reduced motion.
- Decorative faux-interface details must never contain essential information.
- Focus states should feel part of the visual system, not default leftovers.

## Deliverables For Design Team

- Homepage desktop and mobile comps
- Core page template comps: About, Press, News index, Contact
- Opportunity template comp for Casting and Jobs
- Form styling and state system
- Typography scale and spacing rules
- Color palette with accessible pairings
- Component-level interaction notes

## Approval Criteria

- Does the site feel culturally specific to WE OUT HERE rather than broadly premium?
- Does it preserve clarity for press, recruiting, and investor use cases?
- Does it avoid generic AI SaaS aesthetics?
- Does it still work on mobile without losing edge?
