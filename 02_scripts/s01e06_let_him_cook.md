## Episode Title
"Let Him Cook()"

## Logline
Facing reputational damage, client instability, and the seduction of one last comeback, the trio stage a live transparency-demo event around `bl0ng`'s patched-together support workflow, hoping boldness can outrun consequence one more time.

## A/B/C Story Summary
- A Story: The trio accept a live roundtable invitation and secretly turn it into a product demonstration designed to reclaim the Cataclysm narrative.
- B Story: `Jessa Vale` considers cutting ties, while `Owen Kaye` and the audience now watch with informed skepticism instead of awe.
- C Story: The trio briefly recover their old chemistry preparing the event, which only makes the collapse sadder when reality breaks through.

## Theme / Psychodynamic Function
The finale is consequence as contact with reality, not moral redemption. They are still funny, still brilliant together, and still not protected from what they built.

## Cold Open
The trio set up a war room with gas-station snacks and doomed optimism. `bl0ng` says the stack is unstable but expressive. `twinkle.puss` says, "let him cook," and for one brief second it feels like the old magic might work.

## Teaser
Against Ariane's better judgment and because she cannot fully leave the machine she understands too well, the group decides to turn an ethics roundtable into a live demonstration of Cataclysm's actual thinking.

## Full Act Structure
- Cold Open: War room and intoxicating reunion rhythm.
- Teaser: The live event becomes a stealth comeback launch.
- Act One: The trio prep the demo, rehearse narratives, and almost become themselves again.
- Act Two: The roundtable starts strong, then the live system begins revealing the exact shortcuts and training inputs they hoped to aestheticize.
- Act Three: Public consequence lands in real time. The event punctures the company myth without fully ending the friendship.
- Tag: Aftermath. No clean lesson. Just damaged contact.

## Scene List
1. Cold open: war room catering.
2. Decision to do the event.
3. Rehearsal and chemistry return.
4. Ariane's final warning.
5. Live roundtable begins.

8. Fallout call.
9. Final unstable aftermath.

## Full Screenplay-Style Markdown Script

### COLD OPEN

**INT. BL0NG'S APARTMENT - EVENING**

The war room is humiliatingly sincere.

Energy drinks. Gummy worms. Extension cords. One printed run-of-show.

On the whiteboard: `ROUND TABLE / DEMO / NARRATIVE RECLAMATION`.

`bl0ng` hovers over a mess of windows and scripts.

**bl0ng**  
To be clear, the stack is unstable but expressive.

**twinkle.puss**  
Let him cook.

`ariane.emory` looks at both of them like she briefly misses the version of her life where these words had no power.

TITLE: `WE OUT HERE`

### TEASER

### SCENE 1

**INT. BL0NG'S APARTMENT - CONTINUOUS**

The live roundtable invite sits open.

Panel title: `ETHICS, VELOCITY, AND TRUST IN AI CONSULTING`.

**ariane.emory**  
We could simply appear, answer questions, and not convert this into a stunt.

**twinkle.puss**  
That would communicate weakness and a tragic overinvestment in penance.

**bl0ng**  
Also I may have prepared a lightweight live environment that shows how we actually reason through support ambiguity.

**ariane.emory**  
Of course you did.

**twinkle.puss**  
This is perfect. Radical transparency. They want ethics; we give them the machine and the blood type.

**ariane.emory**  
No moral theatrics. No blood types. And if the system starts behaving strangely, we stop.

`twinkle.puss` and `bl0ng` say nothing. Which is its own answer.

### ACT ONE

### SCENE 2

**INT. REHEARSAL MONTAGE - NIGHT**

Desktop realism and live action.

- Twinkle writes intros that sound half-apology, half-coup.
- Bl0ng tunes prompts and routing rules.
- Ariane rewrites the run-of-show into actual sentences people can follow.
- Old rhythms reappear.

**twinkle.puss**  
We are not here to defend ambiguity. We are here to show what institutions look like before they lie about being coherent.

**bl0ng**  
That's almost too good. It may summon God or reporters.

**ariane.emory**  
Please write one sentence that a regular person would survive hearing.

They all laugh. It feels awful and great because it feels like before.

### SCENE 3

**INT. BL0NG'S KITCHEN - LATER**

While Twinkle takes a call, Ariane and Bl0ng stand alone for the first time in a while.

**ariane.emory**  
Tell me plainly if this thing can expose client material.

**bl0ng**  
Not directly.

**ariane.emory**  
That adverb is making me want to die.

**bl0ng**  
It was built on patterns abstracted from prior pilot work and internal notes. It's not a data leak. It's a style leak, maybe.

**ariane.emory**  
That's still a leak.

**bl0ng**  
I know.

Beat.

**bl0ng**  
I just... wanted there to be one thing we made that looked like the intelligence we keep claiming.

That is the most honest he has been all season.

**ariane.emory**  
I know.

Then, because honesty never gets to stay clean:

**ariane.emory**  
Don't send that in machine form.

### SCENE 4

**INT. HALLWAY - CONTINUOUS**

Twinkle overhears enough to know the shape, not the detail.

**twinkle.puss**  
What's the energy.

**ariane.emory**  
The energy is if this goes sideways, it stops immediately.

**twinkle.puss**  
You keep phrasing interruption like it's neutral. For some of us it feels like dying in business casual.

**ariane.emory**  
For some of us not interrupting feels like unpaid custodianship until death.

They hold each other's gaze. No resolution. Just contact.

### ACT TWO

### SCENE 5

**INT. LIVE VIRTUAL ROUNDTABLE - NIGHT**

Panel layout. Moderator. `Owen Kaye`. Two other consultants. The trio.

Viewer count ticks upward.

**MODERATOR**  
Cataclysm, you've had a lively week.

**twinkle.puss**  
Love to be introduced as weather.

Small laugh. He has them.

The roundtable starts well.

`ariane.emory` is measured. `bl0ng` is lucid. `twinkle.puss` is electric but disciplined enough to pass.

Then the moderator asks the wrong right question.

**MODERATOR**  
You talk a lot about ambiguity. Can you show, concretely, what responsible ambiguity management looks like in practice.

Twinkle looks at Bl0ng. There it is. The opening.

**twinkle.puss**  
Actually, yes.

**ariane.emory**  
We can describe-

Too late. Screen share starts.

### SCENE 6

**INT. SCREEN SHARE / LIVE DEMO**

Bl0ng's system appears: support ticket intake, categorization suggestions, escalation notes, confidence flags.

At first it is impressive.

Then a sample ticket generates a note:

`Customer presents with anger masking abandonment by product promises.`

Audience chat moves.

`lol therapy bot`  
`is this based on client data`  
`what the hell`

**bl0ng**  
That phrasing is from an interpretive layer we usually keep behind the curtain-

Another generated recommendation:

`Offer procedural warmth. Avoid truth entering system too early.`

There it is. The leak is no longer a screenshot. It is a product voice.

`ariane.emory` reaches toward him off-screen.

**ariane.emory**  
Stop the share.

**twinkle.puss**  
Wait.

Why. Because for one insane second he thinks maybe they can turn this too.

**twinkle.puss**  
What you're seeing is the tension every company hides: support work is emotional labor plus categorization theater. We built language around that contradiction because pretending it isn't there is how harm scales.

It is smart. It is partly true. It is too late.

`Owen Kaye` speaks quietly.

**Owen Kaye**  
Were these system prompts developed from client-facing work.

Silence.

### ACT THREE

### SCENE 7

**INT. LIVE ROUNDTABLE - CONTINUOUS**

Viewer count spikes.

Audience chat floods but we only see selected lines.

`this is insane`  
`they're proving the thread right live`  
`someone screen record`

**ariane.emory**  
We're ending the demo.

She kills the screen share herself.

The act is tiny and seismic. She has interrupted momentum in public.

**MODERATOR**  
Should we pause.

**twinkle.puss**  
No. We can answer.

**ariane.emory**  
Some of our internal language and prototyping practices moved faster than our boundaries. That's true, and it's on us.

**twinkle.puss**  
Ariane-

**ariane.emory**  
No speeches. Just true. We tried to professionalize confused demand faster than we professionalized ourselves.

That is the closest the season gets to explicit insight, so the show immediately resists sanctimony.

**bl0ng**  
Which, to be clear, is not unique to us.

The moderator blinks. Audience chat groans. He can't help himself.

**twinkle.puss**  
He's right, which is sadly his worst trait.

Even now, a laugh. Because they are still themselves.

### SCENE 8

**INT. BACKSTAGE CALL / POST-EVENT HOLDING ROOM - LATER**

The event has ended early.

Phones erupt.

Message from `Jessa Vale`: `We are done after current invoices. Please preserve all materials.`

Message from `Maya Quill`: `call me if you want triage, not absolution`

Message from `Niko Stern`: `that was either career-ending or category-creating`

`twinkle.puss` laughs once, then doesn't know what to do with his face.

**twinkle.puss**  
Please tell me this is real.

Same line. Different temperature.

No one answers immediately.

**bl0ng**  
Yes.

He says it like a man returning an item to the material world.

### TAG

**EXT. STREET OUTSIDE BL0NG'S APARTMENT - VERY LATE NIGHT**

The three of them stand outside with coats half on, too tired to separate.

Traffic. Siren far away. Phones mostly face down.

**twinkle.puss**  
So what are we now.

**ariane.emory**  
Tired.

**bl0ng**  
Legible.

**twinkle.puss**  
Horrible answer.

Beat.

**ariane.emory**  
We don't have to name it tonight.

**twinkle.puss**  
That's the hottest thing you've ever said to me.

Tiny laugh. Real, wrecked.

Then a notification sound from one of their phones.

None of them check it.

Cut to black.

## Character Objective Tracking
- `twinkle.puss`: Convert scandal into mythic comeback and preserve the company's charge long enough to avoid direct contact with failure.
- `bl0ng`: Prove the underlying intelligence of Cataclysm's methods even if doing so exposes the contaminated inputs that shaped them.
- `ariane.emory`: Prevent irreversible harm, finally interrupt momentum in public, and survive the guilt of doing it too late.

## Running Jokes and Callback Opportunities
- `let him cook` becomes operational command and epitaph.
- `don't send that` returns as Ariane's plain warning about the demo.
- `truth entering the system too early` comes back as public evidence.
- The final unanswered notification preserves the show's instability.

## Production Notes
- The live roundtable should feel like a real digital event, not TV-fake webinar language.
- The demo outputs must be sharp, readable, and few; each one should escalate meaning.
- Ariane interrupting the screen share is the action beat of the finale.
- End on damaged intimacy, not catharsis.
