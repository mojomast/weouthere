## Cold Open Bank

### S01E01 - "Don't Send That"
Logline function: Start with humiliation, misattunement, and the exact moment a bit becomes a business.

1. **The Draft Everyone Knows Should Die**
   Three-way Backchannel chat. `twinkle.puss` writes an unhinged outbound message to a founder who just posted a panic-thread about AI adoption. `ariane.emory` says "don't send that." `bl0ng` says the message is conceptually strong but legally humid. It sends anyway because `twinkle.puss` is screen-sharing and hits enter while defending himself.

2. **Public Panel, Private Spiral**
   Cut between a low-rent industry panel and a private Backchannel channel where the trio live-roast the moderator. `twinkle.puss` is asked what he does now after a recent layoff. He says, before thinking, "I run a consultancy." The others stare at the notification in real time.

3. **Bank Balance Ballet**
   A desktop montage of declining balances, overdue invoices, and ignored recruiters. It plays like a heist setup except nobody has a plan. The cold open ends when a prospect replies, `can you send capabilities?`

### S01E02 - "Terms of Abuse"
Logline function: Show that the company's first adult documents are improvised under panic.

1. **Checkbox Theology**
   `bl0ng` and `ariane.emory` argue about a signup flow on a shared screen. Every checkbox is either meaningless or discoverable. `twinkle.puss` wants one that says `by continuing you acknowledge the future is non-linear`.

2. **The Redline Horror Short**
   Ariane opens a returned contract. The screen fills with comments. `twinkle.puss` says, "if legal loved us there'd be fewer words." `bl0ng` whispers, "they found the sentence where I tried to abolish liability through vibes."

3. **Deposit Fever Dream**
   The first client deposit lands during an argument about data retention. The joy is immediate and vulgar. Nobody becomes more ethical after seeing the number.

### S01E03 - "The Manic Defense"
Logline function: Introduce the doctrine that instability is premium.

1. **Version Naming as Delusion Management**
   A deliverable keeps changing names every few minutes: `beta`, `soft launch`, `insight map`, `cognitive layer`. The open ends on `twinkle.puss` renaming a broken file `v_final_psychographic_MASTER_REAL`.

2. **Calendar Triage**
   `ariane.emory` tries to move a client meeting because the deck does not exist. `twinkle.puss` says rescheduling would be spiritually incriminating. `bl0ng` adds a meeting note: `scarcity increases perceived sophistication.`

3. **The Internal Voice Memo**
   `twinkle.puss` sends a six-minute voice note explaining why a missed deadline is actually a sign of visionary non-compliance. The others listen at 1.5x speed like it is a hostage video.

### S01E04 - "Contain Me Daddy"
Logline function: Make the need for limits explicit and pathetic.

1. **The Advisor Intake Form**
   The trio complete an advisor application together and cannot answer `What support do you need?` without sounding romantic, adversarial, or criminal.

2. **Governance Kink Bit**
   `twinkle.puss` jokes that what they need is "one hot adult with a calendar and the power to revoke permissions." Nobody fully treats it as a joke.

3. **Muted Before Noon**
   A would-be operator joins a call and immediately asks for access controls, decision rights, and boundaries. Cut to the trio privately messaging each other like they are trapped in intake at an expensive clinic.

### S01E05 - "The Superego Collapse"
Logline function: Show archived irony becoming evidence.

1. **Screenshot Roulette**
   `ariane.emory` wakes to thirteen notifications and a single screenshot from a private Backchannel channel posted in a public forum. Nobody knows which leak matters yet, which makes all of them matter.

2. **Brand Voice Possession**
   A scheduled post goes live in Cataclysm's account using internal sarcastic phrasing. It sounds more honest than any apology they could write.

3. **The Search Result**
   `bl0ng` types the company name into a search bar. Before he hits enter he says, "I'm doing violence to myself for clarity." The result page arrives like a verdict.

### S01E06 - "Let Him Cook()"
Logline function: Give the finale one last intoxicating surge before reality lands.

1. **War Room Catering for Three**
   They prepare for a live stream with convenience-store snacks arranged like a launch party. `twinkle.puss` says the spread should communicate both abundance and subpoena risk.

2. **The Demo Machine Breathes**
   On screen, a patched-together automation stack starts generating outputs in real time from internal Cataclysm materials. Everyone knows this is a bad idea and feels briefly chosen anyway.

3. **Hold Music Before the Fall**
   While waiting for a legal callback, the trio rehearse a triumphant transparency event. The banal hold music undercuts every claim to destiny.

## Selection Guidance
- Default to opens that start with a concrete digital artifact.
- Keep cold opens under two and a half pages.
- End each open on a commitment, reply, or exposure event.
- Avoid opens that require extras-heavy spectacle; the shame should be specific, not large.
- Keep `Backchannel` as the locked platform reference in all on-screen language.
- Differentiate joke engines cleanly: `twinkle.puss` sells fate, `bl0ng` abstracts risk, `ariane.emory` drags everyone back to a plain sentence.

## Cross-Episode Use Notes
- Reuse notification sounds strategically so the audience associates them with danger and reward.
- Let message drafts, cursor hovering, and deletion pauses carry tension.
- Bank one strong opener alt per episode for production flexibility.
