## Table Read Summary
The episode reads as a strong midseason containment test. `Dr. Liora Voss` arrives with real authority and instantly reveals the trio's fantasy about structure. The material works best when her scenes feel specific, expensive, and grounded rather than broadly psychoanalytic. The major table-read note is balance: the title joke is excellent, but the episode has to keep earning the deeper read that they want a parent-shaped permission system.

## Recommended Performance Notes
- `Dr. Liora Voss` should be funny through precision and calm, never mysticism.
- `twinkle.puss` should sound almost adolescent when asking how to contain something without killing it.
- `ariane.emory` needs to play exhaustion, not righteousness.
- `bl0ng` should enjoy being decoded more than he wants to admit.

## Line Tightening Pass
- Keep `adult with admin permissions` and `contain me daddy`; both are structurally important.
- Trim one or two riffs around governance if they begin saying the same thing in different phrasing.
- Preserve `The governance layer is me.` exactly.
- In the final fight, keep the lines short and lethal.

## Pace / Runtime Notes
- The cold open is efficient and should stay quick.
- The intake session should take real space; it is the episode.
- The hallway conversation between `ariane.emory` and `Dr. Liora Voss` is the quiet hinge and should not be rushed.
- The tag works because it arrives immediately after the failed containment attempt.

## Emotional Clarity Notes
- The episode is not about whether they need help; it is about what kind of help they can tolerate.
- `ariane.emory` asking `Can this work` should feel like the closest thing she gets to asking for rescue.
- `twinkle.puss`' hurt at being told structure is necessary should register before he converts it back into charm.

## Production Practicality Notes
- The episode can stay visually simple if `Dr. Liora Voss`' presence changes the room.
- Avoid overdesigned framework graphics; one clean advisory slide is enough.
- Side-chat inserts should be brief and sharp so they feel like contamination, not a second episode.

## Priority Revisions
### Must
- Keep `Dr. Liora Voss` grounded and credible; if she feels guru-like, the episode weakens.
- Protect the line `A boundary isn't a critique of aliveness.` as the episode's cleanest piece of outside truth.
- Make the final refusal feel principled, not cold.

### Should
- Slightly trim the joke density during the governance agenda scene.
- Clarify that `ariane.emory` also wanted containment, not just the others.
- Keep the final fight clipped enough that no one wins it rhetorically.

### Optional
- Let `Dr. Liora Voss` clock the side-chat energy without explicitly calling it out.
- Give the unopened framework email a small visual emphasis in the tag.
