## Table Read Summary
The episode reads clearly and escalates well from deposit euphoria to document dread. `Rina` and `Maya Quill` are especially useful because they force plain language into a show built on evasive language. The strongest table-read note is rhythm: the legal material is good, but it needs active comedic compression so the episode feels tense rather than explanatory.

## Recommended Performance Notes
- `ariane.emory` should sound more frightened by imprecision than by conflict.
- `bl0ng` works best when he treats clause-building like a technical art form he knows is spiritually dirty.
- `twinkle.puss` should make ambiguity sound luxurious until the adults in the room refuse the framing.
- `Rina` should be clean, calm, and unwasteful; her power is that she never rises to their temperature.

## Line Tightening Pass
- Cut or simplify any clause joke that requires too much audience parsing on first listen.
- Keep `operationally necessary` and `time-bounded truth`; both are episode-defining contamination lines.
- Compress the `trust posture` riff if the scene starts stacking synonyms for the same joke.
- Preserve `This is evidence with permissions.` exactly; it cleanly links to later episodes.

## Pace / Runtime Notes
- The cold open lands fast and should stay underplayed.
- The Maya call is strong but can be clipped slightly if the hallway geography slows momentum.
- The call with `Jessa Vale` and `Rina` is the centerpiece and should breathe.
- The chicken-shop celebration should feel uneasy, not victorious.

## Emotional Clarity Notes
- The episode is not about whether they understand contracts; it is about whether they can tolerate what contracts remember.
- `ariane.emory`'s compromise in leaving `as part of this pilot` should register as a small moral loss.
- `Jessa Vale` is not fooled; she is actively choosing a narrow, survivable yes.

## Production Practicality Notes
- Keep all onscreen contract language shootable and selective.
- The vendor portal gag should rely on one or two killer fields, not full-form realism.
- The side-letter scene can be staged almost entirely on faces and cursor movement.

## Priority Revisions
### Must
- Make the side-letter negotiation the ethical hinge of the episode, not just a procedural solve.
- Ensure `Rina`'s questions are plain enough that every dodge is audible in real time.
- Trim any legal-comedy density that muddies the audience's understanding of what is actually being promised.

### Should
- Strengthen the deposit beat as a brief intoxication so the later dread bites harder.
- Give `Maya Quill` one line that more directly frames discoverability as future narrative, not just legal exposure.
- Keep `HexadecimalBoy` present but peripheral.

### Optional
- Add a tiny reaction from `twinkle.puss` when plain English wins, just to mark the humiliation.
- Let the chicken-shop scene end one line earlier if runtime is tight.
