## Table Read Summary
The episode reads strong as a pilot because the lie-to-company turn is immediate, social, and specific. The panel humiliation, the Backchannel chat grammar, and `Jessa Vale`'s reply all land. The main table-read opportunity is calibration: the trio are funniest when they sound one half-step from competence, not already like a polished boutique firm. The strongest emotional beat is the pricing exchange, which gives the pilot a real bruise under the bit.

## Recommended Performance Notes
- `twinkle.puss` should play the panel lie as survival intoxication, not slick confidence.
- `ariane.emory` works best when she is fast, dry, and already ten consequences ahead.
- `bl0ng` should sound delighted by language that solves panic, then briefly ashamed of enjoying it.
- `Jessa Vale` should not read as gullible; she is choosing speed because she is trapped.

## Line Tightening Pass
- Trim one or two consultant-premium lines in the panel and deck-build sections so the invention feels fresher when it starts working.
- Keep `spiritually flattening their people`; it is the first line that tells us why this lie works.
- In the apartment war-room stretch, cut duplicate variations on `we are not a real company` once the joke is established.
- Preserve `don't send that` exactly; it is a clean seasonal anchor.

## Pace / Runtime Notes
- The cold open and teaser are fast and should stay fast.
- The overnight deck-build section risks feeling one beat too long if every naming and bio joke lands at equal weight.
- The first `Jessa Vale` call should feel like the episode's hinge, not just the next scene.
- The tag is good and short; keep legal entering as a hard tonal turn.

## Emotional Clarity Notes
- The pilot needs one unmistakable read that the company is born from humiliation, not pure ambition.
- `ariane.emory` is not merely anti-fun; she is trying to stop shame from becoming paperwork.
- `twinkle.puss`' line about needing this to be real by tomorrow should stay lightly played, because it gives the audience the actual wound.

## Production Practicality Notes
- Keep screen inserts minimal and legible: panel chat, outbound DM, reply, proposal acceptance.
- The apartment should carry the whole homemade-firm energy without needing many company-world locations.
- The logo joke works if it is visible for only a beat.

## Priority Revisions
### Must
- Reduce any dialogue that makes `Cataclysm Consulting` sound too operationally mature before `Jessa` validates them.
- Make `Jessa Vale`'s need slightly more concrete on first watch: she needs a support-automation pilot and a sentence leadership, legal, and support can all survive.
- Protect the pricing scene as the pilot's emotional center.

### Should
- Tighten repeated deck-version and bio jokes by 10-15%.
- Give `ariane.emory` one cleaner line early that frames the company as a taxable accident.
- Keep `HexadecimalBoy` funny but brief so he feels like an omen, not a subplot.

### Optional
- Add one tiny visual callback to the `we out here` phrase earlier so the tag feels even more inevitable.
- Let the proposal footer joke land faster.
