## Episode Title
"Terms of Abuse"

## Logline
As `Cataclysm Consulting` tries to close its first paid pilot, the trio discover that every adult document meant to make them safe also creates evidence, forcing them to abuse the gap between comprehension and assent.

## A/B/C Story Summary
- A Story: `Jessa Vale` wants to move forward, but procurement and legal require a contract stack the trio are nowhere near qualified to produce.
- B Story: `ariane.emory` consults her compliance-world contact `Maya Quill`, hoping for a reality check she can live with.
- C Story: The arrival of real money makes everyone immediately more willing to tolerate ambiguity.

## Theme / Psychodynamic Function
Formalization feels like adulthood until the paper starts remembering you. The episode tracks how documentation becomes both a shield and a future crime scene.

## Cold Open
The first client deposit lands at the exact moment an MSA covered in redlines opens on screen. Joy and dread arrive in the same notification stack.

## Teaser
The trio confront vendor intake forms, insurance questions, and data-use clauses that expose how little infrastructure actually exists behind the company they invented overnight.

## Full Act Structure
- Cold Open: Money arrives; institutional scrutiny arrives faster.
- Teaser: Procurement documents reveal the shape of the lie.
- Act One: `bl0ng` assembles a pseudo-legal stack from templates and generated language while `ariane.emory` tries to understand the real exposure.
- Act Two: A client-legal conversation forces the trio to answer clean questions with contaminated language.
- Act Three: They close the deal by making the ambiguity legible enough to sign but muddy enough to survive.
- Tag: Access to sensitive support data is granted.

## Scene List
1. Cold open: deposit and redlines.
2. Procurement intake panic.
3. Maya reality check.
4. Frankenstein contract room.
5. Insurance and data questions.
6. Call with `Jessa Vale` and legal.
7. Side-letter improvisation.
8. Signing celebration.
9. Tag: data access credentials.

## Full Screenplay-Style Markdown Script

### COLD OPEN

**INT. BL0NG'S APARTMENT - MORNING**

Split screen.

On one side: banking app. Incoming ACH: `VALE AI - 50% DEPOSIT`.

On the other: PDF comments blooming in violent red.

`twinkle.puss` screams through speakerphone.

**twinkle.puss (PHONE)**  
Money. Real money. I need everyone to get spiritually upright.

**bl0ng**  
Counterpoint: the MSA contains the phrase `indemnify and hold harmless` seven times.

On Backchannel:

`ariane.emory`: do not spend it in your mind yet  
`twinkle.puss`: too late i already bought dignity and a better lamp  
`bl0ng`: legal has entered the building with knives made of grammar

TITLE: `WE OUT HERE`

### TEASER

### SCENE 1

**INT. ARIANE.EMORY'S APARTMENT - DAY**

All three on laptops. The MSA is projected to the TV like a horror film.

Highlighted clauses:

- professional liability insurance required
- data security controls
- no third-party model training on client data
- mutual confidentiality
- service provider warrants compliance with applicable laws

**twinkle.puss**  
This document has an abusive attachment style.

**ariane.emory**  
This document assumes we're a company.

**bl0ng**  
It also assumes laws are enumerable.

**ariane.emory**  
We need to decide which questions we can answer truthfully.

**twinkle.puss**  
Love that you still think truth is a binary resource.

`ariane.emory` opens the vendor intake portal.

Field: `Upload Certificate of Insurance`.

All three stare.

**bl0ng**  
Can we upload a mood and call it umbrella coverage.

### SCENE 2

**INT. ARIANE.EMORY'S APARTMENT / HALLWAY - DAY**

`ariane.emory` steps out to take a call from `Maya Quill`.

**Maya Quill (PHONE)**  
Why are you whispering like a teenager buying fireworks.

**ariane.emory**  
Hypothetically, if a small advisory shop were doing a narrow AI pilot for a stressed founder and maybe lacked every adult wrapper around that arrangement-

**Maya Quill (PHONE)**  
Then hypothetically the contract is the least interesting problem.

**ariane.emory**  
Great.

**Maya Quill (PHONE)**  
Can they answer where data goes, who sees it, what tools touch it, and whether outputs are reviewable by a human?

**ariane.emory**  
Sort of.

**Maya Quill (PHONE)**  
Sort of is not a legal category. Ariane, if you're calling me, you already know this is discoverable in a weird way.

**ariane.emory**  
We're trying to keep it narrow.

**Maya Quill (PHONE)**  
No. You're trying to move faster than the part of your brain that knows what narrow means.

That lands. Ariane says nothing.

### ACT ONE

### SCENE 3

**INT. BL0NG'S APARTMENT - AFTERNOON**

Tabs everywhere: template MSAs, blog posts, insurance FAQs, generated clause options.

`bl0ng` is in his happiest state: elegant panic.

**bl0ng**  
I can make this internally coherent by four if nobody asks me whether it should exist.

**twinkle.puss**  
That's our whole company mission statement.

**ariane.emory**  
No generated legal text in the final draft.

**bl0ng**  
I'm not generating, I'm adversarially autocomplete-curating.

He pastes a clause into a side letter.

`Service Provider shall not knowingly retain client data for model training or product improvement except as operationally necessary to provide the services.`

**ariane.emory**  
`Operationally necessary` means nothing.

**bl0ng**  
Incorrect. It means whoever has more stamina in discovery wins.

**twinkle.puss**  
Leave it. That's business-adult enough to sedate people.

### SCENE 4

**INT. KITCHEN - LATE AFTERNOON**

`twinkle.puss` is building a new one-pager called `Cataclysm Trust Posture`.

**ariane.emory**  
You cannot name a PDF `trust posture` and think God doesn't see.

**twinkle.puss**  
I need a leave-behind that tells legal we know the words they worship.

**ariane.emory**  
We do not have SOC anything. We barely have chairs.

**twinkle.puss**  
Then we emphasize process discipline.

**bl0ng**  
I can draft a human review loop diagram.

**ariane.emory**  
Do we have one.

**bl0ng**  
If I draw it, morale will.

### SCENE 5

**INT. BACKCHANNEL - CONTINUOUS**

`HexadecimalBoy` has somehow made a checklist bot for the server.

`HexadecimalBoy`: built a lil vendor-intake tracker if useful  
`HexadecimalBoy`: no pressure  
`HexadecimalBoy`: it already has statuses and emoji severity

`twinkle.puss` reacts with a flame emoji.

**ariane.emory**  
Who gave him enough access to know we needed that.

**twinkle.puss**  
The future.

**bl0ng**  
Free labor is the most haunting form of fandom.

### ACT TWO

### SCENE 6

**INT. VIDEO CALL - EVENING**

Participants: `Jessa Vale`, `Jessa`'s calm internal counsel `Rina`, the trio.

`Rina` is not broad-comic scary. She is tired and exact.

**Rina**  
This should be simple. We just need to understand your process. Will customer conversations be entered into third-party models?

The trio all look at each other in tiny boxes.

**bl0ng**  
Not in any way that would degrade your ownership posture.

`ariane.emory` closes her eyes for half a beat.

**Rina**  
I'm sorry. Is that a yes or a no.

**ariane.emory**  
Outputs will be reviewed by humans before use. We can structure the pilot so sensitive inputs stay limited and access is role-based.

**Rina**  
That's more useful. Is the answer no by default unless approved.

**twinkle.puss**  
The answer is we can scope the workflow to match your comfort and risk tolerance.

**Rina**  
That also isn't an answer.

`Jessa Vale` jumps in, gently desperate.

**Jessa Vale**  
What I need from this pilot is a way to move without every team relitigating the internet. Can you give me something narrow enough to survive and useful enough to matter.

There it is. Not morality. Need.

**ariane.emory**  
Yes. Narrow internal support use case. Human review required. No autonomous customer-facing deployment. Time-boxed. Logged.

`Rina` nods.

**Rina**  
Put that in a side letter. Plain English. Then I can probably live with it.

**twinkle.puss**  
Amazing. We become enormous fans of plain English the second legal arrives.

**bl0ng**  
When legally necessary.

### SCENE 7

**INT. ARIANE.EMORY'S APARTMENT - NIGHT**

The call ends. Everyone slumps.

**twinkle.puss**  
Did she just out-negotiate us by speaking like a person.

**bl0ng**  
Weaponized clarity. Very dark.

**ariane.emory**  
We write the side letter exactly as discussed.

**twinkle.puss**  
One sentence of seasoning.

**ariane.emory**  
No seasoning. Salt is how we got here.

**bl0ng**  
What about a garnish of ambiguity.

**ariane.emory**  
Bl0ng.

He looks at her, then softens just a little.

**bl0ng**  
Okay. Fine. Plain.

That almost counts as care.

### SCENE 8

**INT. SHARED DOC - NIGHT**

We watch them write.

`The pilot will be limited to internal support operations. All AI-generated outputs will be reviewed by Cataclysm and Vale personnel before use. No client data will be used to train any external model as part of this pilot.`

**twinkle.puss**  
This reads like being handcuffed in khakis.

**ariane.emory**  
Good. Send it.

**bl0ng**  
Wait.

They both turn.

**bl0ng**  
Add `as part of this pilot` to the final sentence.

**ariane.emory**  
Why.

**bl0ng**  
Because time-bounded truth is still truth.

**ariane.emory**  
That is maybe the worst thing you've ever said.

**twinkle.puss**  
And yet, structurally, he's right.

`ariane.emory` hates that they are making her complicit through grammar.

She leaves it in.

### ACT THREE

### SCENE 9

**INT. VIDEO CALL - LATER**

`Jessa Vale` signs on camera because procurement is slow and everyone wants emotional closure.

**Jessa Vale**  
I am choosing to believe all of you know what you're doing.

**twinkle.puss**  
That is the essence of partnership.

**Rina**  
It is not.

They all laugh too hard.

Ping. Signed side letter. Signed MSA.

The deal is live.

### SCENE 10

**INT. CHICKEN SHOP / SIDEWALK TABLE - NIGHT**

They celebrate with food that arrived too fast to be good.

**twinkle.puss**  
We are a serviced business. I feel disgusting.

**bl0ng**  
I feel briefly taxable.

**ariane.emory**  
I feel like we made something signable that none of us should feel proud of.

**twinkle.puss**  
You say that like it isn't also adulthood.

`ariane.emory` checks her phone. Message from `Maya Quill`: `If you're doing harm reduction, remember harm is still in the sentence.`

She locks the phone without replying.

**twinkle.puss**  
What.

**ariane.emory**  
Nothing. Compliance fan fiction.

They laugh. It lands badly and keeps going.

### TAG

**INT. BACKCHANNEL - LATE NIGHT**

New credentials arrive.

`Vale Support Pilot - Access Granted`

Folders, logs, support transcripts.

**bl0ng**  
This is too much data for people with our nutritional habits.

**twinkle.puss**  
No. This is access dressed as trust.

**ariane.emory**  
No. This is evidence with permissions.

They open the first support transcript anyway.

Cut to black.

- `bl0ng`: Build a language shell around risk so the group can keep moving while he still feels intellectually innocent.
- `ariane.emory`: Make the arrangement narrow enough to survive contact with reality, even if that means helping manufacture permission.
