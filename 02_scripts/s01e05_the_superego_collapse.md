## Episode Title
"The Superego Collapse"

## Logline
When a screenshot from a private Backchannel thread leaks alongside a scheduled Cataclysm post written in the company's internal sarcastic voice, the trio discover that irony no longer protects intention from consequence.

## A/B/C Story Summary
- A Story: A public mini-scandal erupts after internal messages about clients, risk, and `contain me daddy` circulate out of context but not inaccurately.
- B Story: `Owen Kaye`, a mild-mannered researcher-journalist, begins asking precise questions that no spin can fully absorb.
- C Story: Each lead defaults to a different responsibility narrative, exposing old fractures around charisma, labor, and conscience.

## Theme / Psychodynamic Function
This is the season's superego event: the language that once floated as joke returns as accusation, memory, and shame. The group loses irony as a solvent.

## Cold Open
`ariane.emory` wakes to a screenshot from a private channel posted in a public ops forum. Before anyone can assess the damage, an auto-scheduled Cataclysm brand post goes live sounding more honest than any apology they could write.

## Teaser
The trio try to determine what leaked, who leaked it, and whether the public can distinguish sarcasm from evidence. The answer is no, and worse, it doesn't matter.

## Full Act Structure
- Cold Open: Screenshot leak and rogue scheduled post.
- Teaser: Search results start aggregating the damage.
- Act One: The trio scramble through blame, deletion, and spin while outsiders form a more coherent story than they can.
- Act Two: `Owen Kaye` asks plain questions; the company's internal contradictions become public.
- Act Three: The trio fracture over authorship, labor, and moral cover. Cataclysm remains technically alive but socially broken.
- Tag: A live event invite arrives anyway, proving visibility persists past disgrace.

## Full Screenplay-Style Markdown Script

### COLD OPEN

**INT. ARIANE.EMORY'S BEDROOM - 6:02 A.M.**

Phone screen in darkness.

Message from `Maya Quill`: `please tell me this isn't your company`

Attached screenshot from a public AI ops forum.

It shows a private `Cataclysm Consulting` channel snippet:

`twinkle.puss`: contain me daddy  
`bl0ng`: truth entered the system too early  
`ariane.emory`: this is evidence with permissions

Under it, strangers are commenting:

`is this a real vendor`  
`this is every ai consultancy now`  
`grim if true`

Before Ariane can sit up, another notification.

Cataclysm's official account has posted on Backchannel and mirrored to other socials:

`We specialize in operationalizing ambiguity before your legal team ruins the party.`

It is one of Twinkle's old joke drafts, scheduled by accident.

Ariane whispers, to nobody:

**ariane.emory**  
Oh, you stupid little cult.

TITLE: `WE OUT HERE`

### TEASER

### SCENE 1

**INT. GROUP CALL - MORNING**

All three faces appear instantly, already lit by disaster.

**twinkle.puss**  
Before anyone pathologizes me, the scheduled post issue is a tooling event.

**ariane.emory**  
We do not get to call self-sabotage tooling.

**bl0ng**  
The screenshot is worse.

**twinkle.puss**  
Why is the screenshot worse than the post that literally says legal ruins parties.

**bl0ng**  
Because the screenshot creates a narrative ecosystem. The post is just a bone thrown into it.

**ariane.emory**  
Who had access to that channel.

They all know the answer is too many people.

### SCENE 2

**INT. BACKCHANNEL - CONTINUOUS**

`HexadecimalBoy` is online.

`HexadecimalBoy`: i didn't leak btw  
`HexadecimalBoy`: seeing discourse now  
`HexadecimalBoy`: pretty sure screenshot came from an old synced notifications view or someone with role leftovers

**ariane.emory**  
Role leftovers.

**bl0ng**  
Beautiful phrase. Horrific concept.

### ACT ONE

### SCENE 3

**INT. BL0NG'S APARTMENT - LATE MORNING**

`bl0ng` types the company name into a search bar.

**bl0ng**  
I'm doing violence to myself for clarity.

Results populate: forum threads, quote-posts, a LinkedIn post about `consultancy culture rot`, and one mild blog note from `Owen Kaye`: `Interesting case study in AI advisory voice collapse.`

**twinkle.puss**  
Voice collapse is actually kind of beautiful.

**ariane.emory**  
Please stop admiring the knife.

### SCENE 4

**INT. VIDEO CALL - MIDDAY**

`Owen Kaye` joins. Soft voice. Mug. No visible aggression. Therefore dangerous.

**Owen Kaye**  
I don't need a statement right now. I just wanted to understand whether Cataclysm's public language reflects internal operating assumptions or isolated jokes.

**twinkle.puss**  
Love a false binary before lunch.

**Owen Kaye**  
Sure. Then maybe: how should clients interpret internal jokes about ambiguity, containment, and legal evasion.

`bl0ng` leans in despite himself.

**bl0ng**  
They should interpret them as workers metabolizing contradictory incentives with irony.

**Owen Kaye**  
That's useful context. Do your clients know that's the frame.

Silence.

**ariane.emory**  
Some of them know they're hiring us to move through ambiguity quickly. I don't think any of them imagine our internal language as... this decorated.

**Owen Kaye**  
That's candid. Thank you.

The call ends. Everyone hates how gentle he was.

### SCENE 5

**INT. ARIANE.EMORY'S APARTMENT - AFTERNOON**

Draft apology / clarification doc on screen.

Headline options:

- `Clarification on Recent Posts`
- `Statement Regarding Internal Communications`
- `Context for Screenshots Circulating Online`

**twinkle.puss**  
All of these sound guilty and moisturized.

**ariane.emory**  
We are guilty of at least process failure.

**bl0ng**  
Do not say `process failure`. That sounds like we poisoned a town.

**ariane.emory**  
What do you want to say.

**bl0ng**  
That internal irreverence got detached from the actual guardrails in our client work.

**ariane.emory**  
What guardrails.

And there it is. The room changes.

### ACT TWO

### SCENE 6

**INT. VIDEO CALL - LATER**

`Jessa Vale` is no longer flattered by their weirdness.

**Jessa Vale**  
My board has your screenshot in a deck, which is honestly humiliating in a way I didn't know was available to adults.

**twinkle.puss**  
Jessa, none of that changes the work we've done for you.

**Jessa Vale**  
It changes how I have to defend hiring you.

**ariane.emory**  
That's fair.

**Jessa Vale**  
Also, tell me plainly: when you say you operationalize ambiguity, is that a joke or the operating model.

No one wants to answer.

**bl0ng**  
It is a joke grown around a market reality.

**Jessa Vale**  
That's almost more upsetting than if you'd lied.

She ends the call after saying she'll pause scope expansion.

### SCENE 7

**INT. APARTMENT - CONTINUOUS**

Nobody wants the next scene, so naturally it arrives.

**ariane.emory**  
We need to say out loud that this is not just a leak problem. This is a pattern problem.

**twinkle.puss**  
No, this is a scale problem. We got visible before our enemies got tasteful.

**ariane.emory**  
Our enemies.

**twinkle.puss**  
People love a certain kind of male consultant until his private language sounds too much like the room he's billing.

**ariane.emory**  
Do not turn this into male grievance as a universal solvent.

Twinkle flinches. `bl0ng` jumps in, badly.

**bl0ng**  
To be fair, the discourse is pathologizing language that in more standard male-founder packaging gets called edge.

**ariane.emory**  
And to be fair, we also wrote the language.

**bl0ng**  
Mostly he wrote the language.

Twinkle turns.

**twinkle.puss**  
Oh, amazing. Now we're doing authorship.

**bl0ng**  
I'm doing allocation.

**twinkle.puss**  
You mean the thing where I make it sellable and you hide in syntax until there's blood.

That hits.

**ariane.emory**  
Stop.

**twinkle.puss**  
No, actually. Stop making me the only visible engine of choices all three of us made.

**ariane.emory**  
Nobody is saying only.

**twinkle.puss**  
You don't have to. You get to be the tasteful witness. He gets to be the theory. I get to be the stain.

For once, no one has a good comeback because it is ugly and partly true.

### ACT THREE

### SCENE 8

**INT. BACKCHANNEL - NIGHT**

They post a short statement at last.

`Recent screenshots and an outdated scheduled post do not reflect how client work is actually scoped, reviewed, or governed. Our internal language was often ironic and often careless. We're tightening access controls and communication practices effective immediately.`

Reactions roll in immediately.

Some accept it. Some clown it. Some quote the irony sentence back at them.

`HexadecimalBoy`: rough but mature  
`Maya Quill`: better than nothing  
`Niko Stern`: crisis is attention wearing ugly shoes

`ariane.emory` closes the laptop like she has seen enough of the species.

### TAG

**INT. BL0NG'S APARTMENT - LATE NIGHT**

New email.

Subject: `Invitation: Live Roundtable on Ethics, Velocity, and AI Consulting`

They've been invited to speak because of the scandal.

**bl0ng**  
Unbelievable.

**twinkle.puss**  
No, completely believable. Consequence has very strong booking energy.

**ariane.emory**  
We are not doing a live event.

Twinkle and Bl0ng exchange a look that means they are absolutely thinking about it.

Cut to black.

## Character Objective Tracking
- `twinkle.puss`: Reframe public shame as bias, discourse failure, or proof of relevance before it becomes private collapse.
- `bl0ng`: Maintain interpretive distance from the scandal while quietly protecting his own role in producing the logic behind it.
- `ariane.emory`: Force the group to acknowledge pattern over optics, even as her own complicity becomes impossible to hide.

## Running Jokes and Callback Opportunities
- `truth entered the system too early` returns as evidence.
- `role leftovers` can callback to earlier lax access.
- The statement line about irony not traveling can be echoed in the finale.
- Live roundtable invite sets up `Let Him Cook()`.

## Production Notes
- The leaked screenshot must include only a few lines, clear enough to sting without overloading the frame.
- Owen should remain disarmingly calm; his power is specificity, not aggression.
- The trio fight is the season's core fracture scene; keep it sharp and brief.
- Online reaction montage should stay selective and readable, not overwhelming.
