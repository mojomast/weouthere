## Season-Level Running Jokes

### 1. "Don't send that"
Function: Pilot phrase that mutates from practical warning into useless ritual.

- Episode 1: literal warning before the founding outreach message goes out.
- Episode 2: Ariane says it about a liability clause; Twinkle says it flirtatiously about a contract email.
- Episode 3: said before a live demo patch note; ignored.
- Episode 4: said before sending advisor intake answers; becomes foreplay-adjacent.
- Episode 5: nobody says it before the damaging scheduled post because the machine now sends for them.
- Episode 6: the phrase returns as a quiet human plea instead of a punch line.

### 2. Cataclysm is not a real company / Cataclysm is too real
Function: The trio toggle between fraud panic and over-identification.

- Repeated lines: `this is not a company`, `we are weirdly incorporated in spirit`, `stop making the bit taxable`, `I need the company to stop becoming a person in this room`.

### 3. Pseudo-legal language as emotional anesthesia
Function: They speak in disclaimers to avoid feeling.

- `for avoidance of doubt`
- `operationally speaking`
- `notwithstanding the vibe`
- `without admitting liability or need`
- `discoverable but not actionable`

### 4. The cursed deck naming convention
Function: Evidence of overwork, magical thinking, and version-control psychosis.

- `FINAL`
- `FINAL_v2`
- `FINAL_v2_USE_THIS`
- `FINAL_v2_USE_THIS_really`
- `FINAL_BOARD_SAFE`
- Finale callback: the deck folder is simply called `cook`

### 5. HexadecimalBoy's unsolicited competence
Function: A joke about porous labor boundaries that gets less funny over time.

- First appears as a power user doing free moderation.
- Later builds a useful bot nobody asked for.
- Eventually becomes part of the leak/exposure chain by having too much access and too little role clarity.

### 6. Ariane's paper trail language
Function: Her containment style is funny because it is tragically late.

- `I'm documenting this because apparently memory is now a hostile medium.`
- `This email exists so future me has a witness.`
- `I'm creating shape around the damage.`
- Episode 6 callback: `boundaries that arrive too late are just better metadata.`

### 7. Bl0ng's impossible caveats
Function: He turns plain danger into abstract comedy.

- `I'm not endorsing it, I'm describing its inevitability.`
- `The issue is not morality. The issue is sequencing.`
- `Truth entered the system too early.`
- `We're being punished for prototyping in public by people who do worse in private.`

### 8. Twinkle's glamour-vs-squalor bit
Function: He keeps pitching destiny from physically embarrassing spaces.

- Investor call from floor mattress.
- Sales message sent while eating shredded cheese from bag.
- Launch war room staged around pharmacy energy drinks and unpaid parking tickets.

## Emotional Callback Grid

### twinkle.puss
- Wants to be held without being slowed down.
- Repeated joke form: turns panic into branding, seduction, or destiny copy.
- Season callback: `Tell me this is real` shifts from triumphant to frightened.

### bl0ng
- Wants to matter beyond being useful.
- Repeated joke form: abstracts danger into elegant caveats and says intimate things like footnotes.
- Season callback: `answering as a human being` becomes harder each episode.

### ariane.emory
- Wants to stop being the container without everyone breaking.
- Repeated joke form: administrative calm that keeps forcing the room back to one plain sentence.
- Season callback: she keeps asking for one plain sentence from the others; the finale finally gives it to her briefly.

## Visual / Formal Callbacks
- Cursor hovering over `Send`.
- Notification sound used as both joke button and dread cue.
- Split-screen between private Backchannel panic and public-facing composure.
- Surreal inserts of legal/compliance language as bodily sensation: redlines crawling, notification badges multiplying, terms boxes breeding.
- Shared docs treated like sacred texts and crime scenes.

## Production Use Notes
- Keep runners light in any single episode; cumulative force matters more than density.
- A callback should either escalate meaning or curdle it.
- Do not let recurring jokes soften consequences. The joke should survive because the consequence survives.
- Scene outs should usually land on a send, a reply, a receipt, or a clean interruption.
