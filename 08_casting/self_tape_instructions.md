## Self-Tape Instructions
Please tape the provided side for your role and slate according to the instructions below.

## What We Want To See
We are primarily assessing acting craft: listening, specificity, tonal control, thought process, and camera truth. The material is smart and darkly funny, but please do not push for cleverness. Let the language work on you.

## Performance Guidance
- Keep it grounded and behaviorally specific
- Avoid broad comedy or wink-at-the-joke performance
- Do not overplay "tech" or "internet" flavor
- Let shifts in control, shame, urgency, and need register clearly
- Prioritize connection to the reader over presentation to camera

## Tape Setup
- Frame: medium close-up preferred
- Camera: phone or camera is fine if image and sound are clear
- Lighting: simple, even front light
- Background: plain and uncluttered
- Sound: quiet room, reader off-camera

## Slate
Please include:
- name
- role reading for
- location
- representation, if any

Keep the slate brief and natural.

## File Guidance
- Label file with actor name and role
- Submit in the requested format through the designated casting channel
- Include contact information and availability if requested

## Reader Guidance
Use an off-camera reader. Please do not have the reader perform heavily. We want to watch your behavior, not a two-person showcase.

## Optional Note For Actors
If you have a strong instinct about a beat, follow it. Simplicity is welcome. The show lives in contradiction, so it is often more effective to play the need underneath the wit than to emphasize the wit itself.

## Callback Expectation
Selected actors may be asked to:
- perform adjustments
- read an alternate side
- attend callback sessions
- participate in chemistry reads for principal roles

## Final Reminder
The process is performance-first. We are looking for actors who make this world feel inhabited, specific, and human.
