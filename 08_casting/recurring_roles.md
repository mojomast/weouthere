## Recurring Roles Overview
Recurring roles in `WE OUT HERE` should feel like fully lived-in adults from the adjacent ecosystems around AI, startups, operations, media, and digital labor. These parts are not wallpaper. Each recurring character sharpens the show's moral pressure and comic texture.

## Recurring Roles
### Jessa Vale
Recurring Guest

Late 30s to 40s. Founder/CEO type. Controlled, credible, exhausted, overcapitalized, and permanently one breath away from a PR event. Jessa hires `Cataclysm Consulting` because they seem unnervingly native to the collapse logic her company is already living through.

What to look for:
- Actor who can project executive competence and private depletion
- Dry tolerance for absurdity
- Ability to normalize red flags in a funny but truthful way

### Niko Stern
Recurring Guest

30s to 40s. Venture ecosystem floater. Advisor, connector, angel-adjacent personality, podcast frequent flyer. Polished, opportunistic, impossible to pin down, and always arriving with access that may or may not exist.

What to look for:
- Easy social confidence
- Performance that can sell charm and contamination simultaneously
- Fluent status behavior without cartoon villainy

### Maya Quill
Recurring Guest

30s to 40s. Compliance, policy, legal ops, or trust-and-safety professional. Calm, exact, and devastatingly clear. She understands exactly how improper `Cataclysm Consulting` is becoming and is one of the few people willing to say it plainly.

What to look for:
- Actor with intelligence, restraint, and specificity
- Can make procedural reality feel dangerous and funny
- Strong grounded listening opposite more volatile performers

### HexadecimalBoy
Recurring Guest

20s to early 30s. Hyper-online power user, part fan, part troll, part volunteer labor force. Intensely available, unnervingly competent in one narrow domain, and more embedded in the company's digital life than is healthy for anyone involved.

What to look for:
- Actor who can play eagerness, opacity, and awkward usefulness
- Real digital-native cadence without caricature
- Can be funny while remaining slightly destabilizing

### Dr. Liora Voss
Recurring Guest

40s to 60s. Therapist, coach, consultant, or performance psychologist who becomes entangled with the trio's need for insight without consequence. Composed, expensive, perceptive, and increasingly aware that these people are trying to turn treatment into strategy.

What to look for:
- Actor with stillness, authority, and dry wit
- Can hold the room opposite fast-talking leads
- Avoid any generic truth-dispensing mentor energy

### Owen Kaye
Recurring Guest

30s to 40s. Journalist, researcher, or podcaster covering AI and adjacent culture. Mild, observant, disarming, and more consequential than he first appears. He asks the most dangerous questions in the gentlest possible tone.

What to look for:
- Understated intelligence
- Ability to make curiosity feel quietly threatening
- Strong minimalism on camera

## Recurring Role Standard
These actors should be able to enter quickly, define a point of view immediately, and hold their own against highly verbal leads. Each recurring performer should feel cast with as much care as the series regulars.

## Rep Guidance
Submit actors with grounded specificity, not generic guest-star polish. The show rewards actors who can suggest history, profession, and private judgment without overplaying.
