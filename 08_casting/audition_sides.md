## Side Use Note
These are custom audition sides designed to test pace, subtext, contradiction, listening, and tonal control. Actors should not feel pressured to play "tech" as a superficial flavor. The material is about status, defense, and need under pressure.

## Side A: twinkle.puss
Reader role: `ariane.emory`

Scene setup:
An outreach message that was supposed to stay in draft has been sent to a potential client, and the client wants to meet. `ariane.emory` is alarmed. `twinkle.puss` is already converting the mistake into a destiny narrative.

Side:

`ariane.emory`: You sent it.

`twinkle.puss`: I released it.

`ariane.emory`: It was not a white paper. It was a half-feral pitch written at 1:13 a.m. with the phrase "precision collapse concierge" in the second paragraph.

`twinkle.puss`: Which, if we're being honest, is why he responded. Nobody in this market is horny for restraint.

`ariane.emory`: He responded because executives mistake confidence for due diligence every day of their lives.

`twinkle.puss`: Great. Then finally a market condition has met me where I live.

`ariane.emory`: We do not have a deck, a scope, a legal structure, or a stable definition of what we do.

`twinkle.puss`: We have language, instinct, and a calendar invite. That is literally how companies happen now.

`ariane.emory`: That is how inquiries happen.

`twinkle.puss`: No, that's how the timid narrate opportunity after they miss it.

`ariane.emory`: I need you to answer one question as a person and not as whatever this is.

`twinkle.puss`: Rude, but continue.

`ariane.emory`: Are you excited because this could work, or because if it works you never have to feel embarrassed about last week again?

`twinkle.puss`: ... those are not different enough experiences for me to separate cleanly on short notice.

## Side B: bl0ng
Reader role: `twinkle.puss`

Scene setup:
The group is trying to explain a shaky deliverable to a client. `bl0ng` wants to frame the problem as sophistication. `twinkle.puss` wants a usable sentence now.

Side:

`twinkle.puss`: I need a version that sounds reassuring before sounding correct.

`bl0ng`: That's actually the whole industry, so congratulations on your instincts.

`twinkle.puss`: `bl0ng`.

`bl0ng`: Fine. The short version is that the system behaved inconsistently because the client defined success in mutually hostile terms.

`twinkle.puss`: Better. Less dissertation. More "this was always part of the process."

`bl0ng`: But it wasn't.

`twinkle.puss`: Nothing is, until someone invoices for it retroactively.

`bl0ng`: You say things like that and then I have to decide whether we're joking or forming policy.

`twinkle.puss`: That ambiguity is where you do your best work.

`bl0ng`: The actual issue is that they wanted automation with taste, scale with intimacy, and novelty with zero legal exposure. That's not a brief. That's a prayer written by procurement.

`twinkle.puss`: Great. That's good. Cold. Mean. Keep that emotional weather, remove the contempt, and make it client-safe.

`bl0ng`: So you want me to lie in an empathetic register.

`twinkle.puss`: I want you to translate. If you need a moral distinction, write one in the footer.

## Side C: ariane.emory
Reader role: `bl0ng`

Scene setup:
The group has improvised too many promises to a client. `ariane.emory` is trying to stop the slide before it becomes irreversible. `bl0ng` keeps describing the situation instead of interrupting it.

Side:

`bl0ng`: In fairness, no one explicitly promised deployment this week.

`ariane.emory`: We implied deployment, celebrated deployment-adjacent readiness, and attached a document called "deployment confidence map." That's promise with a trench coat on.

`bl0ng`: I just think precision matters if we're assigning blame.

`ariane.emory`: That's beautiful. I love that for you. I am currently less interested in blame than in preventing a future email that begins with "Just flagging."

`bl0ng`: You're assuming the worst-case response pattern.

`ariane.emory`: I'm assuming the ordinary response pattern because one of us has to live on Earth.

`bl0ng`: I do live on Earth.

`ariane.emory`: No. You visit Earth academically. You treat consequence like a case study with posture problems.

`bl0ng`: That's needlessly personal.

`ariane.emory`: Need is, in fact, personal. That's the whole issue here. `twinkle.puss` needs to feel momentum, you need to feel exempt, and I need to stop converting both of those needs into PDFs.

`bl0ng`: That's harsh.

`ariane.emory`: It's late. Harsh is what clarity sounds like when we miss the softer window.

## Optional Adjustment Prompts
- Play the scene with less irony and more urgency.
- Play the scene as if the joke is covering real shame.
- Play the scene more simply; trust the language.
- Let the other person's line affect you before you respond.
