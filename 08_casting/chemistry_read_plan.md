## Chemistry Read Goal
The purpose of chemistry reads is not simply to confirm that the actors are likable together. The goal is to determine whether the trio can generate the specific relationship engine of `WE OUT HERE`: appetite, defense, containment, collusion, resentment, humor, and real attachment.

## What Must Be Tested
- Can the three actors feel like long-standing co-dependents rather than newly assembled scene partners?
- Is each lane distinct without becoming rigid?
- Does the scene stay funny while emotional risk increases?
- Do the actors actually shift one another's behavior on camera?
- Can affection and damage coexist in the same exchange?

## Chemistry Read Structure
1. Pair reads: `twinkle.puss` with `ariane.emory`
2. Pair reads: `twinkle.puss` with `bl0ng`
3. Pair reads: `bl0ng` with `ariane.emory`
4. Full trio read
5. Adjustment pass on the strongest trio combinations

## Pairing Focus
### twinkle.puss + ariane.emory
Test charge, dependence, irritation, and the sense that care can flip into governance.

### twinkle.puss + bl0ng
Test wit, acceleration, rationalization, and whether the pair becomes intoxicating in a dangerous way.

### bl0ng + ariane.emory
Test plain-speech pressure, mutual intelligence, and whether conflict feels buried, old, and specific.

## Full Trio Focus
The trio should feel like a broken regulatory system:
- `twinkle.puss` escalates
- `bl0ng` reframes
- `ariane.emory` contains

Then that pattern should be able to destabilize. If the actors can only play the fixed version of the dynamic, the ensemble will flatten over a season.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Distinct rhythms that still feel compatible
- Strong listening under overlap and interruption
- Ability to hold subtext in fast dialogue
- Behavioral realism in moments of pressure
- Genuine pleasure in one another's company, even when the scene is corrosive
- No single actor overwhelming the structure by playing at a different scale or style

## Session Notes For Producers And Casting
- Watch reaction shots as closely as line readings
- Track whether tension rises naturally or is pushed externally
- Note which combinations make the world feel most lived-in
- Prefer specificity over spark if forced to choose; real chemistry in this show is built on history and damage, not generic charm

## Decision Rule
No lead should be finalized without testing against the other two likely leads. The show's central asset is the trio. Chemistry is not a bonus; it is the engine.
