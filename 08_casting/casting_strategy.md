## Casting Mandate
`WE OUT HERE` should be cast with a performance-first philosophy. The show needs actors who can carry smart, darkly funny, internet-native dialogue without flattening it into parody, stunt comedy, or generic startup satire.

This is an open casting process. Name value may help later in packaging conversations, but the core mandate is to find the best performers for the material, including discoveries, emerging talent, digitally native performers with real craft, and theater-trained actors with sharp comic control.

## Core Principles
1. Cast for psychological specificity, not type shorthand.
2. Prioritize actors who can play intelligence, instability, and need at the same time.
3. Avoid overly polished "cool-tech" casting that drains the humanity out of the roles.
4. Avoid broad improv energy that turns the show's dark comedy into sketch behavior.
5. Look for performers who can handle speed, tonal pivots, and subtext under pressure.
6. Treat internet fluency as useful but not sufficient. The deciding factor is always acting.
7. Protect the show's humane core. These characters are funny because they are exact, not because they are freakish.

## What Casting Should Optimize For
- Distinct vocal and behavioral signatures across the three leads
- Strong command of dense language and fragmented thought
- Credible contemporary professional presence without corporate blandness
- Capacity for deadpan, panic, seduction, and shame in the same scene
- Chemistry that feels like a friendship system, not a generic ensemble
- Actors who can play text, silence, and reaction equally well in desktop-realism scenes

## Lead Casting Priorities
### twinkle.puss
Prioritize magnetism, volatility, and persuasive force. The actor must be capable of making a reckless idea feel temporarily undeniable. The performance has to imply wit, hunger, social dominance, and real fragility without asking the audience for easy forgiveness.

### bl0ng
Prioritize dry authority, buried panic, and language control. The actor must make complex rationalization funny and dangerous. The role fails if it becomes "funny nerd guy" instead of a person using intelligence as a defensive weapon.

### ariane.emory
Prioritize stillness, precision, and deep internal life. The actor must be able to land the sharpest reality-testing in the room while also revealing complicity, desire, and exhaustion. The role should never read as merely managerial.

## Supporting Casting Priorities
Recurring and day-player roles should feel specific to the ecosystem: founders, operators, advisors, clients, journalists, legal-adjacent professionals, and hyper-online orbiters. Cast actors who can suggest a whole working life quickly. Even small roles should feel like they exist beyond the scene.

## Open Casting Process
1. Invite submissions from agencies, management companies, and open-access channels.
2. Actively review emerging talent alongside established lists.
3. Keep first-round materials identical in evaluation standard regardless of representation status.
4. Use self-tapes to assess text handling, tonal intelligence, and camera intimacy.
5. Use callbacks to test adjustment range, listening, and behavioral specificity.
6. Use chemistry reads to evaluate whether the trio generates a believable dependency system.

## Evaluation Lens
Casting, producers, and creative should use the same questions in every round:
- Does this actor make the language feel lived, not written?
- Can they play contradiction without announcing it?
- Can they be darkly funny while staying emotionally credible?
- Do they feel contemporary without feeling trend-cast?
- Do they reveal need underneath fluency?
- Would an audience believe this person could both start and survive inside `Cataclysm Consulting`?

## Red Flags
- Performance feels like commentary on the character instead of embodiment
- Actor plays "online" as a layer of bits rather than a behavioral reality
- Broad comic mugging or excessive riffing
- Generic prestige naturalism with no comic edge
- Charm without danger for `twinkle.puss`
- Intellect without vulnerability for `bl0ng`
- competence without damage for `ariane.emory`

## Outreach Guidance For Reps
When circulating breakdowns, emphasize:
- 6 x 30 minute single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy
- smart, darkly funny, psychologically sharp, humane tone
- open casting and performance-first process
- strong roles for actors who excel at language, subtext, and tonal shifts
- no requirement to be a tech insider; the work is about human behavior under pressure

## Packaging Position
If notable talent becomes available, evaluate fit against the material before value-add. A strong package is useful only if the central trio remains behaviorally credible and compositionally balanced. One miscast lead will distort the entire show.

## Decision Standard
Final decisions should favor the actor who unlocks the show's exact tone on camera, not the actor who feels most familiar on paper. If there is a choice between recognizability and precision, precision should win.
