## Day Player Tone Note
Even the smallest role in `WE OUT HERE` should feel behaviorally sharp and contemporary. Day players are often the people who make the world believable: assistants, founders, clients, moderators, journalists, legal reviewers, junior operators, and contractors who have to react to the trio's warped normal.

## Day Player Buckets
### Founder / Client Types
Early 30s to 50s. Smart, tired, trying to sound decisive while quietly overwhelmed. They buy services partly because they are under pressure to appear proactive.

Use for:
- startup founders
- heads of product
- marketing leads
- innovation executives

Performance ask:
Actors who can play status, anxiety, and self-protective fluency.

### Operator / Assistant / Coordinator Types
20s to 40s. Hyper-competent, under-credited, moving too fast, and often the first to spot that something is off.

Use for:
- executive assistants
- project coordinators
- operations managers
- agency producers

Performance ask:
Actors with speed, precision, and believable workplace muscle memory.

### Legal / Policy / Compliance Types
30s to 60s. Calm, observant, exact, and not easily charmed by language games.

Use for:
- in-house counsel
- outside counsel
- policy reviewers
- trust-and-safety staff

Performance ask:
Actors who can make ordinary professional questions feel like an existential threat to the leads.

### Media / Research / Podcast Ecosystem Types
20s to 50s. Curious, informed, socially nimble, and lightly dangerous because they document for a living.

Use for:
- reporters
- podcasters
- event moderators
- researchers

Performance ask:
Actors who can weaponize mildness and observation.

### Hyper-Online Community Types
20s to 30s. Extremely present in chat culture, quick with language, vibes, and receipts. Funny, invested, and potentially volatile.

Use for:
- community mods
- superfans
- volunteer contributors
- digital orbiters

Performance ask:
Actors with real comfort in contemporary online cadence but grounded enough to stay human.

### Advisor / Consultant / Investor Types
30s to 60s. Smooth, articulate, self-protective, and practiced at implying value before proving it.

Use for:
- angel investors
- startup advisors
- consultants
- board-adjacent operators

Performance ask:
Actors who can play polished ambiguity.

## General Day Player Guidance
- Avoid broad comic stunt casting
- Favor actors who can be specific in one or two scenes
- Every role should feel cast from the same universe as the leads
- Reactions matter; many day-player scenes work because outsiders register the trio accurately

## Quick Casting Filter
Ask of every submission:
- Can this actor suggest a real job, not just a role label?
- Do they feel current without leaning on trend performance?
- Can they handle precise language and awkward rhythm?
- Will they help the leads look more truthful, not less?
