## Strategy Summary

`WE OUT HERE` should feel socially expansive but operationally contained. The audience should infer a wider city and tech world than the production actually has to cover.

The location plan therefore prioritizes:

- `2-3` recurring controllable standing environments
- `4-6` practical location addresses maximum for the full season
- one metro base with short drive times
- interiors over exteriors
- places that can play multiple story functions through art direction, lensing, and schedule design

## Creative Location Logic

The show's world is near-present, over-articulated, and economically strained. Locations should feel:

- plausible for precarious professionals
- recognizable without needing iconic city landmarks
- slightly overused, overdesigned, or improvised
- capable of holding both comedy and low-level dread

Avoid:

- glossy startup campuses
- expensive skyline-dependent exteriors
- locations that only work if a specific city is visually identified
- noisy hospitality spaces with weak dialogue control

## Recommended Location Package

### Standing Environment Package

| Priority | Space | Story Function | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A | Cataclysm base | primary company environment | build or lease controllable interior with multiple playable zones |
| B | personal / domestic spill space | private fallout, calls, late-night work | redressable recurring environment |
| C | threshold / corridor / support zone | transitions, arguments, arrivals, exits | low-cost utility space with high schedule value |

### Practical Package

| Priority | Type | Story Function | Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | client office / conference space | legitimacy theater, pressure meetings | choose one flexible address that can cover multiple clients |
| 2 | cafe / casual public meet spot | pitch conversations, accidental exposure | select location willing to accommodate repeated short blocks |
| 3 | institutional or advisory office | containment fantasy, pseudo-governance | ideally in same building family as another practical |
| 4 | exterior threshold cluster | arrivals, departures, smoking/phone scenes | capture near standing base to reduce travel |
| 5 | utility / parking / service area | consequence and depleted-energy scenes | cheap, controllable, visually useful |

## Base City Selection Criteria

The city should be chosen using production value and operating simplicity, not prestige instinct.

### Selection Matrix

| Criterion | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| tax incentive usability | can materially improve schedule and cash flow viability |
| crew depth for indie series | reduces overtime and weak staffing compromises |
| availability of controllable warehouse / loft interiors | strongest fit for standing environments |
| permit simplicity | supports quick contained production |
| average drive radius | protects day length and company morale |
| sound conditions | dialogue-heavy show cannot fight constant environmental noise |

### Recommendation

- prioritize a single metro where interior control is affordable and local crew bench is real
- do not lock creative identity to a city unless financing or distribution value clearly justifies it
- allow production design and signage to imply a generic contemporary U.S. city rather than a tourism postcard version of one

## Location Count Targets

- ideal total distinct addresses: `6`
- acceptable maximum: `8`
- target recurring controllable spaces covering season page count: `60%+`
- target exterior count: `minimal and motivated`

## Scouting Priorities

### Round 1

- standing environment candidates
- client office candidate that can redress across episodes
- threshold cluster near probable production base

### Round 2

- sound and parking validation
- permit and neighborhood restrictions
- art department access, holding, green room, and equipment staging

### Round 3

- final tech scout with AD, DP, PD, sound, locations, and post-relevant screen considerations

## Location Approval Checklist

A location is not considered viable until production confirms:

- dialogue control is acceptable
- staging, holding, and restrooms are workable
- crew parking/load-in is real, not theoretical
- power and internet needs are understood
- screen glare and practical lighting risks are understood
- owner approvals cover art dressing and on-screen graphics as needed
- permit/insurance requirements match budget reality

## Multipurpose Location Design

Whenever possible, choose spaces that can cover more than one story use:

- one office that can play client office, advisor office, and meeting suite
- one exterior block that can cover multiple arrivals/departures
- one cafe or public venue that can cheat different times/days through angle and dressing

## Location Risk Management

### Mandatory Backups

- backup for every practical location category
- cover interior for every exterior block
- alternate staging plan if a location lacks proper holding

### Locations To Avoid Unless Essential

- active restaurants during business hours
- uncontrolled open-plan coworking spaces
- transit hubs
- rooftops requiring heavy safety and permit layers
- dense street exteriors with impossible sound cleanup burden

## Department Notes

### AD / LP

- protect the schedule from over-romantic location choices
- cluster scouts by geography
- measure every practical against what a standing set could do cheaper

### Art Department

- prioritize dressing rights and reset access
- ask early what can be removed, covered, or replaced

### Camera / Sound

- pre-check reflections, monitor sightlines, HVAC, and traffic bleed

### Post

- flag any location that creates unavoidable VFX cleanup or monitor replacement work
