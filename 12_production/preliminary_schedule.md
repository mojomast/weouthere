## Scheduling Assumptions

- target production start remains `Q3 2026`
- prep should not begin until scripts are far enough along to support a real stripboard pass
- post begins during principal photography, not after wrap
- desktop realism and graphics prep must start before camera tests
- hold days are preserved until late in the process and not casually spent in prep

## Proposed Macro Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Key Outcome |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Pre-prep / greenlight setup | 2 weeks | key hires, budget lock lane, legal and insurance readiness |
| Prep | 8 weeks | scripts, board, scouts, builds, tests, hires, workflow lock |
| Principal photography | 6 weeks | `30` shoot days base |
| Buffer / contingency | 2 days | weather, location loss, recovery |

## Principal Photography Framework

### Week 1

- target: controlled standing-set material only or almost only
- goal: bank pages, stabilize crew rhythm, validate data and sound workflow
- post: editor begins daily assemblies immediately

### Week 2

- target: continue standing-set coverage plus one practical cluster
- goal: avoid early overruns by keeping move count low
- post: first editorial concerns returned to set leadership

### Week 3

- target: practical locations with strongest story value
- goal: use established crew rhythm before entering riskier days
- post: first cut fragments available for workflow diagnosis

### Week 4

- target: dedicated desktop realism block and live-action scenes requiring synced screens
- goal: finish most screen-critical material before late-stage fatigue
- post: graphics and editorial sync check

### Week 5

- target: remaining practicals, domestic fallout scenes, insert work
- goal: close continuity-heavy loops while principal sets still standing
- post: start VFX plate logging and turnover prep

### Week 6

- target: finale work, surreal inserts, pickups, contingency use if needed
- goal: finish story-critical material first, cosmetic pickups second
- post: wrap turnover packet assembled by end of week

## Schedule Controls

### Hard Controls

- no planned sixth day without producer approval
- no more than two consecutive high-complexity days
- no night exterior inserted without updated transport, sound, and recovery plan
- no same-week addition of major graphics-heavy scenes without post sign-off

### Soft Controls

- use desktop capture as schedule pressure valve when weather or company moves fail
- keep low-cast pickup material ready for disruption days
- maintain one standby interior each week that can be shot with minimal reset

## Delivery Milestones Tied To Schedule

| Milestone | Timing |
| --- | --- |
| Editor receives day 1 media | same day as first shoot |
| First workflow review | end of shoot week 1 |
| First multi-episode editorial health check | end of shoot week 3 |
| VFX/screens turnover format locked | by end of shoot week 4 |
| Sound metadata audit | by end of shoot week 5 |
| Picture assemblies complete | within 3 business days of wrap for all shot material |

## Primary Risks To Calendar

- scripts not stabilized by Prep 3
- production design and graphics moving on separate timelines
- late cast holds forcing board rebuild
- location approvals landing after Prep 6
- unclear ownership of screen content approvals
- editorial falling behind because metadata is inconsistent

## Schedule Recovery Ladder

1. Reassign scenes to standing environments where credible.
2. Collapse non-essential practical locations.
3. Move isolated desktop realism work to dedicated capture lane.
4. Convert visual-only moments to insert or pickup package.
5. Spend hold days.
6. Add a paid sixth day only as last resort.
