## Wardrobe Objective

Wardrobe should express a specific internet-literate professional class under stress: aspirational, self-aware, occasionally performative, and often one step removed from actual stability.

The costume world should feel credible enough for close observation and stylized enough to help track character psychology across the season.

## Character Wardrobe Principles

### twinkle.puss

- high-voltage silhouette choices with moments of impulsive polish
- wardrobe can read as charisma-first, sustainability-second
- pieces may suggest self-branding, reinvention, and financial overextension

### bl0ng

- functional layers, pseudo-practical textures, controlled palette bias
- should suggest a person trying to manage entropy through systems and repeats
- wardrobe should support fast reset and believable underthinking of self-presentation

### ariane.emory

- deliberate curation, strong line, psychologically defended elegance
- pieces can feel more composed than the underlying circumstances
- wardrobe should support a person who knows what visual meaning does and uses it knowingly

## Season Arc Through Wardrobe

| Phase | Wardrobe Shift |
| --- | --- |
| Mania | intentionality, self-mythology, launch energy |
| Fragile success | sharper presentation, aspirational professionalism |
| Ethical drift | repeated looks, corners cut, stress visible in details |
| Containment failure | increasing mismatch between intended image and actual state |
| Ironic collapse | fatigue, compromise, maintenance failures |
| Exposure to consequences | attempts to restore control without fully regaining it |

## Practical Rules

- build wardrobes around repeatability and multiples
- avoid costumes that require constant emergency steaming or impossible resets
- coordinate with sound on fabrics, closures, jewelry, and layers
- log all hero looks by episode and emotional state, not just scene number

## Multiples And Continuity

Required planning:

- hero duplicate plan for any look used across multiple board days
- stain / distress notes where applicable
- shoe continuity tracked separately from main look photos
- bag, laptop sleeve, glasses, jewelry, and outerwear tracked as part of the costume package when story-significant

## Fittings Plan

- lead cast initial fittings before Prep 6
- camera test garments available for reflection, texture, and lav evaluation
- backup options approved at fittings for each lead's high-use category

## Department Coordination

### Sound

- test lav compatibility before locking key looks

### Camera

- avoid patterns or surfaces that produce avoidable moire or monitor reflection issues

### Art / Props

- coordinate bags, devices, lanyards, and visible workplace accessories

### AD / Script Supervision

- continuity breakdown should track look changes across cross-boarded scenes

## Shopping Strategy

- prioritize a mix of bought, rented, and pulled contemporary pieces
- concentrate spend on hero recurring items rather than overbuilding one-off extras
- build a flexible background wardrobe package suitable for low-count office/public environments

## Wardrobe Risks

- overly fashionable choices that break plausibility for the characters' means
- too few multiples for block shooting
- fabric noise or mic concealment issues discovered too late
- wardrobe continuity tracked only by episode instead of by schedule day and story time
