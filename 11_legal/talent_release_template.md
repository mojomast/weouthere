# 11_legal/talent_release_template.md

## Purpose

Provide a practical performer and likeness release template for WE OUT HERE so the production has clear rights to use performances, names, images, voices, and related materials in the project and its promotion. This is a starting form for attorney adaptation, not final legal advice.

## Owner/Subagent
legal_agent

## Status
Draft v1 - internal template. Not approved for signature as-is. Counsel should adapt this form for compensation, union issues, minors, nudity or sensitive-content provisions, publicity rights, and local labor-law requirements.

## Dependencies

- Correct legal names of company and performer
- Description of the role and services
- Compensation terms
- Shoot dates and location details
- Any sensitive-content rider needs
- Attorney review before use

## Plain-Language Notes

- A release should cover both the performance itself and the use of the performer's name, likeness, voice, and approved biographical material for promotion.
- If the performer is a principal cast member, a fuller performer agreement may be better than a simple release.
- If a minor is involved, do not use this without parent or guardian and counsel review.

## Attorney Review Flags

- Whether the performer should sign a short release or a full employment or performer agreement.
- Whether SAG-AFTRA or other union rules apply.
- Whether local law requires additional rights-of-publicity, labor, meal-break, overtime, or permit language.
- Whether the remedy and waiver language should be modified.

## Template

**TALENT RELEASE AND PERFORMANCE AGREEMENT**

This Talent Release and Performance Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of `[DATE]` by and between `[COMPANY LEGAL NAME]`, with an address at `[ADDRESS]` ("Company"), and `[PERFORMER LEGAL NAME]`, with an address at `[ADDRESS]` ("Performer").

### 1. Project And Services

Performer agrees to appear in connection with the original scripted sitcom project currently titled **WE OUT HERE** (the "Project") in the role or capacity of `[ROLE / CHARACTER / INTERVIEW / EXTRA / VOICE]` and to provide related services reasonably associated with that appearance.

### 2. Dates And Location

Performer is scheduled to render services on or about `[DATES]` at `[LOCATION(S)]`, subject to production needs and reasonable scheduling changes.

### 3. Compensation

As full consideration for the rights granted and services rendered, Company will pay Performer `[AMOUNT / RATE / DEFERRED COMPENSATION TERMS / OTHER]`. Unless expressly stated otherwise, no additional compensation, residuals, royalties, or profit participation are owed.

### 4. Grant Of Rights

Performer irrevocably grants to Company and its parents, subsidiaries, successors, licensees, distributors, exhibitors, assigns, and designees the perpetual, transferable, sublicensable right throughout the universe to photograph, record, film, edit, dub, reproduce, distribute, transmit, exhibit, advertise, publicize, exploit, and otherwise use Performer's name, approved professional name, image, likeness, voice, performance, biographical material supplied by or approved by Performer, and all recordings and results of the services in and in connection with the Project and the advertising, promotion, publicity, clipping, excerpting, and other exploitation of the Project, in any media now known or later devised.

### 5. Editing And Fictionalization

Company may edit, adapt, crop, dub, subtitle, rearrange, composite, fictionalize, and otherwise modify the recordings and performances in Company's discretion, provided such use is not unlawful.

### 6. Ownership

All recordings, footage, photographs, and other results and proceeds of Performer's services for the Project shall be owned exclusively by Company. To the extent Performer's services create any copyrightable contribution or other rights not automatically owned by Company, Performer hereby assigns all such rights to Company.

### 7. No Approval Rights

Performer acknowledges that Company has no obligation to use Performer's performance and that Performer has no right of approval over the Project, the final cut, advertising, publicity, or related materials.

### 8. Representations

Performer represents and warrants that:

1. Performer has the full right to enter into this Agreement;
2. Performer's performance will not knowingly violate any third-party rights or obligations;
3. Performer is not relying on any promise of specific prominence, screen time, or distribution;
4. any wardrobe, props, or materials supplied by Performer will be lawful for production use and either owned by Performer or supplied with permission.

### 9. Confidentiality

Performer will keep non-public information about the Project confidential, including scripts, plot points, cast information, unreleased footage, and production details, and will not post or disclose such information without Company approval.

### 10. Publicity And Social Media

Performer will not announce participation, share behind-the-scenes materials, or post images, clips, pages, call sheets, or confidential production information without Company's prior written approval.

### 11. Release

To the fullest extent permitted by law, Performer releases Company and its successors, licensees, distributors, exhibitors, and assigns from claims arising out of the authorized use of the rights granted under this Agreement, including claims for invasion of privacy, right of publicity, or false light, except to the extent arising from unlawful use or material breach of this Agreement.

### 12. Remedies

Performer agrees that Performer's remedies for any claim arising out of this Agreement or the Project shall be limited to money damages, if any, and Performer shall not seek to enjoin, restrain, or interfere with the development, production, distribution, advertising, or exploitation of the Project, to the extent permitted by law.

## Practical Completion Checklist

- Confirm whether a short release is enough for the role.
- Add any approved credit terms separately if needed.
- Confirm performer status for payroll or contractor treatment.
- Use a minor-specific review path if anyone under 18 is involved.
- Store the signed release with call sheets and payment records.

## Working Legal Position

This template is a drafting starting point only and not final jurisdiction-specific legal advice. Performer rights, labor rules, and enforceability vary by facts, territory, and union status.
