# 11_legal/NDA_template.md

## Purpose

Provide a practical mutual NDA template for development conversations around WE OUT HERE when the team needs to share non-public creative, business, financing, or production information. This is a starting form for attorney adaptation, not final legal advice.

## Owner/Subagent
legal_agent

## Status
Draft v1 - template for internal use. Not approved for signature as-is. Counsel should adapt the template for the parties, jurisdiction, term, exclusions, remedies, and any investor, contractor, or production-specific needs.

## Dependencies

- Correct legal names and addresses of the parties
- Definition of what will actually be disclosed
- Entity name from formation documents
- Any carve-outs required by investors, producers, or talent reps
- Attorney review before use

## When To Use

Useful for:

- early producer or financier conversations
- sharing decks, pitch materials, and budgets with limited circulation
- discussions with contractors before formal engagement

Usually not useful for:

- standard submissions to parties who refuse NDAs
- situations where the relationship is already governed by a broader services agreement with confidentiality terms
- circumstances where the real issue is ownership, not secrecy

## Plain-Language Notes

- An NDA helps with confidential business information; it is not a substitute for a writer or contractor agreement.
- Many buyers, reps, and production companies will not sign unsolicited-submission NDAs.
- If the team is sharing scripts, the stronger protection is usually clean authorship and chain of title, plus a targeted NDA where appropriate.

## Attorney Review Flags

- Whether the NDA should be mutual or one-way.
- Whether injunctive relief language is appropriate and enforceable.
- Whether any state-law, venue, or arbitration language should be added or revised.
- Whether trade secret treatment, residuals clauses, or compelled-disclosure wording should be customized.

## Template

**MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT**

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of `[DATE]` by and between:

`[DISCLOSING / RECEIVING PARTY 1 LEGAL NAME]`, with an address at `[ADDRESS]` ("Party 1"), and

`[DISCLOSING / RECEIVING PARTY 2 LEGAL NAME]`, with an address at `[ADDRESS]` ("Party 2").

Party 1 and Party 2 may each be referred to as a "Party" and together as the "Parties."

### 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to evaluate a possible business relationship concerning the original scripted sitcom project currently titled **WE OUT HERE**, including potential development, production, financing, distribution, marketing, advisory, or service-provider discussions (the "Purpose").

### 2. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party, whether in written, oral, visual, electronic, or other form, that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

Confidential Information may include:

- scripts, outlines, bibles, decks, pitch materials, and creative notes
- budgets, schedules, financing plans, and investor information
- business strategies, release plans, and marketing concepts
- contact lists, vendor terms, and non-public deal terms
- unpublished artwork, designs, mockups, and fictional platform concepts

Confidential Information does not include information that the receiving Party can show:

1. was already lawfully known without restriction before disclosure;
2. becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;
3. is lawfully received from a third party without breach of any duty; or
4. is independently developed without use of or reference to the disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

### 3. Use Restriction

The receiving Party will use Confidential Information only for the Purpose and not for any other purpose without prior written consent of the disclosing Party.

### 4. Non-Disclosure

The receiving Party will not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except to its employees, contractors, professional advisors, financing sources, or representatives who:

- have a need to know for the Purpose; and
- are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement.

The receiving Party is responsible for breaches of this Agreement by persons to whom it discloses Confidential Information under this section.

### 5. Standard Of Care

The receiving Party will protect the Confidential Information using at least reasonable care and no less than the care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar importance.

### 6. Compelled Disclosure

If the receiving Party is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information, it will, to the extent legally permitted, promptly notify the disclosing Party so the disclosing Party may seek protective treatment. The receiving Party may disclose only the portion legally required.

### 7. No License Or Ownership Transfer

Except for the limited right to use Confidential Information for the Purpose, no license or other rights are granted by this Agreement, whether by implication, estoppel, or otherwise. All Confidential Information remains the property of the disclosing Party or its licensors.

### 8. Return Or Destruction

Upon written request, the receiving Party will promptly return or destroy the disclosing Party's Confidential Information, except that the receiving Party may retain one archival copy for legal or compliance purposes and any automatically created backup copies maintained in the ordinary course of business.

### 9. No Obligation To Proceed

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to enter into any transaction or relationship. Any such relationship must be set out in a separate written agreement signed by the Parties.

### 10. Term

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for `[12 / 24 / 36]` months unless earlier terminated by either Party on written notice. The confidentiality and use restrictions for Confidential Information disclosed during the term will continue for `[2 / 3 / 5]` years after disclosure, or longer for trade secrets to the extent permitted by law.

### 11. Equitable Relief

The Parties acknowledge that unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be inadequate. Subject to applicable law, the disclosing Party may seek equitable relief in addition to other available remedies.

### 12. Disclaimers

All Confidential Information is provided "as is." Neither Party makes any representation or warranty as to the accuracy or completeness of the Confidential Information except as may be stated in a later definitive agreement.

### 14. Miscellaneous

This Agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties on the subject matter hereof and supersedes prior discussions on that subject. Any amendment must be in writing and signed by both Parties. Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's written consent, except to a successor in connection with a merger, sale, or transfer of substantially all relevant assets.
