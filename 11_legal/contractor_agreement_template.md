# 11_legal/contractor_agreement_template.md

## Purpose

Provide a practical independent contractor agreement template for WE OUT HERE covering creative and production vendors whose work should be owned or controlled by the project entity. This is a starting form for attorney adaptation, not final legal advice.

## Owner/Subagent
legal_agent

## Status
Draft v1 - internal template. Not approved for signature as-is. Counsel should adapt this form for local labor rules, classification risk, insurance, indemnities, payment rules, and any department-specific needs.

## Dependencies

- Correct company legal name and contractor legal name
- Detailed scope of work and deliverables
- Payment schedule and expense rules
- Insurance requirements if applicable
- Any equipment, access, or confidentiality requirements
- Attorney review before use

## Plain-Language Notes

- Use this for designers, editors, DPs, animators, social vendors, post vendors, and similar non-employee contributors.
- The contract should say both what they are doing and who owns the output.
- If someone is really functioning like an employee, this template may be the wrong tool.
- If the contractor is creating visible in-show assets, include specific originality and clearance expectations.

## Attorney Review Flags

- Whether the worker is properly classified as an independent contractor.
- Whether any local wage, payroll, or workers' compensation rules change the deal.
- Whether insurance or union issues require a different form.
- Whether the IP clause needs special handling for software, music, or other specialized deliverables.

## Template

**INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT**

This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of `[DATE]` by and between `[COMPANY LEGAL NAME]`, with an address at `[ADDRESS]` ("Company"), and `[CONTRACTOR LEGAL NAME]`, with an address at `[ADDRESS]` ("Contractor").

### 1. Project

The services relate to the original scripted sitcom project currently titled **WE OUT HERE** (the "Project").

### 2. Services

Contractor will provide the following services:

- `[SERVICES DESCRIPTION]`
- Deliverables: `[FILES / CUTS / DESIGNS / ASSETS / REPORTS / OTHER]`
- Schedule: `[DATES OR MILESTONES]`

Contractor will perform the services in a professional manner consistent with industry standards.

### 3. Compensation

Company will pay Contractor as follows:

- Fee or rate: `[AMOUNT / RATE]`
- Payment timing: `[MILESTONES / NET TERMS / OTHER]`
- Approved expenses: `[RULES OR NONE]`

Unless expressly stated otherwise, Contractor is not entitled to residuals, royalties, profit participation, overtime premiums, employee benefits, or additional compensation.

### 4. Independent Contractor Status

Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Company. Contractor controls the manner and means of performing the services, subject to the required deliverables, schedule, and Company specifications. Contractor is responsible for all taxes, withholdings, insurance, and permits arising from amounts paid under this Agreement.

### 5. Ownership Of Deliverables

To the maximum extent permitted by law, all results and proceeds of Contractor's services for the Project, including all designs, edits, footage, graphics, layouts, documents, code, files, and other deliverables created, developed, or reduced to practice by Contractor for the Project (collectively, the "Deliverables") are specially ordered or commissioned by Company as works made for hire for Company.

To the extent any Deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire, Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to Company, exclusively and throughout the universe in perpetuity, all right, title, and interest in and to the Deliverables and all associated intellectual property rights.

Contractor will deliver editable source files and project files where applicable unless otherwise agreed in writing.

### 6. Contractor Materials

If Contractor intends to incorporate any pre-existing materials, templates, stock elements, plugins, fonts, code libraries, or other third-party or Contractor-owned materials into the Deliverables ("Contractor Materials"), Contractor must identify them in writing in advance. No Contractor Materials may be embedded in final Deliverables unless Company approves them in writing and the rights are sufficient for the Project's intended use.

### 7. Originality And Clearance

Contractor represents and warrants that:

1. except for approved Contractor Materials, the Deliverables will be original;
2. Contractor has full authority to enter into this Agreement;
3. the Deliverables will not knowingly infringe any third-party rights;
4. Contractor will not include unlicensed music, fonts, footage, stock assets, software, artwork, or branded elements without prior written approval; and
5. Contractor will comply with Company's clearance and confidentiality requirements.

Special Project note:

- If Contractor creates fictional platform screens, chat graphics, app-style UI, or promo assets for WE OUT HERE, Contractor must avoid literal Discord branding and avoid copying identifiable trade dress too closely.

### 8. Confidentiality

Contractor will keep all non-public information relating to the Project confidential, including scripts, cuts, budgets, schedules, cast information, financing plans, platform strategy, and unreleased assets, and will not use such information except to perform the services.

### 9. Credit

Contractor shall receive credit only if expressly stated here: `[CREDIT OR NONE]`. Failure to accord any such credit shall not be deemed a material breach if Company uses reasonable efforts to cure prospectively where practical. Contractor's sole remedy for a credit dispute, if any, shall be money damages to the extent permitted by law.

### 10. Publicity

Contractor will not issue press releases or make public announcements about the Project, post unreleased materials, or imply endorsement or affiliation without Company's prior written approval.

### 11. Insurance And Compliance

If requested by Company, Contractor will maintain commercially reasonable insurance appropriate to the services and provide proof of coverage. Contractor will comply with applicable laws, permit requirements, and workplace policies while performing services.

### 12. Indemnity

Contractor will indemnify and hold harmless Company and its successors, licensees, distributors, and assigns from third-party claims arising from Contractor's negligence, willful misconduct, or material breach of this Agreement, including breach of the originality and clearance warranties, subject to customary notice and defense provisions.

### 13. Termination

Company may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon written notice, in which case Contractor will be paid for approved services properly performed through the termination date. Company may terminate immediately for material breach if Contractor fails to cure after reasonable notice where cure is possible.

### 14. Remedies

Contractor agrees that any dispute with Company shall not interfere with the development, production, marketing, or exploitation of the Project, and Contractor shall not seek injunctive relief against the Project to the extent permitted by law. Contractor's remedies, if any, shall be limited to money damages.

### 16. Entire Agreement

This Agreement is the entire agreement between the parties regarding its subject matter and may be modified only in a writing signed by both parties.
