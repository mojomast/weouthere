# 11_legal/entity_structure.md

## Purpose

Set the baseline legal and entity assumptions for WE OUT HERE so creative, financing, production, and distribution decisions are made against one working structure. This is a planning document for deal development and internal alignment, not final legal advice.

## Status
Draft v1 - Wave 1 assumptions foundation. Working draft for internal use only. All entity, tax, securities, employment, guild, chain-of-title, and distribution implications require attorney review before formation, fundraising, contracting, or release.

## Core Assumption

The assumed operating entity for the project is **Out Here Productions LLC**, a Delaware limited liability company, used as the primary rights-holding and contracting company for WE OUT HERE unless counsel recommends a different structure.

## Proposed Entity Structure

### Base structure

Recommended Wave 1 structure:

1. **Out Here Productions LLC (Delaware)** serves as the parent operating entity.
2. WE OUT HERE is developed as a project controlled by that entity.
3. The parent entity owns or controls the underlying original sitcom IP and grants project-specific rights as needed for development, production, and distribution.

Why this structure is assumed:

- Keeps early-stage ownership centralized.
- Makes chain of title easier to explain to buyers, investors, insurers, and distributors.
- Gives one entity authority to sign writer, producer, cast, crew, vendor, financing, festival, and distribution agreements.
- Preserves flexibility to later create a single-purpose production subsidiary if financing, liability isolation, or distributor requirements make that useful.

### Practical operating assumption

Unless counsel advises otherwise, Out Here Productions LLC should be treated as the company that:

- owns the project IP or has an exclusive assignment of it
- pays development and production expenses
- enters submission, option, shopping, attachment, and production agreements
- opens and controls project bank accounts
- receives revenue from licenses, grants, prizes, advances, and other exploitation of the project

## IP Ownership Chain

The chain of title should be clean, written, dated, and fully signed.

Recommended ownership chain assumption:

1. The original creator(s) of WE OUT HERE create the original sitcom concept, characters, pilot material, series materials, and related development assets.
2. Those rights are assigned in writing to Out Here Productions LLC, or are confirmed as owned by Out Here Productions LLC under valid work-made-for-hire and assignment language.
3. Any later contributions by writers, producers, directors, designers, editors, composers, or contractors are either:
   - created as work made for hire where legally effective, and
   - backed by present-tense assignment language transferring all right, title, and interest to the designated company.
4. If a project-level SPV is later formed, Out Here Productions LLC may license or assign specified production and exploitation rights into that SPV under a written intra-company agreement, while retaining rights not expressly transferred.

Minimum chain-of-title assumptions for Wave 1:

- No third party owns the underlying format, remake rights, life rights, or source material.
- The project is original and not based on unlicensed underlying IP.
- All collaborators sign IP assignment and release language before substantial contribution or public release.
- All music, artwork, footage, locations, trademarks, and other third-party elements are separately cleared or licensed.

## Principal Roles

The exact titles may change, but the legal function of each role should be clear.

### Members / founders

- Approve company formation and governing documents.
- Approve ownership percentages, capital contributions, and major reserved matters.
- Approve issuance of any profit participation, membership interest, or investor rights.

### Managing member or manager

- Has authority to act for Out Here Productions LLC within limits set by the operating agreement.
- Signs routine agreements, opens bank accounts, engages vendors, and oversees compliance.
- Maintains records of approvals and delegated authority.

### Producer / executive producer

- Handles practical development and production execution.
- May negotiate talent, vendor, and distribution-facing terms, subject to approval thresholds.
- Should not be assumed to own IP unless a separate written grant says so.

### Production counsel / entertainment counsel

- Reviews formation, operating agreement, chain of title, financing documents, release strategy, guild issues, and distribution agreements.
- Confirms whether Delaware plus any operating-state filings are sufficient.
- Flags securities, labor, privacy, trademark, defamation, E&O, and tax risks.

### Accountant / bookkeeper / payroll provider

- Maintains company books.
- Tracks member contributions, production spend, revenue, reimbursements, and participations.
- Supports tax reporting and audit trail requirements.

## Banking And Compliance Assumptions

Out Here Productions LLC should not operate informally.

Baseline assumptions:

- Separate EIN obtained for the entity.
- Separate company bank account opened in the entity name.
- No mixing of personal and company funds.
- Written bookkeeping process in place from first dollar spent.
- Approval rules documented for expenditures, reimbursements, contracts, and wire transfers.
- State formation, foreign qualification, annual report, franchise tax, and beneficial ownership reporting obligations are tracked.
- Payroll is run correctly if cast or crew are treated as employees.
- Contractor classification is reviewed before issuing 1099s or equivalent forms.
- Insurance planning begins early, including general liability, workers' compensation where required, and E&O before distribution.

Operational assumption for a streamer, festival, and digital-first path:

- The entity must be able to receive funds from grants, prizes, brand deals, license fees, platform revenue, and distributor advances.
- The entity should maintain organized copies of all contracts, invoices, releases, and rights documentation in a single controlled repository.
- If crowdfunding or outside investment is pursued, counsel must review securities, consumer protection, and platform-specific requirements before launch.

## Subsidiary / SPV Option

Wave 1 assumption: do **not** create a separate SPV unless there is a concrete reason.

Possible later structure:

1. Out Here Productions LLC remains the parent rights-holder.
2. A project-specific subsidiary or SPV is formed for WE OUT HERE production and financing.
3. The parent grants the SPV only the rights needed to develop, produce, finance, and exploit the project.

Reasons an SPV may become appropriate:

- outside investors require project-only economics
- lenders or distributors want ring-fenced liabilities
- co-production partners need a dedicated vehicle
- insurance, payroll, or collection-account structure works better at project level
- multiple projects under the parent make cost and rights separation important

Risks of creating an SPV too early:

- more legal and accounting overhead
- more tax and reporting complexity
- more documents needed to explain intercompany rights and approvals
- greater chance of chain-of-title confusion if transfers are poorly documented

## Approval Framework

The operating agreement or written company resolutions should define who can approve what.

Suggested reserved matters for member-level approval:

- admitting new members or investors
- issuing equity or profit interests
- borrowing money or granting security interests
- selling, assigning, or exclusively licensing core IP
- approving distribution agreements with meaningful exclusivity or long terms
- approving material settlements or litigation strategy
- dissolving the entity or merging it into another company

Suggested manager-level authority, subject to budget limits:

- routine vendor agreements
- festival submissions
- standard contractor agreements
- account opening and payment processing
- ordinary-course marketing and release expenses

All major approvals should be documented in signed resolutions or written consents.

## Counsel Review Flags

The following issues require direct attorney review before the structure is treated as final:

- Whether Delaware is the best state for the parent entity given where business will actually be conducted.
- Whether foreign qualification is needed in the principal operating state.
- Whether member-managed or manager-managed governance is better for the team.
- Whether the original creator agreements fully transfer all IP needed for development, production, and sequels, remakes, spin-offs, merchandising, and promotional use.
- Whether any contributor could later claim authorship, credit, approval, termination, or ownership rights.
- Whether fundraising plans trigger securities law compliance.
- Whether any planned compensation structure creates wage-and-hour, employment, or tax exposure.
- Whether guild or union obligations may apply.
- Whether title clearance, trademark review, defamation review, privacy review, and E&O requirements are sufficient for streamer or distributor delivery.
- Whether digital-first release plans create additional music, clip, influencer, sponsorship, or platform-terms issues.
- Whether a subsidiary or SPV is needed before financing closes or principal photography begins.

## Working Legal Position

This document is a production planning assumption set only. It is **not** jurisdiction-specific final legal advice, does not replace formation documents, and should not be used as the sole basis for fundraising, hiring, contracting, or distributing WE OUT HERE. Final structure, IP ownership, approvals, financing mechanics, and compliance obligations must be confirmed by qualified entertainment and tax counsel.
