# 11_legal/errors_and_omissions_checklist.md

## Purpose

Provide a practical E&O readiness checklist for WE OUT HERE so the project can prepare for insurer, distributor, festival, and platform diligence before release. This is an internal checklist, not final legal advice or insurance advice.

## Owner/Subagent
legal_agent

## Status
Draft v1 - internal checklist. Working draft only. Actual E&O requirements vary by insurer, territory, release path, and project content. Counsel and insurance broker review are required before relying on this checklist.

## Dependencies

- Final or near-final scripts and locked cut
- Contributor agreements and rights file
- Music, clip, artwork, location, and asset clearance records
- Marketing assets and trailer cuts
- Counsel and insurance broker input

## Plain-Language Notes

- E&O is not just an insurance purchase; it is a paperwork and clearance discipline.
- If the file trail is messy, E&O gets slower and more expensive.
- A show can be creatively finished and still be legally undeliverable.

## Core Checklist

### Rights And Ownership

- Underlying series concept ownership documented
- Writer agreements signed for all writing contributors
- Contractor agreements signed for all creative contributors whose work appears in the project
- Talent releases or performer agreements signed for all on-camera and voice performers
- Entity name used consistently across agreements
- Signed agreements stored in one organized folder

### Script And Content Review

- Script review completed for defamation, privacy, publicity, and sensitive factual references
- Real-person inspired characters reviewed for identifiability risk
- Brand and product references reviewed line by line
- Real websites, apps, interfaces, and online handles reviewed for clearance or replacement
- Fictional platform depiction reviewed to avoid literal Discord branding or close imitation

### Visual Clearance

- Production design review completed for logos, posters, artwork, apparel marks, signage, stickers, packaging, and screens
- Phones, laptops, chat overlays, and notification graphics reviewed frame by frame
- No uncleared screenshots from real services remain in final picture
- Stock footage, photos, and graphics matched to licenses
- Font and software license compliance checked where relevant for deliverables

### Music And Sound

- Original score agreement signed if score is commissioned
- All pre-existing music matched to sync and master licenses where needed
- No temp tracks remain in final exports
- Cue sheet process ready
- Trailer and social cut music cleared separately if needed

### Locations And People

- Location agreements collected where needed
- Releases for recognizable non-cast individuals obtained where appropriate
- Minor documentation complete if any minors are involved
- Crowd scenes reviewed for release needs and practical exposure

### Marketing And Publicity

- Posters, trailers, teasers, clips, and social assets reviewed separately from the show itself
- Key art owned or licensed to the company
- Pull quotes, festival laurels, and endorsements verified before use
- No third-party memes, reposted images, or unlicensed clips used in marketing

### Business And Delivery

- Final chain-of-title folder assembled
- Counsel review memo requested if insurer or distributor expects one
- Claims or threats log maintained if any complaints have already arisen
- Final title decision checked for clearance concerns
- Insurance broker informed of any higher-risk content areas

## Documents Commonly Requested By Insurers Or Distributors

- chain-of-title summary
- contributor agreements
- releases and licenses
- title report or clearance memo
- script and final cut legal review notes
- music licenses and cue sheet information
- production entity details

## Hard Stop Issues

Do not assume the project is E&O-ready if any of these remain unresolved:

- missing writer or creator assignment
- visible use of real-world branded platform interfaces without review
- uncleared music in the cut or trailer
- unlicensed artwork, screenshots, or stock assets
- unresolved complaint from a contributor claiming ownership or approval rights
- a character that the team informally admits is plainly based on an identifiable real person

## Attorney Review Flags

- Review of final cut and final script for content claims exposure.
- Review of fictional platform graphics for trademark and trade dress risk.
- Confirmation that release forms and assignments are sufficient for insurer and distributor purposes.
- Review of title clearance and any known third-party objections.
- Review of any fair use or parody positions before they are relied upon.

## Working Legal Position

This checklist is an internal readiness tool and not final jurisdiction-specific legal advice, insurance advice, or a guarantee of insurer acceptance. Actual requirements depend on the insurer, distributor, territory, and facts.
