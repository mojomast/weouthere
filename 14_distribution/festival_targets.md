## Festival Thesis
Festivals should be used to frame `WE OUT HERE` as a serious, authored, culturally current indie series. The goal is not to collect generic laurels. The goal is to secure the right curatorial stamp, create a premiere story, and generate leverage with buyers, critics, and online tastemakers.

Because festival sections change over time, every target below should be verified against current rules, premiere requirements, and episodic eligibility before submission.

## Primary Festival Targets

### Sundance
Why fit:
- strongest signal for indie authorship and culture impact
- helpful if the show is being framed as a prestige discovery rather than market-product volume content

Best use:
- top-tier launch attempt if the material is fully premium in execution and the team can support a major premiere push

Pitch frame:
- a formally precise dark comedy about AI panic, burnout, and internet-native self-justification

Priority:
- Tier 1

### SXSW
Why fit:
- unusually strong fit for current-workplace, tech-adjacent, internet-literate storytelling
- good environment for cultural press, online conversation, and audience-forward discovery

Best use:
- launchpad for a series that can play both to industry and to digitally fluent public audiences

Pitch frame:
- sharp half-hour comedy built for post-screening conversation, clipping, and press discussion

Priority:
- Tier 1

### Tribeca
Why fit:
- strong hybrid space for independent TV, episodic work, and work that sits between film culture and platform culture
- especially useful if the team wants buyer attention plus New York media visibility

Best use:
- premiere or high-profile North American slot that supports both prestige and practical meetings

Pitch frame:
- premium indie episodic comedy with formal confidence and urban professional-culture specificity

Priority:
- Tier 1

## Secondary Festival Targets

### SeriesFest
Why fit:
- one of the clearest series-specific validation environments
- useful for market-facing meetings, peer validation, and concentrated episodic attention

Best use:
- if the team wants a strong TV-specific festival signal and concentrated industry exposure

Priority:
- Tier 2

### IFP / Gotham-Aligned Episodic Showcases or Successor Programs
Why fit:
- good for prestige indie framing and critic-friendly positioning
- especially useful if the project's New York and media-world relevance is an asset

Priority:
- Tier 2

### Fantasia or Genre-Friendly Prestige Festivals With Episodic Openness
Why fit:
- only if the surreal or psychological edge is highlighted without mislabeling the show as genre-first

Best use:
- selective play where the formal rupture and dark comic instability are being rewarded, not misunderstood

Priority:
- Tier 3, case by case

## Tertiary or Strategic Niche Targets

### Art, New Media, or Digital Storytelling Festivals
These matter if a controlled digital-first path becomes primary and the team wants curator support around form.

Potential fit conditions:
- strong emphasis on desktop realism and screen-language craft
- appetite for hybrid narrative forms
- programming lanes that welcome episodic or serialized work

Use cautiously:
- only if inclusion strengthens prestige or conversation value
- avoid events that would make the project feel like a tech demo instead of a series

## Festival Selection Criteria
Each festival should be evaluated on:

- prestige and critical signal
- buyer attendance and industry usefulness
- suitability for episodic or half-hour work
- premiere requirements and exclusivity restrictions
- press density and culture-writer presence
- whether the audience actually understands dark comedy with internet-native execution

## Submission Materials To Prepare
- polished synopsis in festival language rather than buyer language
- director or creator statement emphasizing form and urgency
- screeners with readable interfaces and clean subtitles where needed
- stills and key art that make the show look premium at thumbnail size
- episode selection strategy: pilot only, first two episodes, or curated excerpt package depending on rules
- brief note on the formal grammar so programmers know the screen language is intentional and production-legible

## Premiere Strategy

### Preferred Outcome
Premiere at one top-tier festival with enough press and buyer density to generate downstream leverage.

### Acceptable Outcome
Premiere at a respected series-focused or culture-forward festival that can still support discovery and a strong campaign narrative.

### Avoid
- scattering submissions without a hierarchy
- burning premiere status on low-signal events
- accepting slots that do not materially help sale, press, or launch positioning

## Programming Angles To Emphasize
- a dark comedy about AI culture that is actually about people, pressure, and self-delusion
- a premium indie series using desktop realism as narrative grammar rather than novelty
- a compact six-episode season with clear rise-and-collapse architecture
- a show for audiences who recognize how group chats, work language, and performative expertise now govern daily life

## Risks

### Risk: Programmers assume the form is technically interesting but emotionally thin
Mitigation:
- center the trio, friendship damage, and psychological pressure in all submission copy

### Risk: The project is routed into tech-themed programming instead of prestige comedy curation
Mitigation:
- lead with dark comedy, character, and formal control; treat AI as context, not genre

### Risk: Festival timing delays a stronger direct release opportunity
Mitigation:
- model each submission against realistic announcement and screening dates before committing

## Recommended Festival Sequence
1. Submit top-tier general prestige festivals first.
2. Submit high-fit episodic and hybrid festivals second.
3. Submit niche digital or experimental targets only if they strengthen, rather than narrow, perception of the show.

## Success Criteria
- selection by at least one festival that materially improves credibility
- at least one meaningful press, buyer, or partner conversation generated by festival presence
- positioning of `WE OUT HERE` as an authored indie series, not a novelty-format experiment
