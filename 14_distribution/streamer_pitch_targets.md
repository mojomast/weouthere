## Targeting Principles
The right buyers for `WE OUT HERE` are not simply the largest platforms. They are the outlets most likely to understand all three of the following at once:

- premium half-hour dark comedy
- formally intentional screen-and-live-action execution
- current cultural subject matter that can drive criticism, conversation, and clipping

The show should be pitched as a prestige indie discovery with audience upside, not as a broad-comedy volume play.

## Primary Targets

### Max / HBO Ecosystem
Why fit:
- strongest fit for prestige dark comedy with tonal confidence
- audience and press ecosystem already supports difficult, current, conversation-heavy work
- formal ambition is more likely to be treated as value rather than friction

Pitch angle:
- a compact prestige indie comedy about AI culture, work panic, and friendship collapse
- internet-native form executed with cinematic control, not gimmick energy

What they need to believe:
- the show can sit adjacent to premium comedy-drama without losing humor density
- the formal language increases critical distinction

Priority:
- Tier 1

### FX / Hulu
Why fit:
- strong home for sharp auteur comedy, psychologically messy ensemble work, and current social material
- can support edgy comedy that still feels premium and culturally literate

Pitch angle:
- `Industry` precision plus `Search Party` spiral energy in a half-hour workplace collapse format
- a series that understands how work, identity, and online fluency now break people

What they need to believe:
- the show is character-first and not dependent on niche tech references
- episode pacing lands as compulsive half-hour storytelling

Priority:
- Tier 1

### Netflix
Why fit:
- global reach and appetite for buzzy, current, highly discussable concepts
- can turn clipped moments and quotable scenes into accelerated discovery if the campaign is right

Pitch angle:
- a darkly funny, highly current ensemble series with strong trailer and social conversation potential
- six episodes, low-friction sampling, high binge completion potential

What they need to believe:
- the project is not too insidery
- the show can cut through high-volume title flow with a sharp campaign hook

Priority:
- Tier 2

### Prime Video
Why fit:
- broad library strategy can make room for culturally current adult comedy if packaged credibly
- a practical buyer if the team emphasizes accessibility plus topical relevance

Pitch angle:
- prestige indie dark comedy with audience-recognizable work-life stakes and a strong hook around AI consultancy culture

What they need to believe:
- the show plays beyond pure tastemaker audiences
- the marketing can land the premise quickly

Priority:
- Tier 2

### Apple TV+
Why fit:
- premium look-and-feel alignment and interest in controlled tone
- potential fit if the series is packaged as a sleek but psychologically disruptive half-hour

Pitch angle:
- precise, authored comedy about people using product language, therapeutic language, and systems thinking to avoid reality

What they need to believe:
- the series has emotional depth, elegance, and awards-adjacent upside
- the project's messiness is intentional rather than chaotic

Priority:
- Tier 2

## Secondary Targets

### MUBI
Why fit:
- especially strong if the series gains festival heat and art-world framing
- could position the show as a curated discovery rather than a mainstream comedy buy

Pitch angle:
- festival-launched prestige indie episodic work with a distinctive formal voice

Priority:
- Tier 2 for post-festival conversations

### AMC+ / IFC Films Unlimited / Similar Boutique Platforms
Why fit:
- useful if a boutique positioning strategy is stronger than a scale strategy
- can offer tastemaker credibility if a top-tier premium streamer pass does not invalidate the project's heat

Pitch angle:
- a sharp auteur dark comedy built for critics, culture writers, and digitally fluent adults

Priority:
- Tier 3

### International Public or Prestige Broadcaster Streamers
Examples to evaluate:
- Channel 4 streaming platforms
- BBC-adjacent digital arms
- ARTE
- CBC Gem or similar curated public platforms

Why fit:
- good targets if the show's social diagnosis and formal execution translate especially well to curator-driven environments

Pitch angle:
- a contemporary workplace and internet-culture comedy with strong critical framing value

Priority:
- Tier 3, territory dependent

## Tertiary or Opportunistic Targets

### YouTube Originals-Style Brand Partners, FAST Platforms, or AVOD Services
These are not first-choice prestige homes, but they matter if:

- a controlled digital-first release becomes primary
- the team wants library monetization after an initial window
- clips, compilations, and discovery mechanics become central to long-tail strategy

Priority:
- only after primary prestige and curated options are exhausted or intentionally bypassed

## Buyer Ranking Summary

### Tier 1
- Max / HBO ecosystem
- FX / Hulu

### Tier 2
- Netflix
- Prime Video
- Apple TV+
- MUBI after festival traction

### Tier 3
- Boutique streamers and curated regional buyers
- specialty or secondary digital homes

## Packaging Priorities By Buyer Type

### Premium Prestige Buyers
Lead materials:
- treatment
- creator statement
- teaser or sizzle
- tonal comps
- audience positioning

Main story:
- the show is authored, current, and formally disciplined

### Scale Buyers
Lead materials:
- sharp short logline
- trailer or teaser with instant hook
- simple audience promise
- proof that the show is accessible beyond tech workers

Main story:
- current concept plus bingeable compact season

### Curated Boutique Buyers
Lead materials:
- festival strategy
- formal statement on screen grammar
- stills and visual identity package
- critical positioning memo

Main story:
- the show is a conversation object with cultural and formal edge

## Outreach Rules
- never blast the same message to every buyer tier
- tailor the first paragraph to the buyer's brand identity and programming taste
- lead with why the series belongs on that platform, not with generic excitement
- avoid overexplaining AI mechanics; explain the human stakes instead
- if using comps, keep them disciplined and consistent across outreach

## What To Avoid In Streamer Pitches
- calling the project a "web series" even when describing digital adaptability
- pitching it as anti-tech satire or issue programming
- over-indexing on internet references at the expense of emotional engine
- apologizing for the formal execution instead of presenting it as a differentiator
