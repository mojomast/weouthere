## Path Evaluation Standard
Every release path should be judged against the same five questions:

- does it increase legitimacy?
- does it preserve the show's formal identity?
- does it create audience efficiently?
- does it protect future rights value?
- does it give the team enough control over framing and timing?

## Path A: Direct Streamer Sale or License

### Definition
Sell or license the series directly to a streamer or prestige digital platform before a major public release.

### Why It Works
- fastest route to scale, legitimacy, and discoverability
- strongest validation signal for press and talent
- offloads much of delivery, marketing, and audience servicing burden

### Tradeoffs
- lower control over release framing and campaign positioning
- risk that the show is under-marketed inside a crowded slate
- rights and exclusivity asks may limit future flexibility

### Best If
- a buyer is genuinely enthusiastic, not merely opportunistic
- the platform brand strengthens prestige positioning
- the economics outperform controlled self-release scenarios

### Operational Needs
- delivery specifications ready
- legal chain of title clear
- buyer-ready teaser, stills, metadata, and creator materials prepared

## Path B: Festival-First Then Streamer or Distributor Sale

### Definition
Premiere at a major festival or episodic showcase, use curation and press to increase leverage, then pursue sale or licensing conversations.

### Why It Works
- creates scarcity and context before broad release
- helps buyers understand the show as a discovery object
- can generate critical framing that sharpens market appetite

### Tradeoffs
- slower clock to audience access
- premiere restrictions may reduce flexibility
- success depends on actual selection quality, not merely submitting

### Best If
- early buyer response is interested but noncommittal
- the formal execution is strong enough to benefit from curatorial framing
- the team can support festival travel, screenings, and press moments

### Operational Needs
- submission calendar management
- premiere status policy
- festival cut and screening assets finalized early

## Path C: Festival-First Then Digital-First Launch

### Definition
Use festival selection as a prestige marker, then release directly through owned or partner-supported digital channels instead of waiting indefinitely for a sale.

### Why It Works
- combines cultural validation with release control
- lets the project market itself as festival-selected while still owning audience relationship
- useful if buyers admire the work but do not move fast enough

### Tradeoffs
- team still carries most launch execution burden
- revenue may be slower and more fragmented than a sale
- requires strong direct marketing discipline

### Best If
- festival heat is real but acquisition appetite is soft
- press and online tastemakers are responding strongly
- the team is prepared to act while awareness is still warm

### Operational Needs
- direct release infrastructure
- newsletter, website, and audience funnel ready
- episode rollout calendar, clips plan, and PR rhythm built

## Path D: Controlled Digital-First Premiere

### Definition
Launch directly online through owned channels, curated platform partners, or a hybrid eventized digital rollout without waiting for festival validation.

### Why It Works
- maximum control over brand, schedule, and audience data
- fits the show's internet-native formal identity
- enables fast iteration on messaging, clips, and community response

### Tradeoffs
- hardest path for immediate prestige signaling
- requires real marketing execution, not just posting episodes
- audience attention must be built actively rather than borrowed from a platform or festival

### Best If
- the package is strong and direct audience appetite is visible
- buyer conversations are slow or misaligned
- the team values retained rights and long-tail brand building

### Operational Needs
- release site or platform hub
- trailer, key art, social cutdowns, press rollout, and launch calendar
- clip licensing and music rights cleared for direct online exploitation

## Path E: Hybrid Windowed Strategy

### Definition
Run a limited event-screening or festival window, then move into a scheduled digital-first or boutique-platform release, with later AVOD or transactional windows as appropriate.

### Why It Works
- balances prestige, control, and monetization flexibility
- creates multiple beats instead of one launch spike
- useful for a project with both curator appeal and online-native discoverability

### Tradeoffs
- operationally more complex
- requires careful rights management and release discipline
- can dilute urgency if windows are too slow or poorly explained

### Best If
- the team can manage a phased campaign
- there is a credible event layer and a credible digital layer
- the project benefits from multiple narratives: premiere, release, long-tail clip circulation

### Operational Needs
- detailed rights calendar
- partner alignment across all windows
- audience messaging that makes the sequence feel intentional

## Recommended Default Ranking
If all else is equal, the current preferred order is:

1. direct premium streamer sale or license
2. festival-first then sale
3. festival-first then digital-first launch
4. controlled digital-first premiere
5. hybrid windowed release if complexity is justified by upside

This ranking should change only if actual market evidence points elsewhere.

## Digital-First Design Principles
If digital-first becomes primary, the release should not imitate a compromised streamer launch. It should lean into the strengths of the project.

Required principles:

- eventize each drop or screening beat
- use clips, quotes, and screen-language moments as discoverability assets
- make the website the canonical trust layer for press, partners, and audience onboarding
- release with editorial context: interviews, creator notes, or press essays that frame the project as a prestige indie work
- preserve a premium visual system across all thumbnails, assets, and copy

## Trigger Events That Should Force Re-Evaluation
- a strong buyer request for exclusivity
- a top-tier festival selection
- a meaningful press commitment tied to launch timing
- evidence that the audience is responding strongly to teaser materials online
- rights complications that make one path materially worse than expected

## Minimum Go Criteria Before Public Release
- final picture and sound delivery ready
- rights cleared for the intended window and territory
- trailer, stills, synopsis, and metadata locked
- website and contact routing live
- social rollout and press plan approved
- analytics and conversion tracking in place for direct channels

## Recommendation
Prepare all materials as if pursuing a streamer-first sale, but operationally remain ready to pivot into festival-first or digital-first release without rebuilding the entire package. That dual-readiness is the most strategic posture for `WE OUT HERE`.
