## Distribution Thesis
`WE OUT HERE` should be sold and released as a prestige indie dark comedy with internet-native formal execution. The project's commercial edge is not scale spectacle. It is precision, contemporaneity, and authorship.

The distribution strategy should preserve three truths at once:

- the show has clear streamer upside because it is legibly current, compact, and premium in taste
- the show has festival value because its form, voice, and cultural diagnosis are curator-friendly
- the show has viable digital-first options because its formal grammar, quotable writing, and internet literacy can travel natively online without feeling downgraded

## Release Goals
- Secure a high-credibility home that frames the series as a serious cultural object rather than disposable niche comedy
- Maximize discovery through tastemaker ecosystems: festivals, critics, platform editors, cultural writers, and clipped social circulation
- Preserve flexibility between sale, licensed window, and self-distributed digital-first release paths
- Build a release story that makes the show's form feel intentional and event-worthy
- Convert internet-native distinctiveness into press, programmer, and buyer curiosity rather than allowing it to be mistaken for "web-only" scale

## Primary Strategic Position
The preferred top-line position is:

**A prestige indie half-hour dark comedy with a sharp contemporary point of view, designed for streamer, festival, and digital-first circulation without creative compromise.**

This position does four jobs:

- signals quality to premium buyers
- signals form and authorship to festivals
- signals adaptability to digital partners
- avoids overcommitting too early to either a traditional sale narrative or a self-release narrative

## Core Buyer Promise
Partners are not being asked to rescue an experimental object. They are being offered a compact, highly discussable, culturally current comedy with built-in audience conversation hooks.

What the market gets:

- a six-episode season with clean bingeability and limited buyer risk
- a current subject without preachy issue framing
- distinctive formal execution that feels native to how people actually work and speak now
- strong critical-discourse potential because the show is about AI culture, language, burnout, and self-justification without being reducible to "AI satire"

## Distribution Priorities

### Priority 1: Streamer-Oriented Packaging
Build materials and outreach first so the project can be evaluated by premium buyers, prestige-comedy labels, and acquisition teams.

Why first:

- streamer validation improves every downstream option
- even a pass can sharpen language and target fit
- packaging for buyers also improves festival and digital materials

### Priority 2: Festival Legitimacy Track
Use selective festivals to create discovery, press context, and prestige framing if a direct streamer path does not materialize early or if festivals can improve leverage before sale.

Why second:

- festivals can manufacture scarcity, signal taste, and create urgency
- they help reframe formally unusual work as curator-endorsed rather than commercially uncertain

### Priority 3: Digital-First Release Readiness
Prepare a controlled direct-to-audience path from the beginning rather than treating it as a fallback of last resort.

Why this matters:

- the show's internet-native execution can win online if released with discipline
- digital-first readiness improves negotiating posture because the project retains a credible alternative path
- owned release channels create audience and data assets even if an eventual licensing deal comes later

## Preferred Order of Operations

### Phase 1: Packaging and Market Readiness
- lock master positioning language
- cut teaser, sizzle, and proof-of-form materials
- prepare one-sheet, pitch deck, creator statement, and target list
- define rights strategy and holdback assumptions with legal

### Phase 2: Streamer and Sales Conversations
- run targeted outreach to premium buyers, boutique distributors, and sales partners
- prioritize buyers whose brands benefit from culturally current auteur comedy
- track responses to identify whether market pull is strongest around direct acquisition, co-financing, or completed-series licensing

### Phase 3: Festival Submission Window
- if direct sale is not closed quickly, route into top-tier episodic and hybrid festivals
- use submissions to generate launch timing, press beats, and social proof
- coordinate premiere status requirements carefully so digital release optionality remains intact

### Phase 4: Decision Gate
At a defined gate, choose among:

- direct sale or license
- festival-first then sale
- festival-first then digital release
- controlled digital-first launch without festival dependency

### Phase 5: Release Execution
- coordinate trailer, key art, press rollout, critic access, and social clipping plan
- turn the show's quotable lines and screen grammar into a discovery engine
- support launch with interviews, live events, and targeted online community engagement

## Decision Framework
Choose the lead path based on which of the following conditions becomes true first.

### Choose Streamer-First If
- a buyer clearly understands the show's tone and current relevance
- the deal meaningfully expands audience, press reach, and legitimacy
- the platform will not flatten the formal identity in marketing
- the economics are materially better than self-distribution plus long-tail licensing

### Choose Festival-First If
- tastemaker validation would materially improve leverage
- the show benefits from premiere framing and communal first-watch energy
- the current market is crowded and a curation signal would help separate it from generic startup/AI material

### Choose Digital-First If
- the team can control a disciplined release calendar and audience funnel
- buyer interest is soft but audience/press appeal remains high
- the materials are strong enough to convert online discovery into credibility rather than noise
- holding the asset longer creates more upside than waiting for a low-conviction sale

## Windowing Principles
- Protect premiere status where it has real value, but do not fetishize it.
- Keep short-form promo, clips, and behind-the-scenes materials separate from full-episode rights so outreach can continue while the series remains marketable.
- If festival rules require exclusivity, compare that value against digital momentum lost by waiting.
- Maintain flexibility for territory-by-territory or platform-by-platform licensing if a full-series exclusive buyer does not emerge.

## Rights Strategy Considerations
- retain as many ancillary rights as practical during early negotiations
- preserve the ability to exploit clips, stills, trailers, and BTS materials for marketing
- clarify whether any buyer is asking for worldwide exclusivity, extended holdbacks, sequel rights, remake rights, or derivative digital rights
- favor deals that do not block future community building, soundtrack, educational, or event-screening opportunities unless compensation justifies the restriction

## Packaging Requirements
Before major outreach, the project should have:

- one-page overview
- full pitch treatment
- target audience and tonal comps documents
- concise creator statement on why the show is urgent now
- teaser or sizzle that proves desktop realism plus live action plus surreal inserts can coexist cleanly
- stills, key art direction, and look references
- release logline and short synopsis variants tailored for buyers, programmers, and press

## Marketing Logic By Path

### Streamer Path
Lead with prestige, voice, and critical-discourse upside.

Marketing emphasis:

- current without being trend-trapped
- auteur but accessible
- premium half-hour binge object

### Festival Path
Lead with formal execution, cultural specificity, and ensemble precision.

Marketing emphasis:

- internet-native form used with discipline
- social diagnosis through comedy
- compact, conversation-starting episodes with event-screening value

### Digital-First Path
Lead with urgency, directness, and shareability without diluting taste.

Marketing emphasis:

- clips and quote moments as entry points
- controlled weekly or eventized drops
- critic and newsletter outreach timed to each beat of release

## Key Risks

### Risk: Misread as niche tech satire
Mitigation:
- always foreground friendship collapse, burnout, and status anxiety
- avoid jargon-heavy summaries in outward-facing materials

### Risk: Misread as "web series" rather than prestige indie series
Mitigation:
- lead with craft, format, and curatorial framing
- use premium visual materials and precise comps

### Risk: Festival strategy blocks stronger digital or buyer timing
Mitigation:
- model submission calendars against release windows before entering exclusivity situations

### Risk: Streamer pitch flattens the project's weirdness
Mitigation:
- pitch the formal execution as a disciplined storytelling advantage, not a gimmick to apologize for

## Success Metrics
- meaningful inbound interest from at least one premium buyer cohort and one curated festival cohort
- a clear primary release path selected without compromising rights unnecessarily
- materials strong enough that the same core package can convert across buyers, programmers, press, and direct audience channels
- launch framing that positions `WE OUT HERE` as a prestige indie discovery, not merely a topical experiment

## Immediate Action Items
- finalize buyer-facing positioning language
- build and prioritize the streamer, festival, and digital target lists
- define a premiere-status policy before festival submissions begin
- align legal, marketing, and website materials around the same release thesis
- prepare a distribution decision gate with timing, criteria, and owners
