## Positioning Statement
`WE OUT HERE` is a prestige indie dark comedy with internet-native formal execution: a six-episode half-hour series that turns AI consultancy culture, burnout, and group-chat-enabled self-delusion into a compact, premium, highly discussable viewing object.

## Market Definition
This is not broad startup parody and not issue-driven "AI content." It belongs in the lane of premium adult comedy with a sharp authorial point of view, a contained season order, and strong critical-conversation upside.

## What We Are Selling
- a 6 x 30 minute single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy
- a formally distinctive but production-manageable series
- a contemporary culture story that feels immediate without becoming trend-chasing
- a trio-led ensemble engine with strong performance, quote, and clip potential

## Core Sales Hooks

### 1. Prestige Indie With Topical Urgency
The show is current because it understands AI culture and online work life from the inside. It is not topical in the shallow sense of chasing headlines. It dramatizes a real social condition: people turning language, interfaces, and confidence theater into economic survival.

### 2. Formal Execution That Matches Lived Experience
Desktop realism, live action, and surreal inserts are not decorative. They let the series tell contemporary work life in the grammar people actually inhabit.

### 3. Compact Season, High Completion Potential
Six half-hours is an attractive sales shape. It lowers viewer commitment, supports bingeability, and reduces acquisition risk while preserving a strong escalation arc.

### 4. Critical and Social Conversation Value
The show can generate criticism, essays, interviews, and clipped social circulation because it is quotable, recognizable, and precise about language-driven self-protection.

## Audience Promise
The series promises viewers sharp recognition. People who have watched a group chat become a company, a deck become a moral shield, or a self-aware adult explain themselves into a worse choice will feel seen immediately.

## Buyer-Friendly Descriptions

### One-Line Version
A prestige indie dark comedy about three burned-out technologists who launch an AI consultancy from `Backchannel` and spiral from manic legitimacy into public consequences.

### Short Paragraph Version
`WE OUT HERE` is a 6 x 30 minute single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy about three internet-native technologists who turn burnout, fluency, and hype-cycle panic into a real AI consultancy. Combining desktop realism, live action, and surreal inserts, the series plays as a premium contemporary comedy about work, language, friendship, and self-delusion.

### Programmer or Distributor Version
An authored indie half-hour series with clear festival, streamer, and digital-first release logic, `WE OUT HERE` uses internet-native form to tell a psychologically sharp workplace collapse story that is as funny as it is culturally current.

## Key Selling Points By Partner Type

### For Streamers
- premium tone and compact order
- culturally current hook with broad recognition value
- critical-discourse and clipping potential

### For Festivals
- distinctive formal execution
- strong curatorial language around contemporary labor, AI, and internet-mediated selfhood
- authored voice with audience-legible stakes

### For Sales Agents or Distributors
- multiple release pathways without identity confusion
- manageable runtime footprint
- a marketing story that can travel across press, social, and cultural partnerships

## Comps Discipline
Use comps to clarify tone, not to overclaim scale.

Recommended frame:
- `Industry` for workplace precision
- `Search Party` for spiraling self-delusion
- `Severance` for systems-conscious formal control

Optional phrasing:
- prestige indie comedy for digitally fluent adults
- a dark workplace psychocomedy for the AI panic era

## What The Show Is Not
- not a broad anti-tech spoof
- not a sci-fi cautionary tale
- not a niche coding comedy for insiders only
- not a gimmick screenlife experiment

## Objection Handling

### Objection: "Is this too niche for non-tech viewers?"
Response:
No. Tech specificity is texture. The actual drama is friendship, status anxiety, burnout, and self-justification.

### Objection: "Is the form too experimental?"
Response:
No. The form is disciplined and legible. Screens carry evidence and workflow. Live action carries consequence. Surreal inserts carry subjectivity.

### Objection: "Is this just an AI trend piece?"
Response:
No. AI is the immediate pressure system, but the underlying subject is contemporary professional life and the psychology of legitimacy.

### Objection: "Can this travel internationally?"
Response:
Yes. The details are specific, but the pressures are global: work precarity, platform behavior, expertise theater, and collapsing personal-professional boundaries.

## Messaging Guardrails
- never lead with jargon density
- never market the show as if it requires insider literacy to enjoy
- never let "internet-native" slide into disposable or unserious framing
- never describe the formal execution as an apology, workaround, or budget necessity

## Closing Pitch Frame
`WE OUT HERE` should be sold as a prestige indie comedy that happens to understand the internet better than most television does. Its commercial value is that it is exact, current, compact, and formally alive.
