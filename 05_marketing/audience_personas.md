## Persona Framework
These personas are not demographic stereotypes. They are practical audience models for messaging, outreach, and conversion.

## Persona 1: Burned-Out Platform Worker

### Snapshot
- Age range: 27-41
- Works in tech, product, design, startup ops, consulting, or adjacent knowledge work
- Heavy user of group chats, Slack-like tools, decks, and productivity theater
- Watches smart comedy at night as decompression and self-recognition

### What They Recognize Immediately
- fake confidence at work
- AI panic as a social script
- group chats turning into pseudo-organizations
- people using irony to hide actual fear

### What They Want
- comedy that understands work without dumbing it down
- characters who feel painfully accurate
- quotable lines about labor, status, and digital behavior

### Emotional Hook
"This is about the exact kind of work-life unreality I already inhabit."

### Content That Converts Them
- scene clips about meetings, decks, pitch language, or pseudo-strategy
- quote cards with workplace-defense-mechanism dialogue
- newsletter copy framing the series as a comedy of professional instability

### Best Channels
- X
- Bluesky
- Instagram
- newsletter

### CTA
Sign up for updates and share the sharpest line with coworkers or friends.

## Persona 2: Online-Native Comedy Viewer

### Snapshot
- Age range: 23-36
- Lives online culturally even if not professionally in tech
- Finds shows through clips, edits, group chats, reposts, and creator discourse
- Responds to specificity, rhythm, and recognizability faster than formal prestige language

### What They Recognize Immediately
- over-articulate damage
- meme logic as coping logic
- friend groups building delusional consensus
- chat language becoming real life

### What They Want
- dark comedy that feels current
- sharp edits and bite-size hooks
- character dynamics they can map instantly

### Emotional Hook
"These people are too fluent to be safe."

### Content That Converts Them
- TikTok clips with instant behavioral recognition
- Instagram reels and subtitled scene excerpts
- short premise-led posts that read like a joke and a warning at once

### Best Channels
- TikTok
- Instagram
- X
- Discord announcements

### CTA
Follow for clips, join the community, and tag the friend who would absolutely start this company.

## Persona 3: Prestige Comedy Tastemaker

### Snapshot
- Age range: 28-49
- Watches auteur-driven half-hours and follows critics, festivals, and boutique distributors
- Less interested in "AI" as a topic than in tonal control and character precision
- Often overlaps with critics, screenwriters, and cultural journalists

### What They Recognize Immediately
- psychologically grounded dark comedy
- formal ambition that serves character
- serialized escalation inside a compact season
- writing that is socially precise rather than generic

### What They Want
- proof of authorship
- confidence of tone
- comps that signal ambition without imitation

### Emotional Hook
"This feels like a real show with a real point of view, not topical content."

### Content That Converts Them
- trailers with clear tonal authorship
- stills and key art with disciplined captioning
- thoughtful Bluesky posts and newsletters
- press quotes framing the show as behaviorally exact and formally current

### Best Channels
- Bluesky
- Instagram
- newsletter
- festival press

### CTA
Watch the trailer, read the overview, and track the project through festival or press beats.

## Persona 4: Festival Programmer

### Snapshot
- Curates episodic, indie, hybrid-form, or culturally current work
- Evaluates programming fit, originality, and screening-discussion value
- Reads quickly and looks for confidence, clarity, and distinctiveness

### What They Need To See
- clear format and run time
- strong hook and aesthetic identity
- evidence the project will play in a room and in conversation
- proof the series extends beyond niche in-jokes

### Emotional Hook
"This could spark a strong audience reaction and a smart Q&A."

### Content That Converts Them
- concise festival-facing synopsis
- polished stills and trailer
- clean one-sheet and press-ready copy
- carefully timed launch updates tied to milestones

### Best Channels
- email outreach
- website press pages
- LinkedIn for credibility reinforcement
- selective X and Bluesky amplification after announcement

### CTA
Request screener materials or schedule a meeting.

## Persona 5: Streamer Or Buyer-Side Executive

### Snapshot
- Balances creative enthusiasm with packaging, audience clarity, and market signal
- Has seen too much generic startup satire and too much vague prestige positioning
- Needs quick proof this is both distinct and saleable

### What They Need To See
- clear positioning in one paragraph
- audience segments that are real and reachable
- evidence of cultural conversation potential
- a premium tone that can still travel commercially

### Emotional Hook
"This is current, specific, and legible enough to sell internally."

### Content That Converts Them
- tight synopsis and marketplace logline
- concise social proof and press framing
- professional LinkedIn and website presence
- teaser assets that demonstrate tone control immediately

### Best Channels
- direct outreach
- website
- LinkedIn
- industry-facing newsletter forwards

### CTA
Take a call, request deck or screener, evaluate fit for development or acquisition.

## Persona 6: Collaborator / Future Team Member

### Snapshot
- Could be cast, crew, composer, editor, designer, strategist, or advisor
- Assesses seriousness fast through language discipline and execution quality
- Wants a project that feels both creatively alive and operationally credible

### What They Need To See
- clear voice and world
- signs of movement and organization
- respectful, specific external communication
- a campaign that does not feel chaotic or vague

### Emotional Hook
"These people know what the show is and how to talk about it."

### Content That Converts Them
- LinkedIn updates
- website materials
- recruiting copy with real tone
- Discord/community communications that feel intentional, not sloppy

### Best Channels
- LinkedIn
- website
- email
- Discord announcements

### CTA
Reach out, apply, refer, or share the project with aligned collaborators.

## Persona Matrix

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Burned-Out Platform Worker | Recognition | Work has become performative unreality | X, Bluesky, Instagram, newsletter | Sign up and share |
| Online-Native Comedy Viewer | Immediate intrigue | These people are too fluent to be safe | TikTok, Instagram, X, Discord | Follow and join |
| Prestige Comedy Tastemaker | Authorship | Current, sharp, behaviorally exact | Bluesky, Instagram, newsletter | Watch and track |
| Festival Programmer | Programming value | Distinctive half-hour with discussion value | Email, website, LinkedIn | Request materials |
| Streamer / Buyer | Saleable specificity | Premium and legible with audience upside | Direct outreach, website, LinkedIn | Take meeting |
| Collaborator | Confidence | Serious project, clear voice, real motion | LinkedIn, website, email, Discord | Inquire or refer |

## Message Adaptation Rules
- For viewers, emphasize recognition and tone.
- For buyers, emphasize positioning and market clarity.
- For festivals, emphasize formal identity and cultural timeliness.
- For collaborators, emphasize seriousness, movement, and clarity.
- For all personas, avoid overexplaining AI. Lead with human behavior.

## Creative Implications
- Always create both clip-led and quote-led assets.
- Keep a pipeline of polished visuals for prestige audiences and press.
- Maintain a parallel stream of more immediate recognition-led content for TikTok, X, and Discord.
- Ensure every major launch beat has at least one buyer-facing version and one audience-facing version.
