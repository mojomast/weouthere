## Outreach Objective
Secure earned amplification from people whose audiences overlap with `WE OUT HERE`'s ideal viewers, collaborators, and cultural validators.

## Outreach Principle
Favor alignment over scale. A smaller creator or critic with real cultural overlap is more valuable than a large generic entertainment account.

## Priority Outreach Categories

### Category 1: Culture And TV Critics
Why they matter:
- they frame the project for tastemakers and prestige-comedy audiences
- they create quotable validation for later press use

Best asks:
- preview screening
- first-look exclusive
- quote or recommendation

### Category 2: Tech-Culture Commentators
Why they matter:
- they reach viewers who recognize the work and AI behavior immediately
- they can translate the premise to people who normally do not track indie TV launches

Best asks:
- share the teaser or trailer
- react to a clip
- include the project in a cultural roundup

### Category 3: Comedy Editors And Clip Accounts
Why they matter:
- they can drive fast discovery through recognition-led content
- they extend lifespan of quotable clips

Best asks:
- repost clip with credit
- feature in weekly comedy roundup
- collaborate on a short-first asset drop

### Category 4: Creative-Industry Newsletters And Hosts
Why they matter:
- they reach writers, producers, programmers, and collaborators
- they reinforce seriousness and market relevance

Best asks:
- include project in newsletter recommendation
- interview creators
- mention launch in industry notes

### Category 5: Niche Online Communities With Taste Authority
Why they matter:
- they generate the kind of word of mouth that feels discovered, not bought
- they often produce high-quality conversation and referral traffic

Best asks:
- post the trailer
- host a Q&A
- share launch note or link

## Who To Avoid
- generic engagement-farm entertainment accounts
- creators whose tone is too broad or brand-safe for the project
- anyone likely to flatten the series into anti-AI discourse bait
- accounts that require gimmicky trend participation to justify coverage

## Outreach Timing
- soft list building during pre-launch
- light warm contact before major reveal
- concentrated ask window during reveal and first sustain week
- follow-up with strongest-performing clips and press validation afterward

## Outreach Tiers

### Tier A
High-value critics, programmers, newsletter writers, and tastemakers.

Approach:
personalized, direct, concise, specific to their work.

### Tier B
Aligned creators and community voices with engaged niche audiences.

Approach:
personalized template with one strong reason for fit.

### Tier C
Broader amplification accounts and clip curators.

Approach:
short asset-forward outreach with easy repost materials.

## Outreach Package
Each contact should have access to:
- one-sentence project framing
- short synopsis
- watch link or clip link
- stills or poster
- social handles
- one explicit ask

## Sample Asks
- watch and share the trailer if it feels aligned
- include `WE OUT HERE` in your next recommendation roundup
- post this clip with your own framing
- join a short virtual preview or Q&A
- consider covering the project in a launch-week note

## Personalization Angles
- for critics: tone, format, current relevance
- for tech commentators: precise depiction of AI and work performance culture
- for comedy accounts: quotable lines and recognizable behavior
- for industry newsletters: market positioning and project momentum

## Outreach Message Rules
- keep emails and DMs short
- lead with why they specifically make sense
- make the ask easy and singular
- never sound mass-blasted
- never overstate the relationship or project stage

## Compensation And Ethics
- prioritize earned coverage and organic alignment
- if paid partnerships are considered later, label them clearly and keep them selective
- do not pay for creators whose audience fit is weak just to inflate vanity metrics

## Measurement
- response rate by tier
- share rate
- referral traffic by source
- follower and signup lift after major reposts
- quality of resulting audience comments and community joins

## Sample Outreach Email Skeleton
Subject: `WE OUT HERE` might be in your lane

Body logic:
- one sentence on why them
- one sentence on what the show is
- one sentence with the ask and link

## Sample Outreach DM Skeleton
"Your audience might be very in tune with this: `WE OUT HERE` is a 6 x 30 min dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy about three burned-out technologists who start an AI consultancy on `Backchannel`. We just dropped [asset]. If it feels aligned, would love for you to take a look."
