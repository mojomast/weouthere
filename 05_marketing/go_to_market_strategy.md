## Market Objective
Position `WE OUT HERE` as a premium, internet-literate, culturally current 6 x 30 min dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy that can travel across:

- streamers seeking distinctive half-hour series with sharp point of view
- festivals seeking formally contemporary work with strong auteur identity
- digital-native comedy viewers who share clips, lines, and aesthetics socially
- industry professionals evaluating cultural relevance, packaging, and execution quality
- collaborators who want proof the project is serious, specific, and moving

## Core Positioning

### Positioning Statement
`WE OUT HERE` is a dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy about three burned-out technologists who turn `Backchannel` into a chaotic AI consultancy and discover that their intelligence, irony, and shared delusion are commercially valuable right up until they become publicly catastrophic.

### Why It Is Timely
- AI hype has made expertise feel theatrical and unstable.
- Work now happens through interfaces, decks, chats, and confidence rituals.
- Digital-native audiences are ready for a comedy that understands online language from the inside.
- Industry buyers continue to seek premium half-hours with clear cultural specificity and quotable social traction.

### What Makes The Project Distinct
- It is about AI culture without becoming issue satire.
- It is internet-native without being disposable meme comedy.
- It combines desktop realism, live action, and surreal inserts in a producible way.
- It treats friendship, labor anxiety, and self-protective language as the real engines of comedy.

## Priority Audiences

### Tier 1: Streamers And Buyers
Who they are:
- development executives
- acquisitions teams
- independent producers
- reps and packaging allies

What they need to believe:
- the show has a sharp, marketable hook
- the execution is specific enough to break through
- the audience is real and legible
- the creative package can generate press, clips, and tastemaker uptake

Primary message:
`WE OUT HERE` is a premium half-hour with strong formal identity, cultural timeliness, and a built-in discourse engine around work, AI, and online behavior.

### Tier 2: Festivals And Programmers
Who they are:
- episodic festival programmers
- indie TV showcases
- cultural curators and selectors

What they need to believe:
- the project has authorial confidence
- the formal language is contemporary
- the humor travels beyond niche tech references
- screenings can generate post-screening discussion and coverage

Primary message:
`WE OUT HERE` is a formally modern, psychologically sharp ensemble comedy that captures platform-shaped work life with unusual specificity.

### Tier 3: Digital-Native Comedy Viewers
Who they are:
- millennials and older Gen Z viewers
- workers in tech, media, design, consulting, academia, startups, and freelance creative labor
- viewers who discover series via clips, quote cards, socials, and newsletters

What they need to feel:
- this show understands how people actually talk online
- it is funny, dark, and painfully recognizable
- it is not explaining the internet to them

Primary message:
This is the comedy for people who have watched a group chat become a company and knew that was a terrible sign.

### Tier 4: Industry And Collaborators
Who they are:
- cast, crew, department heads, creative partners, advisors, publicists, community moderators

What they need to believe:
- the team has a coherent strategy
- the world, tone, and audience are clearly defined
- joining the project is professionally and creatively worthwhile

Primary message:
`WE OUT HERE` is organized, legible, and culturally alive enough to justify serious participation.

## Messaging Hierarchy

### Message 1: The Hook
Three burned-out technologists launch an AI consultancy from `Backchannel`.

### Message 2: The Tone
Dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy with desktop realism, live action, and surreal inserts.

### Message 3: The Human Engine
The comedy comes from defense mechanisms, labor anxiety, mutual enabling, and failed containment.

### Message 4: The Cultural Relevance
The series captures a real contemporary condition: expertise as performance under AI panic and platform logic.

### Message 5: The Market Value
The show is built for critical conversation, social clipping, festival interest, and streamer relevance.

## Brand Language Rules
- Always render the title as `WE OUT HERE`.
- Always describe format as `6 x 30 min dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy` or `6 x 30 min single-camera dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy`.
- Use `Backchannel` in outward-facing copy. Do not state the platform as literally Discord in canonical materials.
- Avoid generic startup language like `disruptive`, `visionary founders`, or `future of work` unless used ironically in quoted character voice.
- Avoid pitching the show as sci-fi, sketch comedy, or broad satire.

## Go-To-Market Phases

### Phase 1: Foundation And Signal
Objective:
Establish a clear, premium, legible public identity.

Actions:
- launch website with canonical overview, email capture, contact path, and press-ready basics
- publish brand-forward social handles and locked bios
- prepare copy bank, teaser messaging, and a concise partner-facing synopsis
- begin soft audience warming through mood, quotes, premise hooks, and project-intent posts

Success signal:
- visitors quickly understand premise, tone, and seriousness

### Phase 2: Credibility And Proof
Objective:
Show the project is real, curated, and in motion.

Actions:
- share milestone updates, look development, team announcements, and select process artifacts
- begin direct outreach to press, tastemakers, collaborators, and early supporters
- build mailing list segmentation by audience type
- start community onboarding in a controlled way

Success signal:
- qualified inbound interest from collaborators, press, and audience early adopters

### Phase 3: Trailer, Festival, And Press Activation
Objective:
Convert interest into attention spikes and coverage.

Actions:
- release teaser, trailer, stills, and key art in a coordinated sequence
- use festival selections or launch milestones as major announcement anchors
- run a concentrated cross-platform copy campaign tailored to each platform's social grammar
- activate quote cards, clips, newsletter editorials, and Discord announcement cascades

Success signal:
- measurable lift in coverage, shares, signups, and industry meeting requests

### Phase 4: Premiere And Sustain
Objective:
Turn launch attention into ongoing conversation and retention.

Actions:
- publish episode-specific copy, clips, reactions, behind-the-scenes material, and critical pull quotes
- sequence character-forward and line-forward content between release beats
- maintain community and newsletter rhythm to convert attention into durable support

Success signal:
- sustained social conversation and recurring earned-media mentions beyond launch week

## Platform-Specific Copy Strategy

### X Strategy
- Lead with the sharpest premise or line.
- Keep most posts under two sentences.
- Use threads only for milestone context, festival recaps, or press roundups.
- Pair clips with a single observational sentence, not explanation-heavy setup.

### Bluesky Strategy
- Let posts breathe slightly more than on X.
- Use the platform for context, intent, and cultural framing.
- Prioritize quote-card stills, launch notes, and appreciative dialogue with early champions.

### Instagram Strategy
- Treat captions as concise extensions of the image, not mini-essays.
- Use carousels to move from premise to tone to hook.
- Build recurring visual series: quote cards, character cards, episode cards, review cards.

### TikTok Strategy
- Put the recognition moment in the first two seconds.
- Use subtitles on all clips.
- Favor behavior viewers instantly understand over references requiring lore.
- Test formats: clip, captioned still montage, direct-to-camera creator framing, mock artifact.

### LinkedIn Strategy
- Emphasize the project's craft, market fit, and production progress.
- Frame milestones as evidence of execution rather than self-congratulation.
- Keep humor present but restrained.

### Discord Announcement Strategy
- Write for members who have already opted in.
- Make announcements actionable: what happened, why it matters, what to do next.
- Include links, dates, and moderation expectations when relevant.

### Newsletter Strategy
- Open with one strong editorial sentence.
- Give readers a clear sense of movement.
- Include one primary CTA only.
- Archive each edition on the website for credibility and SEO.

## Audience Funnel Logic
1. Discovery through social clip, quote, press mention, or collaborator share.
2. Validation on website through synopsis, visuals, and legitimacy signals.
3. Commitment through newsletter signup or Discord join.
4. Deepening through recurring updates, launch beats, and behind-the-scenes context.
5. Amplification through audience sharing, press references, and collaborator advocacy.

## Outreach Priorities
- first: collaborators, tastemakers, targeted press, and aligned industry contacts
- second: digital-native comedy viewers and newsletter subscribers
- third: broader audience expansion after proof points exist

## KPIs
- email signups by source
- press responses and coverage rate
- collaborator inquiry quality
- social engagement by platform and content type
- website conversion to signup and contact
- Discord joins and announcement click-through rate
- trailer views and completion rate

## Risks And Countermeasures

### Risk: The show gets flattened into generic AI satire
Countermeasure:
lead every outward-facing message with character, friendship, and behavioral specificity.

### Risk: The campaign feels too niche or too online
Countermeasure:
pair tech-language specificity with universal hooks around work, status, burnout, and mutual self-delusion.

### Risk: Visuals signal startup brand parody instead of premium comedy
Countermeasure:
anchor all assets in dark-comedy authorship, performance, and emotional stakes.

### Risk: Too many channels create fragmented messaging
Countermeasure:
maintain one messaging hierarchy and adapt syntax per platform instead of rewriting the premise each time.
