## Newsletter Objective
Turn casual discovery into durable attention by giving high-intent audiences a reliable editorial channel that feels more thoughtful, more informative, and more intimate than social media.

## Newsletter Role In The Communications Stack
- website: canonical home
- social: discovery and velocity
- Discord: community immediacy
- newsletter: continuity, context, and conversion

## Primary Audience Segments
- audience members who want project updates
- collaborators and industry supporters tracking progress
- journalists, festival programmers, and tastemakers who prefer inbox over algorithmic discovery
- friends-of-project who want the easiest way to stay current

## Segmentation Recommendation
Use one main newsletter brand with lightweight tagging:

- audience / fans
- industry / press
- collaborators / recruiting

This keeps the system simple while allowing tailored calls to action.

## Editorial Voice
- intelligent
- concise
- culturally aware
- warm but not gushy
- specific rather than promotional

## Voice Rule
Every edition should sound like a strong editorial note from people who know exactly what the project is, not like automated campaign copy.

## Core Newsletter Promises
- useful updates, not filler
- better context than social posts provide
- early or direct access to important drops
- a clean sense of what is happening next

## Signup Strategy

### Main Website CTA
Use a simple, high-value promise:
"Get launch updates, first looks, and occasional notes from the world of `WE OUT HERE`."

### Social CTA Variants
- "Join the list for first looks and launch notes."
- "If you want the sharpest updates without relying on the timeline, the newsletter is here."
- "The inbox version is where we put the fuller context."

### Discord CTA Variant
- "If you want the official recap and next-step links in one place, the newsletter is the cleanest way to get them."

## Cadence
- pre-launch: 1 edition per month
- active launch window: 1 edition per week
- sustained post-launch period: 2 editions per month
- extra editions only for major announcements

## Newsletter Types

### Type 1: Introductory Note
Purpose:
introduce the project, premise, and why it exists now.

### Type 2: Milestone Update
Purpose:
announce a major project step such as trailer, festival selection, team addition, or site launch.

### Type 3: Behind-The-Scenes Note
Purpose:
share selective craft, visual, or development context without overexposing process.

### Type 4: Press / Launch Roundup
Purpose:
collect coverage, reactions, and next-step links in one place.

### Type 5: Community And Event Note
Purpose:
announce screenings, AMAs, watch-alongs, or Discord events.

## Recommended Structure Per Edition
1. Strong opening sentence.
2. Main update paragraph.
3. One supporting block: image, quote, still, clip, or note.
4. One clear CTA.
5. Brief closing line that signals what's next.

## Subject Line Strategy
- keep it clean and specific
- avoid over-cleverness that hides the actual update
- use the project name regularly for recognition

Examples:
- `WE OUT HERE is live`
- `First look: WE OUT HERE`
- `The trailer for WE OUT HERE is here`
- `Why WE OUT HERE had to be made now`
- `Festival news for WE OUT HERE`

## Launch Sequence

### Welcome Email
Send immediately after signup.

Include:
- one-paragraph introduction to the series
- what subscribers should expect
- one primary link to the website or latest asset

### Launch Letter
Send on major reveal day.

Include:
- editorial framing
- major asset link
- one image or still
- CTA to watch, share, or RSVP

### Launch Follow-Up
Send 3-5 days later.

Include:
- strongest response so far
- press or quote roundup
- reminder of next milestone

## Personalization Logic
- audience segment gets the most emotionally resonant framing
- industry segment gets cleaner milestone and market context
- collaborators segment gets more operationally useful calls to action when relevant

## Content Boundaries
- do not over-send
- do not dump every small update into the inbox
- do not use clickbait subject lines
- do not make the newsletter a generic marketing funnel

## Metrics
- signup conversion rate
- open rate
- click-through rate
- unsubscribe rate
- forward/share rate
- site visits from newsletter
- event or screening conversions from newsletter

## Suggested First Six Editions
1. `Why WE OUT HERE, why now`
2. `First look at the series`
3. `Meet the world of WE OUT HERE`
4. `Trailer / teaser launch`
5. `Launch-week roundup`
6. `What comes next`
