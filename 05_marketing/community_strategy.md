## Community Objective
Build a small but high-quality audience community that feels intentional, informed, and useful to the project's growth, while preserving the series' premium identity and preventing tone drift.

## Community Thesis
`WE OUT HERE` should build a community of people who feel culturally aligned with the show, not a mass audience assembled through generic engagement tactics.

## Primary Community Functions
- retain high-intent supporters between public launch moments
- convert social interest into deeper affiliation
- create a controlled channel for updates, events, and calls to action
- give collaborators and advocates a place to stay informed
- generate a small amount of audience intelligence about what resonates

## Primary Hub
Discord is the primary community layer, but it should be treated as a curated announcement-and-conversation space, not a freeform chaos pit.

## Community Audience Segments
- early fans who want closer access to updates
- collaborators and friends-of-project
- press-adjacent and industry-adjacent supporters
- creative-tech and comedy communities who recognize the show's social world

## Community Promise
Members should get:
- earlier or fuller context than public socials provide
- direct updates on launch moments and events
- occasional behind-the-scenes material
- a tone-consistent environment with moderation and boundaries

## What The Community Is Not
- not a place to crowdwrite the show
- not a substitute writers' room
- not a space for unmoderated discourse spirals
- not a fan-labor machine
- not a replacement for the website or newsletter

## Discord Structure Recommendation
- `#welcome`
- `#announcements`
- `#about-the-project`
- `#watch-read-listen` or cultural references channel
- `#general`
- `#events`
- `#questions`
- moderator-only operations channels as needed

## Announcement Strategy

### Discord Announcements
Use announcements for:
- teaser and trailer drops
- festival selections
- screening details
- newsletter publication
- major press hits
- community events or AMAs
- major recruiting or collaborator opportunities when appropriate

### Announcement Writing Formula
1. What happened.
2. Why it matters.
3. What to do next.

Example structure:
- "Trailer is live."
- "This is our clearest look yet at the tone and world of `WE OUT HERE`."
- "Watch here and forward it to the friend who has definitely tried to turn a chat into a company."

## Participation Model

### Allowed Energy
- smart conversation
- reaction sharing
- references, comps, and discussion
- event participation
- respectful Q&A

### Discouraged Energy
- spammy self-promo
- speculative parasocial behavior
- constant requests for unreleased material
- derailing discourse about real-world tech politics unrelated to the project

## Moderation Principles
- be clear early
- enforce lightly but consistently
- protect tone and psychological safety
- keep announcements clean and high-value
- do not let irony become an excuse for hostility

## Community Programming
- launch-day live chat or watch-along if appropriate
- periodic AMA with creators or team members
- reference drops: comps, influences, music, stills
- milestone recaps after festivals, reveals, or screenings
- newsletter discussion prompts in a contained format

## Relationship To Newsletter
- newsletter is for editorial continuity and wider reach
- Discord is for immediacy and membership feeling
- every newsletter should be announced in Discord
- selective Discord moments can be recapped in the newsletter to encourage crossover

## Relationship To Social
- social drives discovery
- Discord converts interest into belonging
- Discord members can become early amplifiers, but they should never be pressured into unpaid street-team behavior

## Growth Strategy

### Phase 1
Invite close supporters, collaborators, and aligned early audience.

### Phase 2
Open community selectively via social and newsletter.

### Phase 3
Use major launch beats to drive signups, while preserving moderation quality.

## Metrics
- join rate from social and newsletter
- announcement click-through rate
- event attendance
- active member percentage
- moderation burden
- community-driven referral quality

## Risks And Controls

### Risk: The community gets too broad too early
Control:
grow in stages and keep onboarding intentional.

### Risk: The tone becomes messy or hostile
Control:
set rules early and moderate consistently.

### Risk: The community replaces canonical channels
Control:
keep website and newsletter as the primary source of formal information.

### Risk: Members expect excessive access
Control:
state boundaries clearly and repeat them when needed.

## Recommended Initial Rules Copy
- Be sharp, not hostile.
- Keep discussion relevant to the project and the culture around it.
- No harassment, dogpiling, or private-info speculation.
- No spam or self-promo without permission.
- Respect release timing and unreleased material boundaries.
