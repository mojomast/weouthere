## Launch Goal
Introduce `WE OUT HERE` to the market as a premium, timely, internet-literate series with enough specificity to attract viewers, enough polish to attract buyers and festivals, and enough momentum to attract collaborators and press.

## Launch Thesis
The launch should not feel like a generic entertainment campaign. It should feel like a culturally aware project arriving with authorship, sharp timing, and a coherent point of view.

## Launch Structure
The campaign should move through four public stages:

1. Premise recognition
2. Credibility and curiosity
3. Major public reveal
4. Sustained conversation

## Phase 0: Pre-Launch Infrastructure

### Objective
Make sure every public signal routes to a polished destination.

### Required Before Public Activity
- website live with synopsis, contact path, and newsletter signup
- platform bios aligned
- visual identity and teaser stills ready
- copy bank prepared for all core channels
- launch-day press list and outreach language ready
- Discord announcement templates ready
- newsletter welcome flow drafted

## Phase 1: Quiet Signal Build

### Timing
4-6 weeks before major reveal

### Objective
Warm the audience without overexposing the project before proof assets are ready.

### Tactics
- publish identity-setting posts: title, tonal phrase, project category, selected stills
- tease the premise through one-line hooks and visual fragments
- begin collecting newsletter signups
- soft-share with close collaborators, friendly press, and tastemakers

### Primary Messages
- a dark sitcom / tech psychocomedy is coming
- it understands digital work life from the inside
- the project has taste and control

### Outputs
- 6-10 social posts
- 1 introductory newsletter
- 1 Discord server welcome announcement
- lightweight outreach to close network

## Phase 2: Credibility Build

### Timing
2-4 weeks before trailer or first major public drop

### Objective
Add proof of seriousness and movement.

### Tactics
- publish team announcements, selected process notes, or look development
- share audience-facing framing and collaborator-facing legitimacy signals
- release first quote cards or micro-clips if available
- seed direct outreach to select journalists, critics, programmers, and industry contacts

### Primary Messages
- this project is real and moving
- the creative identity is specific
- the team knows exactly what show this is

### Outputs
- 1-2 LinkedIn milestone posts
- 4-6 social assets across Instagram, X, Bluesky, TikTok
- 1 newsletter edition
- direct outreach wave to high-priority contacts

## Phase 3: Major Reveal

### Timing
Launch week

### Objective
Create the largest coordinated attention spike of the campaign.

### Core Reveal Asset Options
- teaser
- trailer
- festival announcement
- first-look stills and poster
- premiere or screening announcement

### Reveal-Day Sequence
1. Website updates first with canonical materials and signup CTA.
2. Newsletter goes live to existing list with the editorial framing and primary link.
3. Discord announcement posts immediately after newsletter.
4. X and Bluesky publish the sharpest premise-led version.
5. Instagram publishes the most visual and premium-facing asset.
6. TikTok publishes the most recognition-led clip version.
7. LinkedIn publishes the milestone framing for industry and collaborators.
8. Press outreach begins as soon as the public landing page is live.

### Day-One Content Package
- 1 hero post per platform
- 1 press email wave
- 1 newsletter edition
- 1 Discord announcement
- 1 website homepage refresh or news entry
- 1 internal partner-forward update for close collaborators

### Reveal-Day Messaging Split

#### Audience Version
Lead with the premise, recognition, and tonal hook.

#### Press Version
Lead with why the show is timely, distinct, and worth covering.

#### Buyer / Collaborator Version
Lead with the market-ready positioning, format, and evidence of response.

## Phase 4: Launch Week Sustain

### Objective
Avoid the common mistake of spending everything on day one.

### Sustain Schedule
- Day 2: quote card or review line
- Day 3: short clip or creator framing post
- Day 4: stills carousel or character-forward asset
- Day 5: newsletter recap and next-step CTA
- Day 6: community-facing Discord activation
- Day 7: press roundup or thank-you thread

### Sustain Content Types
- quote cards
- character intros
- behind-the-scenes fragments
- creator notes
- critical responses
- audience reactions

## Phase 5: Ongoing Conversation

### Objective
Convert launch awareness into durable attention.

### Tactics
- continue weekly content rhythm
- publish milestone follow-ups
- spotlight team, craft, and process selectively
- sequence new clips and social proof across subsequent weeks
- maintain newsletter and Discord cadence

## Core Audiences By Launch Stage

| Stage | Primary Audience | Secondary Audience | Main Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quiet Signal Build | collaborators, tastemakers | early audience | establish tone |
| Credibility Build | press, collaborators, industry | early audience | prove seriousness |
| Major Reveal | digital-native viewers, press | buyers, festivals | concentrate attention |
| Launch Week Sustain | viewers, community | press, collaborators | extend momentum |
| Ongoing Conversation | subscribers, community | new viewers | retain and grow |

## Asset Checklist
- teaser trailer
- cutdown clips for TikTok, Instagram, X
- poster / key art
- stills set
- character cards
- quote cards
- synopsis block
- creator statement
- press outreach copy
- Discord announcement copy
- newsletter launch letter

## Launch Metrics
- website traffic during reveal window
- newsletter signup conversion rate
- trailer view count and completion rate
- press pickup volume
- social engagement by platform
- Discord joins and click-throughs
- meeting requests from collaborators, festivals, and buyers

## Launch Roles
- marketing_agent: overall sequencing and message discipline
- social_content_agent: platform execution and scheduling
- press_agent: media outreach and press responses
- web_agent: landing page updates and conversion paths
- publicity_recruiting_agent: collaborator-forward messaging as needed

## Failure Modes To Avoid
- revealing too much before assets are strong enough
- using the same copy on every platform
- forgetting the industry/collaborator audience during audience-facing pushes
- driving traffic to a weak landing page
- treating launch as one post instead of a sequence

## Recommended Cadence Summary
1. Warm-up for 4-6 weeks.
2. Build credibility for 2-4 weeks.
3. Coordinate a major reveal across all channels in one day.
4. Sustain with daily posts for 7 days.
5. Shift to weekly rhythm for at least 6 weeks after reveal.
