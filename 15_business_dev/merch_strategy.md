## Content

### Merch Objective

Merch for `WE OUT HERE` should function as both revenue and audience signaling. The right items should feel like artifacts from a failed startup, a group chat with delusions of legitimacy, or a prestige-comedy object that a culture-literate viewer would actually wear or display.

### Core Merch Thesis

The audience is unlikely to respond to lazy logo merch alone. They are more likely to buy items that feel:

- in-world
- dry and specific
- office-coded or platform-coded
- wearable without looking like a generic TV promo item
- limited enough to feel discovered rather than mass-market

### Brand And Canon Guardrails

- Use the show title as `WE OUT HERE`.
- Any in-world product language can reference `Backchannel` or `Cataclysm Consulting` as fictional entities.
- Do not brand fictional community materials as Discord merchandise; Discord may only appear as a real-world external community channel outside the fiction.
- Keep the tone deadpan, sharp, and specific. Avoid broad novelty humor.

### Product Strategy

### Tier 1: Core Low-Risk Items

These should launch first because they are operationally simple and easy to test.

- t-shirt with minimal `WE OUT HERE` lockup
- cap or beanie with `Cataclysm Consulting`
- mug with faux `Backchannel` admin copy
- tote bag with internal-memo style design
- sticker pack based on statuses, alerts, or moderation language

Why these work:
- low setup complexity
- can be sold print-on-demand or in short runs
- good entry-point pricing

### Tier 2: In-World Office Objects

These differentiate the line from standard screen-project merch.

- branded notebook or legal pad
- employee-style ID badge and lanyard
- desk tent card or "visitor" placard
- mouse pad or keyboard cloth
- water bottle or thermos with pseudo-corporate copy

Why these work:
- strong audience fit with knowledge-work and desk-culture habits
- turns the show's satire into usable objects

### Tier 3: Premium Limited Drops

These should only launch after the audience shows real engagement.

- heavyweight hoodie with subtle chest hit and dense back graphic
- varsity-style jacket or overshirt for a collaboration drop
- framed faux `Backchannel` compliance notice or manifesto print
- art-booklet zine with character artifacts, deck fragments, and interface ephemera

Why these work:
- higher margin potential
- stronger press and influencer pickup
- supports collaboration partners

### Design Languages To Explore

### 1. Faux Corporate Minimalism

Looks like a company that wants to seem competent.

- monochrome palettes
- tiny text blocks
- employee-swag realism
- dry compliance or onboarding language

### 2. Platform Artifact Graphics

Looks like exported remnants from `Backchannel`.

- status badges
- moderation notices
- permission labels
- channel taxonomy and alert language

### 3. Prestige-Comedy Cultural Object

Looks like a limited-run art/fashion item rather than TV merch.

- understated typography
- one high-concept phrase or visual motif
- premium blanks and restrained colorways

### Recommended Initial SKU Mix

Launch with `5-7` SKUs only.

Recommended first wave:
- `WE OUT HERE` tee
- `Cataclysm Consulting` cap
- `Backchannel` mug
- sticker pack
- notebook or legal pad
- one premium hoodie if preorders justify it

This is enough to test demand without spreading design and fulfillment effort too thin.

### Price Bands

Working price targets:

- stickers: `$8-$15`
- mugs: `$20-$28`
- totes: `$22-$32`
- tees: `$32-$48`
- caps: `$34-$45`
- notebooks / pads: `$16-$28`
- hoodies: `$68-$110`

Prices should reflect design and object quality. Cheap pricing will make the line feel promotional instead of desirable.

### Fulfillment Approach

### Phase 1: Low-Risk Fulfillment

- use print-on-demand or short-run vendor for apparel basics
- use one fulfillment partner where possible
- hold inventory only on high-confidence items

### Phase 2: Hybrid Fulfillment

- pre-order premium drops
- batch-produce small office-object runs
- reserve collaboration items for event or timed exclusives

### Merch Drops By Campaign Moment

### Development / Early Awareness

- small internal-looking drop
- focus on stickers, notebook, cap, tote
- objective: signal taste and test audience appetite

### Trailer / Release Window

- hero apparel item
- bundle with event tickets or digital bonuses
- timed drop tied to clips, press, or screenings

### Post-Launch / Community Phase

- deeper in-world references
- character-quote or episode-themed drops if specific language breaks out organically
- collaboration capsule with partner brand

### Bundling Ideas

- screening ticket + tote + sticker pack
- hoodie + zine pre-order bundle
- newsletter or community member early access code
- limited partner bundle for event attendees

### Merch Copy Direction

Product descriptions should read like smart, slightly overbuilt internal documentation, not generic e-commerce hype.

Good qualities:
- deadpan
- precise
- mildly absurd
- culturally fluent

Bad qualities:
- loud joke-shirt energy
- ironic filler copy
- vague brand language about disruption or hustle

### Revenue Logic

Merch should be treated as a layered business line:

- direct margin from sales
- audience acquisition through visible wearables and desk objects
- sponsor and collaborator inventory
- event bundle upsell
- proof of fandom intensity for future buyers or partners

### Risks And Mitigations

### Risk: Merch feels too inside-baseball

Mitigation:
- keep at least half the launch line visually appealing even if the buyer does not know every reference

### Risk: Too many SKUs too early

Mitigation:
- launch with a narrow assortment and review sell-through before expansion

### Risk: Cheap production undermines the brand

Mitigation:
- prioritize fewer items with better blanks, print quality, and packaging

### Risk: Rights or platform confusion

Mitigation:
- keep fictional `Backchannel` branding clearly original and avoid resemblance to specific real-world trademarks beyond genre-level platform familiarity

### KPIs

- sell-through rate by SKU
- gross margin by item
- preorder conversion rate
- attach rate to events or community offers
- repeat purchase rate
- organic social posting and earned visibility

### Immediate Next Actions

- develop first-wave creative concepts for `5-7` SKUs
- identify one low-risk fulfillment vendor and one premium collaboration vendor
- map launch timing against website and community rollout
- define approval guardrails for in-world copy and graphic treatments
