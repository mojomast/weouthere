### Partnership Objective

`WE OUT HERE` should pursue partnerships that do at least one of the following:

- put the project in front of digitally native, culture-literate viewers
- signal taste and legitimacy to press, talent, and industry buyers
- create sponsor-safe but distinctive activations tied to work culture, internet behavior, and comedy
- generate owned audience growth that can compound into launch and post-launch value

The core filter is simple: every partner should make the show feel sharper, more current, or more credible. If a partner only adds generic reach, it is likely the wrong fit.

### Audience Fit

Best-fit partners will understand audiences who are:

- online-native but skeptical of corporate internet-speak
- adjacent to tech, design, media, consulting, startup, and creator economies
- attracted to irony, cultural specificity, and premium-comedy framing
- likely to share clips, references, memes, and language-based moments

### Partnership Principles

- Keep the title presented as `WE OUT HERE` in all external materials.
- Use `Backchannel` for any in-world community or interface storytelling; do not refer to the fictional platform as Discord.
- Avoid partnerships that require the show to become pro-tech propaganda, anti-tech preaching, or generic founder satire.
- Favor partners that can support editorial, experiential, or community value over simple logo placement.
- Build packages that scale from development-stage relationships to launch-stage activations.
- Protect tone: the comedy is sharp, internet-literate, and psychologically grounded, not broad brand comedy.

### Priority Partnership Categories

### 1. Culture And Editorial Partners

Targets:
- digital culture publications
- internet and media newsletters
- comedy podcasts
- creator economy and work-culture podcasts
- art-house or prestige-TV adjacent publications

Why they fit:
- they can frame the show intelligently for early adopters
- they create quoteable, high-context coverage rather than flattening the premise
- they over-index on tastemaker audiences who influence broader discovery

Possible activations:
- creator interviews about work culture, AI language, and screenlife storytelling
- annotated scene or script excerpts
- character dossier drops framed as editorial artifacts from the `Backchannel` world
- preview newsletter exclusives or clip premieres

### 2. Comedy And Live Event Partners

Targets:
- alt-comedy venues
- comedy festivals
- culture festivals with media programming
- screening series in creative-tech cities

Why they fit:
- the project benefits from communal laughter and quote circulation
- live events can prove audience response before a full release campaign

Possible activations:
- script reading or staged scene performance
- pilot preview plus creator Q&A
- panel on digital labor anxiety and comedy craft
- themed networking event framed as a fake `Cataclysm Consulting` recruiting night

### 3. Tech-Culture But Non-Corporate Partners

Targets:
- design conferences
- independent tech newsletters
- future-of-work communities
- creative-tool companies with strong cultural taste

Why they fit:
- they speak to the industry's adjacent audience without forcing enterprise messaging
- they can amplify the show's specificity if handled as cultural commentary rather than product endorsement

Possible activations:
- sponsored salon on work, burnout, and platform behavior
- branded microscreening for members or subscribers
- co-published essay or conversation around internet language and performance

### 4. Fashion, Object, And Lifestyle Partners

Targets:
- streetwear labels
- niche office-object brands
- stationery and desk brands
- design-forward beverage or snack brands

Why they fit:
- the audience likes artifacts, uniforms, and in-jokes
- merch-adjacent partnerships can monetize taste while building world texture

Possible activations:
- limited-run collaboration capsule
- launch-night gifting and props crossover
- faux-corporate objects from the `Backchannel` / `Cataclysm Consulting` ecosystem

### 5. Community Platform Partners

Targets:
- newsletter platforms
- membership/community software tools
- event ticketing or live-stream partners

Why they fit:
- they can help acquire and organize the early audience in measurable ways
- they support the external community layer without confusing it with the fictional `Backchannel` platform

Possible activations:
- gated community pre-release access
- event RSVPs and member-only discussions
- campaign analytics and audience segmentation support

### High-Priority Partnership Ideas

### Idea 1: Tastemaker Media Rollout

Build a small launch circuit with 3-5 editorial partners that each receive a different angle: creator interview, clip premiere, culture essay, cast conversation, or production diary.

Value:
- controlled message discipline
- layered discovery across adjacent audiences
- press proof for larger buyers and sponsors

### Idea 2: `Backchannel Leak` Campaign

Create a partnership-friendly teaser format where selected outlets receive fictional `Backchannel` artifacts: onboarding copy, moderation drama, consultancy decks, or crisis screenshots.

Value:
- highly shareable
- tone-accurate worldbuilding
- lets partners publish assets that feel native to the series

Guardrail:
- package clearly as in-world fiction so it is legible and legally safe

### Idea 3: Work-Culture Comedy Night

Partner with a venue, comedy organizer, or festival to host a screening and panel around burnout, digital labor, and pseudo-expertise.

Value:
- turns the show's themes into a live social experience
- creates sponsorship inventory without awkward product placement inside the narrative

### Idea 4: Limited Office-Core Merch Collaboration

Partner with a design-forward brand on a tight capsule: hat, mug, notepad, tote, lanyard, desk card, or hoodie inspired by `Cataclysm Consulting` or `Backchannel` admin culture.

Value:
- monetizable with low SKU count
- deepens fan identity
- provides sponsor-friendly physical touchpoints

### Idea 5: Creator-Economy Newsletter Bundle

Offer partner newsletters a package of exclusive assets, affiliate merch codes, and event access in exchange for list placements and editorial treatment.

Value:
- measurable conversion
- useful before and after release
- strong fit for industry-adjacent audiences

### Partnership Packages

### Package A: Awareness Partner

Best for media and newsletters.

Includes:
- early access materials
- exclusive quote or still
- linkback to website and signup funnel
- optional affiliate code for ticket or merch sales

### Package B: Event Partner

Best for venues, festivals, and community operators.

Includes:
- co-branded screening or panel
- event toolkit and visuals
- creator or cast participation if available
- community capture via email or external Discord invite

### Package C: Product Collaboration Partner

Best for design, apparel, desk-object, and beverage brands.

Includes:
- limited-edition product rights for a defined period
- co-branded campaign assets
- launch event tie-in
- revenue share or minimum guarantee

### Package D: Strategic Sponsor

Best for aligned tools or platforms.

Includes:
- category exclusivity if pricing justifies it
- presence across events, owned media, and community touchpoints
- branded but taste-protected content series
- post-campaign reporting

### What To Avoid

- major enterprise sponsors that require softened satire or approval over creative framing
- crypto, gambling, or extractive-growth categories unless explicitly re-evaluated later
- partnerships that rely on confusing the fictional `Backchannel` with a real product
- partners who want meme aesthetics without respecting the audience's intelligence
- broad consumer brands with no relevance to work, screens, comedy, or culture

### Outreach Priorities

### Phase 1: Credibility

- target tastemaker press, comedy, and culture partners
- secure 2-3 editorial relationships and 1 live-event relationship

### Phase 2: Conversion

- add newsletter, community, and event operators that can drive signups and attendance
- test affiliate and tracked-link packages

### Phase 3: Revenue

- add limited product collaborations and launch sponsors
- negotiate bundles that combine events, merch, and community access

### Basic Evaluation Criteria

Score every partner `1-5` against:

- audience overlap
- tone compatibility
- editorial or cultural credibility
- measurable conversion potential
- revenue upside
- operational complexity

Only pursue partners with strong scores in audience overlap and tone compatibility. Revenue alone is not enough.

### Success Metrics

- qualified email signups or community joins
- event RSVPs and attendance
- merch or ticket conversion by partner code
- earned media quality, not just volume
- inbound partnership and talent interest after activation
- repeat collaborations from high-fit partners
