## Content

### Sponsorship Objective

Sponsors should help fund launch activity, events, community, and ancillary campaigns around `WE OUT HERE` while preserving the series as a culturally credible comedy rather than a branded-content object.

### High-Level Rule

If the sponsor makes the audience ask "why is this brand here?" the fit is probably weak. If the sponsor feels native to work culture, digital behavior, comedy audiences, or design-forward lifestyle behavior, the fit is more plausible.

### Sponsor Fit Matrix

### Strong Fit Categories

#### Design-Forward Productivity Or Desk Brands

Examples:
- notebooks
- desk accessories
- headphones
- ergonomic but style-aware work products

Why they fit:
- natural overlap with the show's work and screen environment
- easy event and merch bundle integration

#### Beverage Brands With Cultural Taste

Examples:
- coffee
- canned tea
- adaptogen or energy-adjacent drinks if brand tone is credible

Why they fit:
- obvious event utility
- good for screenings, green rooms, and community activations

#### Creative Tools And Media Platforms

Examples:
- editing, writing, note-taking, design, or publishing tools
- newsletter or membership platforms

Why they fit:
- audience adjacency
- useful for co-branded conversations or workshops

#### Fashion Or Lifestyle Labels With Internet Credibility

Examples:
- streetwear
- accessories
- niche basics labels

Why they fit:
- supports capsule drops and launch events
- helps the project read as culture, not just content

### Conditional Fit Categories

#### Consumer Tech Hardware

Can work if:
- the sponsor is used mainly in event, lounge, or experiential settings
- the activation does not suggest the show is endorsing a worldview it satirizes

Risk:
- can easily become generic and expensive without adding taste value

#### Recruiting, Learning, Or Career Platforms

Can work if:
- framed around work culture conversation, not hustle propaganda

Risk:
- tonal hypocrisy if the brand language is too earnest or extractive

#### Telecom Or Infrastructure Brands

Can work if:
- limited to broad campaign support, not creative integration

Risk:
- low cultural specificity and weak audience affection

### Weak Or High-Risk Categories

#### Enterprise SaaS

Why weak:
- usually too generic
- often requires safe messaging and category jargon
- risks flattening the satire into a sales environment

#### Big Tech Platform Branding

Why risky:
- the audience may read it as ideological capture
- can confuse the fiction, especially around the `Backchannel` world

#### Crypto, Web3, Gambling, And Get-Rich-Quick Categories

Why risky:
- trust erosion
- weak long-term brand safety
- too easy to make the show feel cynical rather than sharp

#### Pharma Or Sensitive Regulated Categories

Why risky:
- high approval overhead
- poor tonal fit for comedy-led activations

### Integration Rules

### 1. Keep Sponsors Mostly Outside The Narrative

Primary sponsor value should come from:
- events
- community programming
- merch collaborations
- newsletter and website support
- branded editorial or conversation formats

Not from:
- awkward plot insertion
- character endorsement behavior
- product dialogue that breaks realism

### 2. Protect The Fictional World

- `Backchannel` remains the in-world platform
- sponsor assets should not imply that a real sponsor owns, powers, or replaces `Backchannel`
- any in-world-style asset built with a sponsor should be clearly campaign material, not accidental canon

### 3. Protect The Audience Relationship

- sponsorship language should be concise and transparent
- prefer labels like "presented with support from" over inflated partnership jargon
- do not let sponsor copy infect creator voice or community tone

### Recommended Sponsor Products

### Product A: Event Support

Includes:
- sponsor presence at screenings or panels
- sampling, gifting, or utility placement
- logo on event page and recap materials

Best for:
- beverage brands
- desk-product brands
- hardware brands

### Product B: Community Support

Includes:
- sponsor-supported AMA or workshop
- perks for Discord members or newsletter subscribers
- tracked conversions and post-event report

Best for:
- creative tools
- membership platforms
- media partners

### Product C: Merch Collaboration

Includes:
- limited-edition drop
- co-branded packaging
- revenue share or sponsor underwriting

Best for:
- fashion
- stationery
- desk objects

### Product D: Editorial Series Underwriter

Includes:
- sponsorship of a short-run interview, essay, or clip-discussion series
- tasteful sponsor crediting only

Best for:
- cultural or creator-economy brands with editorial patience

### Deal Filters

Before advancing any sponsor, confirm:

- audience overlap is real
- the sponsor can work with limited and tasteful branding
- no demand for creative approval over story or canon
- legal and reputational risk are manageable
- there is a measurable conversion or revenue case

### Suggested Approval Questions

- would a core fan think this sponsor makes sense here?
- would a press or industry reader see this as additive or embarrassing?
- does the sponsor want adjacency to the show's intelligence, or do they want to sand it down?
- does the package earn enough to justify operational effort?

### Metrics

- sponsor revenue by category
- event attendance and conversion by sponsor-supported activation
- community or newsletter growth attributable to sponsor campaigns
- earned-media lift from sponsor-backed moments
- audience sentiment and backlash signals

### Red Flags In Negotiation

- requests for script review or story control
- insistence on heavy logo visibility inside creative assets
- sanitized brand tone requirements that flatten the project voice
- vague promises of reach with no measurable deliverables
- category conflict with higher-value future partners

### Immediate Next Actions

- create a target list by category with `strong`, `conditional`, and `avoid` labels
- draft a lightweight sponsor deck with example packages
- define minimum pricing for event, community, and merch support
- align legal review process for sponsor approvals and rights language
