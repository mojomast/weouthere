## Content

### Channel Clarification

This document concerns a real external `Discord` server used for marketing, audience retention, events, and community management.

Inside the fiction, the show's chat platform is `Backchannel`. Community copy should make this distinction cleanly so fans understand that:

- `Backchannel` is the fictional story-world platform
- `Discord` is the real community tool for audience engagement

### Community Objective

The Discord server should help `WE OUT HERE`:

- convert passive interest into repeat attention
- gather early superfans, culture workers, and industry-adjacent advocates
- create a home for screenings, AMAs, drops, and announcements
- surface audience language, references, and organic reactions that inform marketing
- support launch without feeling like a desperate growth funnel

### Community Thesis

The right Discord for this project is not a chaotic open chatroom and not a corporate announcement channel. It should feel like a well-moderated selective clubhouse for people who are genuinely aligned with the show's taste: digitally native, comedy-forward, and industry-adjacent.

### Audience Segments Inside The Server

- early fans who want behind-the-scenes access
- comedy and culture followers who share clips and references
- creative-tech and media workers who relate to the show's themes
- potential partners, talent, and press lurking for signal
- event attendees and merch buyers who want continued connection

### Community Principles

- prioritize quality of conversation over raw member count
- keep channels legible and low-clutter
- post enough to prove life, not so much that the server becomes work to follow
- reward participation with context, access, and artifacts rather than empty gamification
- maintain a clear line between in-world play and actual community operations

### Recommended Server Role

The server should sit between newsletter, fan club, and event hub.

Primary functions:
- announcements and release moments
- screenings and live chat events
- creator Q&A and AMA sessions
- merch and drop early access
- community discussion around work culture, internet comedy, and media

### Recommended Channel Structure

### Start Here

- `#welcome`
- `#rules`
- `#announcements`
- `#how-this-relates-to-backchannel`

The last channel should explain the fiction/reality distinction in one short pinned note.

### Main Community

- `#general`
- `#episodes-and-clips`
- `#work-culture-nightmare-rotation`
- `#comedy-and-media`
- `#fan-artifacts`

### Access And Commerce

- `#events`
- `#merch-drops`
- `#partner-offers`
- `#support-and-questions`

### Temporary Event Channels

- screening-specific text channel
- AMA stage or voice room
- partner event room when relevant

### Roles

- admins
- moderators
- founding members
- event attendees
- press / industry optional role by manual assignment
- bots hidden from community hierarchy where possible

Do not overcomplicate the role system early. Roles should signal access and responsibility, not fake status ladders.

### Content Cadence

### Weekly Baseline

- `1-2` announcement or editorial posts
- `2-3` conversation prompts or artifact drops
- active moderation and replies

### Monthly Rhythm

- one notable event: AMA, screening, listening room, table read excerpt, or partner session
- one exclusive asset or merch/community perk

### Launch Windows

- increased cadence around trailers, premieres, festival dates, and drops
- use scheduled posts so activity feels intentional rather than frantic

### Content Types That Fit

- annotated stills or frames
- script fragments or production notes cleared for sharing
- fake `Backchannel` artifacts clearly labeled as in-world material
- polls on merch, events, or favorite lines
- watch-party prompts
- curated discussion topics around burnout, internet language, and work performance

### Moderation Model

The server should be actively moderated from day one.

Minimum moderation rules:
- no hate speech, harassment, doxxing, or targeted hostility
- no spam, self-promo flooding, or AI slop posting
- no pressure for creators or cast to disclose private development details
- no confusing fan speculation with official canon
- spoilers contained to marked channels during release windows

Operational rule:
- moderators should intervene quickly, quietly, and consistently

### Tone Of Moderation

The moderation voice should be direct, adult, and lightly witty when appropriate. It should not perform fake corporate friendliness or hostile irony.

### Growth Strategy

### Entry Sources

- website CTA
- newsletter invites
- event QR codes
- merch inserts and checkout page
- selective partner co-promotions
- social posts tied to drops or screenings

### Growth Tactic

Use intentional invites rather than blasting the server everywhere. Better to have a smaller server with strong retention than a large dead server.

### Incentives To Join

- early event access
- limited merch preorder windows
- member-only clips or assets
- Q&A priority submissions
- visible access to creator notes and production updates when appropriate

### Measurement

Track:
- total joins and invite-source attribution
- 30-day active members
- event attendance from Discord
- merch conversion from Discord members
- message quality and channel health, not just post volume
- churn after major campaign beats

### Risks And Mitigations

### Risk: Server becomes inactive

Mitigation:
- keep channels limited, schedule recurring prompts, and anchor activity to real events and drops

### Risk: Server becomes too insider-industry and alienates fans

Mitigation:
- balance creator/process talk with audience-facing fun and cultural conversation

### Risk: Confusion between `Backchannel` and Discord

Mitigation:
- include a persistent orientation note and avoid sloppy naming in posts

### Risk: Moderation burden grows too fast

Mitigation:
- start invite-controlled, appoint mods early, and document escalation rules

### Launch Recommendation

Open the Discord only when there is enough programming to support it for at least eight weeks. Minimum launch package:

- welcome copy and rules
- three seeded discussion channels
- first event announced
- first exclusive asset ready
- named moderators in place
