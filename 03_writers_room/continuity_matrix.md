## Carryover Obligations

### From Episode 1 to Episode 2

- The leak should remain known internally and semi-known externally; it cannot vanish.
- HelixBell's positive response to harm creates Cataclysm's dangerous confidence.
- twinkle's draft addiction and screenshot paranoia should continue.

### From Episode 2 to Episode 3

- The coercive language developed for KindRoot should seed later automation vocabulary.
- bl0ng's compliance UX tools become a prototype base for future control systems.
- ariane carries guilt from failing to stop policy rollout.

### From Episode 3 to Episode 4

- Leadership's faith in twinkle's manic method leads directly to need for external containment.
- bl0ng's experience writing in twinkle's voice informs later automation/impersonation dynamics.
- Ariane's failure to intervene should sharpen her urgency but not fully free her.

### From Episode 4 to Episode 5

- Hidden visibility in Backchannel rooms must make the award nomination even more obscene.
- The consultant's optics framework helps explain why Cataclysm looks ethical from outside.
- twinkle should feel both craving and disgust toward institutional approval.

### From Episode 5 to Episode 6

- Former worker evidence becomes part of the consequence pipeline in finale.
- bl0ng's live-edit reflex evolves into full automation confidence.
- Ariane's speech crisis foreshadows her partial leak choice.

## Logic Checks

- Backchannel is fictional and should consistently function as a hybrid of workplace chat, stakeholder visibility layer, and accidental confessional archive.
- Client names should sound plausible, slightly cursed, and satirically specific.
- No episode should resolve the moral issue it raises; at most, it should redistribute cost.
- Surreal inserts must emerge from stress, shame, manic cognition, or platform logic, not random whimsy.

## Continuity Questions To Track During Drafts

2. Which pieces of coercive language have already entered the company's reusable vocabulary?
3. Is bl0ng's level of access justified by prior scenes?
4. What concrete evidence does ariane have at this stage?
5. What object, phrase, or UI element visually carries the prior episode into this one?
