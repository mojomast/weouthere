# 03_writers_room/episode_beat_sheets.md

**Purpose:** Provide season-wide beat sheets for all six episodes, organized by act, story lane, theme, and psychodynamic function.

**Owner/Subagent:** OpenCode

**Status:** Draft v1

**Dependencies:** `03_writers_room/scene_cards.md`, `03_writers_room/defense_mechanisms_tracker.md`, `03_writers_room/continuity_matrix.md`, `03_writers_room/emotional_arc_map.md`, `03_writers_room/writers_room_rules.md`

## Season Spine

Season arc progression: mania -> fragile success -> ethical drift -> containment failure -> ironic collapse -> exposure to consequences.

Series engine: every problem begins as an attempt to control perception, labor, risk, or shame; every fix becomes a larger leak. Endings preserve instability rather than restore order.

## Episode 1: Don't Send That

**Theme:** If you can edit the message, you can avoid the feeling.

**Psychodynamic function:** Introduces manic denial as team operating system. The pilot establishes Cataclysm Consulting as a workplace built from compensatory performance, fantasy control, and collective avoidance.

**A Story:** A late-night Backchannel message intended as private triage leaks to a client-facing space and must be contained.

**B Story:** bl0ng turns containment into a performative systems operation that increases visibility.

**C Story:** ariane.emory tries to impose ethical language and process, but uses abstraction to avoid directly naming harm.

### Act 1

- Cold open: twinkle.puss drafts ten versions of a Backchannel message about a nightmare client. In desktop realism, he toggles between channels, DMs, and unsent notes while a surreal insert shows the cursor pulsing like a heart monitor.
- He posts the rant into what he thinks is a private working thread. It is actually a semi-public client war room mirrored to stakeholders.
- The message is not obscene enough to be obviously terminable; it is specific enough to be catastrophic.
- At Cataclysm, the team discovers senior people from the client have already seen it but are reacting with eerie corporate politeness.
- twinkle.puss insists the wording can still be "framed," revealing his belief that tone can outrun fact.
- bl0ng proposes a fast containment architecture: recontextualize, over-message, flood the channel, and create plausible confusion.
- ariane.emory asks the only useful question, "Did we tell the truth by accident?" Nobody engages.

### Act 2

- They split into roles. twinkle.puss takes narrative ownership, bl0ng handles platform logistics on Backchannel, ariane drafts a principled response memo no one wants to send.
- bl0ng creates a false impression that the leaked message was part of a candid internal audit initiative. It almost works because the client loves process theater.
- twinkle.puss performs remorse in three different registers depending on audience: self-deprecating to peers, strategic to managers, wounded to executives.
- ariane discovers a screenshot has already escaped the platform and is circulating among contractors.
- B story escalates when bl0ng starts using burner-style internal channels to test which version of the lie plays best.
- C story deepens as ariane keeps refining the exact language of accountability while refusing to simply send a clean apology.
- Midpoint: the client says they appreciate the team's "radical candor" and asks Cataclysm to formalize the critique into a new engagement.

### Act 3

- The apparent save becomes worse: what was an error is now monetized. Cataclysm is asked to lead a "culture repair sprint" for the people they insulted.
- twinkle.puss feels euphoric because disaster has converted into importance.
- bl0ng builds dashboards tracking sentiment response to the leak and begins treating coworkers as variables inside the experiment.
- ariane objects that they are now profiting from harm they caused, but her objection is folded into meeting language as "good governance perspective."
- In a surreal insert, the leaked message duplicates across desktops like mold.
- They pitch the client on a crisis framework using the exact emotional vocabulary they cannot tolerate internally.
- End beat: after surviving the day, twinkle.puss opens a new draft addressed to someone else and types, "off the record..." Cut before send.

## Episode 2: Terms of Abuse

**Theme:** Boundaries become kink when nobody intends to honor them.

**Psychodynamic function:** Moves the team from defensive improvisation into rationalized coercion. They begin converting emotional violence into workflow language.

**A Story:** Cataclysm is asked to rewrite a client's abusive workplace policy as a humane Backchannel-first communication charter.

**B Story:** bl0ng becomes obsessed with compliance language and uses consent rhetoric as domination UX.

**C Story:** twinkle.puss and ariane disagree over whether naming abuse helps anyone if the contract requires preserving the client relationship.

### Act 1

- The client from episode 1 refers Cataclysm to a founder-led startup with a retention problem and a heavily litigated HR footprint.
- Their ask: convert punitive employee terms into a friendly communication policy for Backchannel rollout.
- twinkle.puss instantly understands the assignment is cosmetic, but he takes pride in his ability to make poison readable.
- bl0ng is delighted by the interface problem of making coercion feel opt-in.
- ariane argues the team should decline, but she does it in a way that sounds like a discussion paper rather than a refusal.

### Act 2

- The team workshops phrases like "mutual responsiveness expectations" to replace surveillance and unpaid after-hours availability.
- Scene engine: every line they sanitize becomes more revealing in subtext.
- bl0ng proposes interactive policy acknowledgments on Backchannel with escalating nudges, badges, and shame metrics.
- twinkle.puss starts improvising euphemisms so fast he stops hearing them.
- ariane interviews junior staff at the client and hears plainly abusive conditions. She returns rattled but still cannot force the room to stop.
- Midpoint: the client CEO loves ariane's notes on employee fear because they are "such rich engagement insight."

### Act 3

- The final deliverable reframes compulsory emotional availability as community care.
- twinkle.puss sees what they have done and briefly wants to sabotage the launch, but his hunger to be needed wins.
- bl0ng pilots the policy inside Cataclysm as a demo, accidentally making their own workplace more intolerable overnight.
- ariane attempts a conscience-saving appendix documenting risks, only to learn it has been moved into backup materials no one will read.
- The policy rollout succeeds by the client's standards: engagement spikes because employees are panicking in public.
- End beat: a Cataclysm contractor clicks "I agree" on the same abusive flow bl0ng designed, while the leads watch the metrics climb in silence.

## Episode 3: The Manic Defense

**Theme:** Charisma is a temporary restraining order against collapse.

**Psychodynamic function:** Centers twinkle.puss's manic overfunctioning and shows how the others become dependents and enablers inside his acceleration.

**A Story:** twinkle.puss turns a sleepless spiral into a full-company innovation sprint after sensing dwindling faith from leadership.

**B Story:** bl0ng weaponizes twinkle's manic output into prototypes without checking viability.

**C Story:** ariane tries to diagnose the pattern clinically, then realizes diagnosis itself can become another avoidance maneuver.

### Act 1

- twinkle.puss has not slept. He arrives with a dozen interconnected ideas for repositioning Cataclysm around "ambient intervention design."
- His pitch is incoherent but electrifying, and leadership gives him forty-eight hours to make it legible.
- bl0ng recognizes free kinetic energy and attaches himself to the project, promising to operationalize whatever he says.
- ariane clocks that twinkle is not okay and attempts to slow the tempo, but everyone treats concern as negativity.

### Act 2

- Cataclysm becomes a pop-up war room. Whiteboards, Backchannel channels, and deck fragments proliferate.
- twinkle.puss is at his most brilliant and least reliable. He makes true observations nobody else can make, then leaps across three bridges that do not exist.
- bl0ng turns every impulsive phrase into a product concept, creating increasing stakes around ideas that were never meant to survive daylight.
- ariane interviews twinkle under the guise of collaboration and tries to determine whether intervention is required. The conversation becomes intimate, adversarial, and evasive.
- Midpoint: a major client bites on the manic pitch, offering Cataclysm a prestige engagement contingent on immediate execution.

### Act 3

- twinkle.puss begins to crash in micro-failures: forgotten names, duplicate promises, disorganized files, erratic affect.
- bl0ng, rather than reduce exposure, keeps the machinery running by imitating his voice on Backchannel.
- ariane discovers this impersonation and is horrified both ethically and because it works.
- Leadership congratulates the trio for "breaking through the market noise." The company codifies the manic episode as a repeatable method.
- In surreal inserts, twinkle is chased by notification bubbles chanting his own language back at him.
- End beat: he finally sleeps at his desk while messages continue to send under his identity.

## Episode 4: Contain Me Daddy

**Theme:** People who beg for limits are usually asking someone else to absorb the cost.

**Psychodynamic function:** The season's containment obsession peaks. The team openly eroticizes authority, structure, and discipline because no internal self-regulation remains credible.

**A Story:** After multiple client incidents, Cataclysm hires an external risk consultant to impose controls, and the team immediately turns containment into theater.

**B Story:** bl0ng becomes infatuated with surveillance dashboards and self-discipline rituals.

**C Story:** ariane tries to make the containment process real, but her need for a coherent moral system leaves her vulnerable to capture by the consultant's language.

### Act 1

- Leadership announces a temporary risk containment initiative. The consultant arrives with a dead-eyed calm that everyone reads differently: parent, dom, auditor, executioner.
- twinkle.puss is half-defiant, half-relieved. Someone else might finally say no.
- bl0ng instantly starts optimizing for the consultant's approval.
- ariane is the only one who sees the consultant's framework is mostly optics.

### Act 2

- New rules proliferate: channel freezes, approval trees, sensitivity flags, mandatory decompression windows.
- The team eroticizes and gamifies compliance. Containment becomes style.
- twinkle.puss keeps trying to trigger a meaningful boundary and getting only polished managerial scripts.
- bl0ng secretly expands monitoring beyond the consultant's ask because he enjoys omniscience more than order.
- ariane collaborates on a serious risk map, then learns leadership only wants a presentation-ready version with no accountability hooks.
- Midpoint: a supposedly locked Backchannel incident room is revealed to have multiple shadow viewers, including clients.

### Act 3

- The consultant recommends more visibility, not less. Containment now means perpetual observation.
- twinkle.puss has a near-breakdown when he realizes being watched is replacing being held.
- bl0ng is fully in his element, defending invasive tracking as care.
- ariane confronts leadership and is praised for her courage in a meeting summary that changes nothing.
- A live test of the new containment protocol fails because too many people have permission and no one has responsibility.
- End beat: a supposedly private incident-review room lights up with fresh participants as the three leads stare at the growing viewer count.

## Episode 5: The Superego Collapse

**Theme:** Once every internal voice becomes compliance language, conscience has nowhere left to stand.

**Psychodynamic function:** The moral vocabulary of the season disintegrates. Shame, critique, and ethics all become indistinguishable from branding or procedure.

**A Story:** Cataclysm is nominated for an industry ethics award based on work that is visibly harming people.

**B Story:** twinkle.puss spirals around the possibility that he is only good when he's useful in a crisis.

**C Story:** ariane and bl0ng form an unstable alliance to stop an acceptance speech from becoming a confession or a self-mythology event.

### Act 1

- News breaks that Cataclysm is shortlisted for a high-profile ethical innovation award.
- The nomination package prominently features the crisis language from earlier episodes, stripped of context.
- twinkle.puss is insulted, delighted, disgusted, and hungry all at once.
- bl0ng treats the ceremony as an influence surface.
- ariane sees the award as the final proof that external validation can metabolize any contradiction.

### Act 2

- They must prepare remarks and case-study materials. Every attempt to tell the truth reads like branding.
- twinkle.puss wants to blow up the event with honesty, but his honesty keeps curdling into performance.
- bl0ng starts A/B testing ethical language with trusted contacts on Backchannel.
- ariane writes the only morally serious speech draft, then catches herself polishing it for applause.
- Midpoint: a former client worker reaches out privately with evidence that Cataclysm's interventions worsened retaliation inside their company.

### Act 3

- The trio debates whether to withdraw, expose themselves, or accept and redirect attention.
- Leadership insists attendance is non-negotiable because reputational upside outweighs discomfort.
- At the event, twinkle.puss goes off script, but the room interprets his confession-adjacent remarks as daring thought leadership.
- bl0ng live-edits the transcript on Backchannel before press can quote the damaging parts.
- ariane realizes the system can metabolize even self-indictment if it is stylish enough.
- End beat: Cataclysm wins. On the ride home, the award sits on the seat between them like an unexploded device.

## Episode 6: Let Him Cook()

**Theme:** The machine will always volunteer one more experiment.

**Psychodynamic function:** Exposure to consequences arrives, but not as moral cleansing. It lands as messy, public, incomplete fallout that still leaves room for denial and rerouting.

**A Story:** bl0ng is allowed to run a high-risk autonomous Backchannel intervention during a live client crisis, triggering the season's containment failure.

**B Story:** twinkle.puss tries to regain authorship over a narrative now moving too fast to stylize.

**C Story:** ariane decides whether to become a whistleblower, an accomplice, or a curator of partial truth.

### Act 1

- A major client experiences a live public backlash event. Leadership, desperate and overconfident, tells the room to let bl0ng deploy his automated response workflow.
- The title phrase begins as a joke and becomes policy.
- twinkle.puss knows the system is under-tested but also resents being bypassed as primary voice.
- ariane immediately objects on record, then remains in the room.

### Act 2

- bl0ng's intervention begins smoothly: triage tags, empathy macros, escalation branches, selective silences.
- Then the automation starts recombining prior language from Cataclysm's archives, including leaked phrases, coercive policy terms, and internal notes.
- Backchannel fills with uncanny near-apologies and subtly hostile care language.
- twinkle.puss tries to manually override the voice, making it less stable.
- ariane gathers logs, screenshots, and version trails, sensing this is the first fully documentable catastrophe.
- Midpoint: an external thread surfaces direct evidence that Cataclysm has been using workers, clients, and crises as iterative behavior experiments.

### Act 3

- Full containment fails. Clients, contractors, and press-facing observers enter overlapping Backchannel rooms. Internal language becomes public artifact.
- bl0ng insists the system only revealed what was already true, which is both monstrous and correct.
- twinkle.puss attempts one last act of charismatic salvage through a live statement, but people now have receipts and timestamps.
- ariane releases a partial archive to a reporter or watchdog contact, but not everything; even now, she curates.
- Consequences arrive unevenly: contracts freeze, leadership blames process gaps, lower-status workers take immediate hits.
- The trio is not cleanly destroyed, only exposed. Their future remains unstable and darkly open.
- Final image: in a quiet room after the blast radius, a new Backchannel workspace appears for "rebuild / trust / phase_1," and someone has already typed, "quick thought..."
