## Org Design Principles

WE OUT HERE is being built as an indie professional production with a hybrid operating model:

- remote development and planning work happen first
- prep and physical production are coordinated through a central production team
- post-production mixes remote workflows with scheduled review sessions
- creative decisions stay centralized, but execution authority is delegated to qualified department heads

The structure below assumes a lean core team in development, then a controlled expansion in prep, production, and post.

## Authority Map

| Area | Final Decision Owner | Required Input | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Creative direction | Showrunner | EP, Director, Head Writer | Showrunner owns story, tone, character integrity, and final creative calls. |
| Budget lane and greenlight | Executive Producer | Showrunner, Line Producer, Finance | EP decides what level of spend is approved. |
| Hiring department heads | Executive Producer | Showrunner, Line Producer, relevant lead | No department head is hired without EP approval. |
| Hiring below-the-line crew | Line Producer | Department Head, Production Manager | For key creative hires, Showrunner may request review. |
| Shooting schedule | Line Producer | 1st AD, Director, DP, Production Manager | Final schedule must fit budget, safety, and location realities. |
| On-set safety | Line Producer | 1st AD, Department Heads | Safety concerns can be escalated by anyone and must be acted on immediately. |
| Casting recommendations | Showrunner | Casting Director, Director, EP | Final cast approvals route through Showrunner and EP. |
| Editorial picture decisions | Showrunner | Director, Editor, Post Supervisor | Practical delivery constraints route through Post Supervisor. |
| Workplace conduct response | Executive Producer | HR support, Legal if needed | Serious complaints may bypass normal chain if conflict exists. |

## Hiring Sequence

The intended order of attachment is:

1. Executive Producer / Producing Lead and Showrunner
2. Line Producer
3. Casting Director and Production Accountant support
4. Director, if not already the Showrunner
5. Production Manager and Production Coordinator
6. Core department heads
7. Production crew
8. Post Supervisor and Editor
9. Remaining post vendors and collaborators

## Escalation Paths

Use the shortest path that protects people and the work.

- Creative disagreement: department head -> Director -> Showrunner
- Budget or scope problem: department head -> Production Manager -> Line Producer -> EP
- Schedule problem: crew member -> department head -> 1st AD / Production Manager
- Conduct issue: any team member -> HR contact, EP, or alternate reporting path in policy docs
- Safety issue: immediate escalation to 1st AD and Line Producer; work pauses if needed

## Span of Control Guidelines

- No one should directly manage more than 8 active reports during prep or production without coordinator support.
- Production Coordinator support becomes mandatory once more than 12 crew members are attached.
- A dedicated Post Supervisor is required if the series uses more than one editor, outside sound, VFX cleanup, or platform deliverables.
- If remote contributors span more than 3 time zones, one designated coordination owner must publish a weekly status summary.

## Operating Notes

- This org chart is intentionally lean. It is meant for an indie professional series, not a studio-size staffing model.
- Some people may hold more than one function early in development, but approval authority should still remain explicit.
- No one should serve as both the sole HR escalation point and the subject of a complaint. Alternate reporting routes must always exist.
