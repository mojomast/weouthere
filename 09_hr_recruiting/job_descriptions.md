## Hiring Notes

These descriptions are written for a lean indie professional series. They can be used as:

- direct outreach summaries
- posting copy for job boards and crew networks
- baseline scope for offer memos

Each role should also receive project-specific details on dates, rate, location expectations, and reporting line before posting.

## 1. Line Producer

**Reports to:** Executive Producer  
**Phase:** Development through wrap  
**Work model:** Hybrid remote + in-person production leadership

**Role summary**

Own the practical build of WE OUT HERE. The Line Producer translates creative ambition into a realistic budget, schedule, staffing plan, and production workflow.

**Key responsibilities**

- build and maintain budget and schedule assumptions
- recommend staffing levels and hiring sequence
- supervise Production Manager and production office operations
- approve vendors, rentals, and operational spend within authority
- flag budget, safety, and schedule risks early
- partner with EP and Showrunner on tradeoffs

**Must have**

- professional episodic, indie feature, or premium digital production experience
- strong budgeting and scheduling judgment
- calm communication under pressure
- experience hiring and supervising crew

**Success looks like**

- the show is staffed appropriately for the real workload
- prep stays ahead of production
- tradeoffs are surfaced before they become emergencies

## 2. Production Manager

**Reports to:** Line Producer  
**Phase:** Prep through wrap  
**Work model:** Hybrid in prep, mostly in-person during production

**Role summary**

Run day-to-day production logistics. The Production Manager keeps departments aligned, vendors moving, paperwork current, and the schedule operational.

**Key responsibilities**

- own prep calendars and logistics tracking
- manage vendors, rentals, permits, and day-to-day production operations
- coordinate with department heads on needs, deadlines, and changes
- support hiring paperwork and start logistics
- maintain production office discipline and information flow

**Must have**

- production management or strong coordinator experience
- strong organizational systems
- ability to juggle changing priorities without losing detail

**Success looks like**

- departments know what is happening and what is due
- paperwork is not drifting behind the actual production
- set days are supported by prep, not sabotaged by it

## 3. Production Coordinator

**Reports to:** Production Manager  
**Phase:** Prep through wrap  
**Work model:** Hybrid in prep, in-person or production-office based during shoot

**Role summary**

Keep communications, calendars, documents, and crew logistics moving. This role is the operational glue of the production office.

**Key responsibilities**

- manage crew communications and onboarding logistics
- maintain contact lists, paperwork trackers, and call-related admin
- support travel, start packets, and vendor follow-up
- route documents for signature and filing

**Must have**

- clear written communication
- strong admin discipline
- comfort with production docs, spreadsheets, and fast updates

## 4. Casting Director

**Reports to:** Showrunner  
**Phase:** Packaging through principal casting  
**Work model:** Mostly remote with live sessions as needed

**Role summary**

Lead the strategy and process for identifying cast who can carry the show's tone: smart, internet-literate, psychologically grounded, and funny without flattening the characters into sketch types.

**Key responsibilities**

- refine role breakdowns with creative leadership
- source talent across film, TV, theater, comedy, and digital-native communities
- run submissions, outreach, and audition logistics
- present calibrated options, not random volume
- support offer and hold communication

**Must have**

- strong taste and a deep network
- experience calibrating tone-sensitive comedy and drama
- organized session management

## 5. Director

**Reports to:** Showrunner for creative alignment; collaborates with Line Producer on execution  
**Phase:** Prep through production, plus select post reviews  
**Work model:** Hybrid prep, in-person production

**Role summary**

Translate the scripts into a shootable visual and performance language that preserves the show's hybrid form: desktop realism, live action, and surreal inserts.

**Key responsibilities**

- partner with Showrunner on tone and performance calibration
- lead prep with DP, Production Designer, AD team, and cast
- direct principal photography safely and efficiently
- support editor and Showrunner with creative notes in post

**Must have**

- experience balancing comedy, performance nuance, and practical coverage
- comfort with hybrid or screen-based storytelling
- collaborative leadership style

## 6. 1st Assistant Director

**Reports to:** Line Producer operationally; works closely with Director  
**Phase:** Prep through wrap  
**Work model:** In-person during production, active in prep scheduling

**Role summary**

Run the set safely and on time. The 1st AD converts the schedule into a workable day plan and keeps the company moving.

**Key responsibilities**

- build and update stripboards and day plans
- manage call times, set discipline, and company moves
- support safety communication and escalation
- help production make practical decisions under time pressure

**Must have**

- strong scheduling skills
- respected on-set communication style
- safety-minded leadership

## 7. Director of Photography

**Reports to:** Director  
**Phase:** Prep through shoot  
**Work model:** Hybrid prep, in-person production

**Role summary**

Own camera and lighting strategy for a show that must feel precise, contemporary, and achievable within indie constraints.

**Key responsibilities**

- design visual language with Director and Showrunner
- recommend camera, lens, lighting, and crew package
- lead camera and lighting teams on set
- protect image consistency across live action and screen-based work

**Must have**

- strong visual judgment
- practical indie execution skills
- experience collaborating with editorial and post needs

## 8. Production Designer

**Reports to:** Director; coordinates closely with Line Producer and Production Manager  
**Phase:** Prep through shoot  
**Work model:** Hybrid prep, in-person production

**Role summary**

Shape the physical world of WE OUT HERE so it feels lived-in, specific, contemporary, and funny without becoming broad or fake.

**Key responsibilities**

- define location and set dressing approach
- lead props and environment planning
- coordinate with costume, hair/makeup, and camera departments
- work inside budget without losing specificity

**Must have**

- strong taste and practical problem-solving
- experience with character-driven environments
- ability to prep quickly and communicate clearly

## 9. Editor

**Reports to:** Showrunner with Post Supervisor on workflow matters  
**Phase:** Production through final picture lock  
**Work model:** Remote-friendly with scheduled live reviews

**Role summary**

Build the show in the cut. The Editor should be comfortable with performance nuance, rhythm-based comedy, screen media, and finding the emotional truth inside chaotic material.

**Key responsibilities**

- assemble episodes on schedule
- manage notes from Showrunner, Director, and producers
- prep turnovers with support from Assistant Editor or Post Supervisor
- help maintain pacing, clarity, and tonal control

**Must have**

- narrative editing experience
- strong instincts for comedy timing and performance
- organized project management and version control habits

## 10. Post Supervisor

**Reports to:** Line Producer and Showrunner  
**Phase:** Late prep through delivery  
**Work model:** Hybrid remote with key review sessions

**Role summary**

Own the post pipeline. This person connects editorial, sound, music, color, finishing, and final delivery so nothing falls through the cracks.

**Key responsibilities**

- create and maintain post schedule
- define workflows for media, turnovers, reviews, and delivery
- coordinate vendors and finishing milestones
- track approvals and final outputs

**Must have**

- post workflow and delivery experience
- strong communication and schedule discipline
- ability to translate technical constraints for non-technical stakeholders

## 11. Production Sound Mixer

**Reports to:** Line Producer  
**Phase:** Shoot  
**Work model:** In-person production

**Role summary**

Capture clean, usable production sound under fast-moving indie conditions.

**Key responsibilities**

- plan and execute location sound capture
- flag sound risks in prep and on the day
- coordinate with AD, camera, and post workflow

**Must have**

- strong field recording skills
- practical problem-solving in imperfect locations
- professionalism with cast and crew

## 12. Remote Production and Post Coordinator

**Reports to:** Production Manager in prep/production, Post Supervisor in post  
**Phase:** Prep through delivery  
**Work model:** Remote-heavy

**Role summary**

Support the hybrid workflow by keeping remote contributors informed, assets organized, and approvals visible.

**Key responsibilities**

- maintain status trackers and review calendars
- route cuts, links, and notes securely
- keep meeting notes and approvals current
- support remote collaborator onboarding and handoffs

**Must have**

- excellent written communication
- strong file and version discipline
- comfort working across time zones and departments

## Standard Expectations for All Roles

- work professionally and respectfully
- follow `09_hr_recruiting/code_of_conduct.md`
- follow `09_hr_recruiting/anti_harassment_policy.md`
- communicate risks early
- protect confidential material
