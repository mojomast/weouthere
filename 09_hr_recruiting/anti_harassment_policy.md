## Content

## Policy Statement

Harassment, discrimination, and retaliation are not allowed on WE OUT HERE.

This includes conduct by:

- founders and principals
- producers and managers
- cast and crew
- freelancers and contractors
- vendors, guests, and visitors

This policy applies whether the conduct happens:

- in person
- on set
- in auditions or rehearsals
- in remote calls and chat channels
- by email, text, or direct message
- during travel, meals, lodging, or after-hours events tied to the project

## Protected Characteristics

Harassment or discrimination based on legally protected characteristics is prohibited. Depending on jurisdiction, this may include:

- race or color
- ethnicity or national origin
- religion or creed
- sex, gender, gender identity, or gender expression
- sexual orientation
- age
- disability or medical condition
- pregnancy or caregiver status
- marital status
- veteran status
- any other status protected by applicable law

## What Harassment Can Look Like

Harassment can be verbal, physical, visual, digital, or situational. It does not need to happen more than once to be serious.

Examples include:

- slurs, insults, or degrading jokes
- comments about someone's body, identity, accent, disability, or appearance
- repeated unwanted romantic or sexual attention
- unwanted touching or blocking movement
- sexual comments, images, innuendo, or propositions
- mocking someone's pronouns, background, or religion
- isolating or undermining someone because of a protected characteristic
- hostile or degrading messages in project chats or review notes

## Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes:

- unwanted sexual advances
- requests for sexual favors
- sexual comments or jokes
- sharing sexual images or messages in work channels
- conditioning access, advancement, work, or favorable treatment on romantic or sexual compliance
- punishing someone for rejecting attention

Power imbalance matters. Conduct from a producer, director, department head, or lead performer can be especially coercive even if framed as joking or informal.

## Bullying and Abusive Conduct

Some behavior may violate this policy even if it does not target a protected characteristic.

Examples include:

- screaming at staff
- repeated humiliation in front of others
- threatening someone's job, credit, or access to punish disagreement
- hostile pile-ons in chat or meeting settings
- targeting junior staff because they are less able to push back

This conduct may be handled under this policy, the code of conduct, or both.

## Reporting Options

Anyone who experiences or witnesses harassment, discrimination, retaliation, or abusive conduct should report it as soon as they reasonably can.

Reports may be made to:

- direct manager or department head
- Production Manager
- Line Producer
- Executive Producer
- designated HR or people-ops contact
- alternate contact identified in onboarding materials

If the person receiving the complaint is involved in the issue, use a different reporting path.

## What To Include In A Report

People do not need to write a perfect report to be heard. If possible, include:

- what happened
- who was involved
- when and where it happened
- whether there were witnesses or messages
- whether immediate safety support is needed

## Response Process

WE OUT HERE will respond in a way that is proportionate to the situation and protective of the people involved.

Typical response steps:

1. Receive and document the report.
2. Assess immediate safety, schedule, and reporting-line issues.
3. Decide who needs to review the matter.
4. Gather information from relevant people and records.
5. Take interim measures if needed.
6. Decide on corrective action.
7. Communicate available outcome information to the extent appropriate.

## Interim Measures

When needed, interim steps may include:

- separating people on set or in reporting lines
- changing meeting or call structure
- removing someone from a channel, location, or session
- pausing an engagement while review happens
- providing support around schedule, transportation, or safety planning

## Confidentiality and Privacy

Reports will be handled as privately as possible, but absolute confidentiality cannot be promised. Information will be shared only with people who need it to respond, investigate, protect safety, or comply with the law.

## Anti-Retaliation

Retaliation is prohibited against anyone who:

- makes a good-faith report
- participates in a review
- provides information as a witness
- asks for help under this policy

Good-faith means the person honestly believed the concern was real, even if the review cannot confirm every detail.

Lack of proof is not the same thing as a false report.

## Corrective Action

Depending on the facts, corrective action may include:

- coaching or mandatory policy review
- warning or final warning
- removal of supervisory authority
- removal from set or project channels
- suspension
- termination of engagement
- referral to law enforcement or emergency services when necessary

## Manager Duties

Managers, producers, and department heads must:

- act when they see or hear about possible harassment
- not promise secrecy they cannot keep
- not pressure people to stay quiet for the good of the project
- escalate serious concerns promptly

## No Waiver By Informality

WE OUT HERE may have a flexible tone and a close-working creative culture. That does not waive consent, legal obligations, or basic respect.
