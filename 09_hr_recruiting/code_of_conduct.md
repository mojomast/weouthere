## Content

## Statement of Intent

WE OUT HERE is an indie professional production. That means we are building a real workplace, not an excuse for chaos, disrespect, or boundary collapse.

Everyone on the project deserves:

- respect
- clarity
- safety
- professional treatment
- a way to raise concerns without being punished for it

This code applies to cast, crew, founders, producers, contractors, vendors, collaborators, and guests while participating in project work.

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- remote work sessions
- meetings and calls
- rehearsals and auditions
- prep, production, and post work
- work travel, lodging, and meals tied to the project
- project chats, email, review links, and shared folders
- social events that are production-related or attended in a work capacity

## Expected Conduct

Everyone working on WE OUT HERE is expected to:

- treat people with respect regardless of role or status
- communicate clearly and professionally
- honor boundaries around time, privacy, and personal dignity
- follow lawful instructions and safety requirements
- raise concerns early instead of letting resentment or risk build quietly
- protect confidential scripts, footage, documents, and personal information
- help maintain a workplace where people can do strong work without being humiliated

## Unacceptable Conduct

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- harassment, discrimination, or retaliation
- bullying, intimidation, or deliberate humiliation
- threats, yelling, or abusive language directed at a person
- sexual comments, unwanted flirting, or pressure for personal attention
- racist, sexist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or otherwise degrading language
- repeated disrespect toward coordinators, assistants, PAs, or junior crew
- sabotaging another person's work, credit, or access
- sharing confidential material without permission
- showing up impaired in a way that creates risk or disruption
- using authority to pressure someone into personal, romantic, financial, or social compliance

## Professional Boundaries

- Creative intensity is not a free pass for bad behavior.
- Fast schedules are not a reason to insult people.
- Informal team culture is not a reason to ignore consent or professionalism.
- Private jokes are not harmless if they isolate or demean someone.

## Reporting Options

Anyone can raise a concern through any of the following:

- direct manager or department head
- Production Manager or Line Producer
- Executive Producer
- designated HR contact or onboarding contact
- alternate reporting contact if the normal chain is involved in the issue

If someone feels unsafe using the normal reporting path, they should use an alternate path immediately.

## Response Expectations

When a concern is raised, WE OUT HERE will:

- take the report seriously
- assess immediate safety and workplace needs
- limit information-sharing to people who need to respond
- document next steps
- avoid retaliation against the reporting person or witnesses

Not every concern will be handled the same way, but every good-faith report should be treated respectfully.

## Anti-Retaliation

Retaliation is prohibited.

That includes punishing someone for:

- making a good-faith report
- asking for help
- participating in an inquiry
- supporting another person who raised a concern

Retaliation may include exclusion, threats, schedule punishment, credit interference, reduced access, or social pressure. It will be treated as a separate policy violation.

## Accountability

Violations may result in:

- coaching or warning
- removal from certain duties
- change in reporting structure
- suspension from work
- termination of engagement
- removal from set or work channels
- escalation to legal, payroll, or outside authorities if required

Responses depend on severity, pattern, and immediate risk.

## Manager Responsibilities

Anyone supervising others is expected to:

- model professional behavior
- stop disrespect when they see it
- not minimize complaints because the person is talented or important
- escalate issues promptly when policy, safety, or legal risk is involved

## Acknowledgment

All hires should acknowledge that they have received and understood this policy before starting work.
