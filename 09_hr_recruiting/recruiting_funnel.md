## Recruiting Principles

WE OUT HERE should recruit like a real production company, not a vague creative collective.

- respond quickly
- describe the work honestly
- pay people fairly for real work
- keep decision-makers visible
- do not waste candidate time
- document decisions well enough to defend them later

## Funnel Overview

| Stage | Owner | Output | Target turnaround |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Requisition approval | EP + Line Producer + hiring lead | Approved search brief | 2 business days |
| 2. Sourcing | HR support + hiring lead | Active candidate pool | 5 business days to first shortlist |
| 3. Resume / reel review | Hiring lead + HR support | Screen list | 3 business days |
| 4. Initial screening | HR support or hiring lead | Qualified shortlist | 5 business days |
| 5. Hiring panel interview | Hiring lead + cross-functional reviewer | Finalists | 5 business days |
| 6. References / verification | HR support | Verified finalist | 3 business days |
| 7. Offer and negotiation | EP or Line Producer | Accepted or declined offer | 3 business days |
| 8. Handoff to onboarding | HR support + coordinator | Start-ready hire | 2 business days |

## Stage Details

### 1. Requisition Approval

Before any role opens, confirm:

- role title and reporting line
- employee or contractor assumption
- pay structure or target rate band
- start window and duration
- who interviews and who decides
- whether the role is essential now or should wait

No search should open without this brief.

### 2. Sourcing

Use multiple channels depending on role:

- direct outreach through trusted crew networks
- referrals from department heads and producers
- casting submissions for talent roles
- targeted job boards and production communities
- festival, theater, comedy, and digital-creator circles when role fit justifies it

For each role, build a slate with a mix of:

- proven candidates
- credible step-up candidates
- new or nontraditional candidates who still meet the actual bar

### 3. Resume / Reel Review

Review against the role scorecard, not vague vibes.

Screen for:

- relevant credits or analogous experience
- clear communication
- evidence of reliability
- fit for hybrid production workflow
- tone alignment where creative judgment matters

### 4. Initial Screening

The first screen should answer five questions:

1. Can this person actually do the work?
2. Can they work the dates and location model?
3. Are their rate expectations realistic for the lane?
4. Do they communicate like someone the team can trust under pressure?
5. Are there any immediate red flags around conduct or professionalism?

### 5. Hiring Panel Interview

Every finalist interview should assess four categories:

| Category | What to test |
| --- | --- |
| Craft | Can they explain how they work and solve problems? |
| Judgment | Do they make practical decisions under real constraints? |
| Collaboration | Can they work with a lean team without ego or chaos? |
| Fit for this project | Do they understand the tone, format, and hybrid workflow? |

Panel size should stay small. Usually 2 to 4 people is enough.

### 6. References and Verification

Run references for final candidates before offer or before final confirmation, depending on urgency.

At minimum verify:

- role and dates
- reliability and professionalism
- communication style under pressure
- whether the person would be rehired

Do not ask illegal or irrelevant personal questions.

### 7. Offer and Negotiation

Offers should be verbal first, then written promptly.

Every written offer should include:

- title
- scope and reporting line
- start date and term
- pay structure
- expected work model
- condition that formal paperwork must be completed before start

### 8. Handoff to Onboarding

The recruiting process is not complete until the hire has:

- signed their agreement
- submitted required paperwork
- received policy documents
- been added to the onboarding tracker

## Scorecard Template

Use a 1 to 5 scale for each category.

| Category | Weight | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Relevant experience | 25 | Similar productions, role scope, complexity |
| Practical judgment | 20 | Can solve real problems without drama |
| Communication | 20 | Clear, timely, professional |
| Collaboration and conduct | 20 | Respectful, accountable, low-ego |
| Project-specific fit | 15 | Understands hybrid workflow and tone |

Minimum recommendation:

- no hire if collaboration and conduct scores below 3
- no hire if practical judgment scores below 3 for leadership roles
- final written rationale required for every hire decision

## Candidate Experience Standards

- acknowledge serious applicants within 3 business days when possible
- do not ghost finalists
- do not request unpaid spec work for crew hires
- if a task is necessary, keep it short and pay for it when it goes beyond normal interview materials
- be honest about timelines, budget lane, and uncertainty

## Diversity and Access Practices

- source beyond the same five referral loops
- avoid filtering only for studio-style credits if the actual work can be handled by a strong indie candidate
- provide remote interview options whenever possible
- judge experience in context, not only by prestige markers

## Red Flags

Escalate or stop the process if any of the following appear:

- repeated disrespect toward coordinators or junior staff
- inability to discuss past work concretely
- evasiveness about dates, rates, or availability
- credible conduct concerns from references or prior collaborators
- pressure to start work before paperwork is complete

## Recruiting Metrics

Track the following per role:

- days from role approval to first shortlist
- days from first interview to decision
- offer acceptance rate
- source of hire
- dropout reasons
- post-hire quality after 30 days or project milestone

## Candidate Status Labels

Use simple labels in the tracker:

- Sourced
- Screened
- Interviewing
- Finalist
- References
- Offer Out
- Hired
- Declined
- Closed
