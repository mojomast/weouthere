## Content

## Onboarding Goals

Every new hire should finish onboarding with five things:

- a signed agreement
- clear expectations
- the right tools and contacts
- policy awareness
- a real point of contact for questions or problems

## Onboarding Owners

| Area | Owner |
| --- | --- |
| Agreement and paperwork | HR support + Production Coordinator |
| Access to tools and folders | Ops support or assigned coordinator |
| Role-specific kickoff | Direct manager or department head |
| Workplace policy review | HR support or EP delegate |
| Payroll and payment setup | Payroll / bookkeeping support |

## Pre-Start Checklist

Complete before the first paid workday.

- signed offer or engagement agreement received
- tax and payment paperwork completed
- emergency contact collected where appropriate
- confidentiality and IP paperwork completed if required
- code of conduct and anti-harassment policy shared
- remote collaboration expectations shared if applicable
- reporting line and primary contact confirmed
- start date, schedule, and first meeting confirmed

## Standard Welcome Pack

Every hire should receive a welcome pack containing:

- short project overview for WE OUT HERE
- role summary and reporting line
- contact list for key people
- policy documents
- tool and file access instructions
- schedule for first week or first engagement block

## Day 1 Checklist

Day 1 should be simple and concrete.

1. Welcome and role context.
2. Confirm schedule, communication channel, and reporting line.
3. Review workplace expectations and escalation paths.
4. Confirm access to files, calendars, and messaging tools.
5. Confirm payment and paperwork status.
6. Assign immediate next steps for the first 3 to 5 working days.

## First Week Expectations

By the end of the first week, each hire should:

- know how decisions get made
- know who to ask for approvals
- know where key files live
- understand response-time expectations
- understand how to raise safety, scheduling, conduct, or creative concerns

## Role-Specific Onboarding

### For remote development and post collaborators

- provide a working folder structure
- provide file naming and version expectations
- explain note cycles and review cadence
- explain what should be async versus live discussion

### For prep and production crew

- review the production calendar and location assumptions
- review set expectations, safety chain, and paperwork flow
- clarify call-sheet distribution and day-of communication norms
- confirm meal, turnaround, overtime, and reimbursement rules

### For cast

- provide project overview and role-specific materials
- explain audition, fitting, rehearsal, and shoot communication flows
- identify producer and creative contacts
- confirm support path for wardrobe, transportation, accessibility, and concerns

## Workplace Policy Review

Every onboarding must include a plain-language review of:

- `09_hr_recruiting/code_of_conduct.md`
- `09_hr_recruiting/anti_harassment_policy.md`
- reporting options, including alternate reporting if the normal manager is part of the issue
- anti-retaliation expectations

Acknowledgment can be written electronically but should be logged.

## Access Setup

Only grant the access needed for the role.

Typical access categories:

- project email or approved communication channels
- scheduling and calendar invites
- file storage and folder permissions
- editing, review, or media links for post roles
- production documents for prep and set roles

Remove or reduce access at wrap or when scope ends.

## 30-Minute Kickoff Agenda

Use this for most new hires.

1. Introductions and role purpose
2. Current phase of the project
3. What success looks like in this role
4. Key deadlines or milestones
5. How we communicate and escalate issues
6. Questions and next actions

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- starting work before paperwork is done
- assuming a freelancer will figure out the org structure alone
- sending policy docs without explaining who to contact
- overloading Day 1 with too much background and no immediate tasks
- forgetting to offboard access at the end of engagement

## Offboarding Checklist

Use at wrap, end of term, or early exit.

- confirm final deliverables received
- confirm payment status and expense submission deadlines
- remove or reduce systems access
- collect company property if any
- document transition notes where needed
- provide an exit contact for payroll or credit questions
