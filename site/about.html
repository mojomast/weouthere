About the Series

Work, intimacy, and self-invention under AI-era pressure.

WE OUT HERE is a premium indie dark sitcom about three burned-out technologists who launch a chaotic AI consultancy from Backchannel and mistake fluency for safety.

Premise

Everyone in this world is trying to become undeniable. Some want money. Some want relevance. Some want cultural legitimacy. Some just want to stop feeling replaceable. The problem is that the same systems promising transcendence keep producing insecurity, self-surveillance, and new forms of intimacy failure.

The show follows `twinkle.puss`, `bl0ng`, and `ariane.emory` as they turn a group-chat joke into Cataclysm Consulting, a boutique AI consultancy built on speed, rhetoric, unstable judgment, and the market value of sounding certain before anyone has verified anything.

Tone

Smart and Internet-Literate

The show understands startup language, online ambition, consultant theater, and pseudo-technical status signaling from the inside.

Psychologically Sharp

The comedy comes from defense mechanisms, labor anxiety, failed containment, and the precise ways bright people stay unstable together.

Humane and Darkly Funny

The series is absurdist but emotionally grounded. It never drifts into issue-piece moralizing or generic tech satire.

Season Arc

Mania

A joke becomes a company.

Fragile Success

Legitimacy appears before structure.

Ethical Drift

Improvisation hardens into method.

Containment Failure

The search for structure exposes the damage.

Ironic Collapse

The moral infrastructure fails in public.

Exposure to Consequences

Reality makes contact without offering redemption.

Form

The production language combines desktop realism, live action, and surreal insert sequences. Backchannel is a real dramatic space, not a decorative messaging overlay. Chat is workplace, archive, Greek chorus, intimacy device, and future evidence trail.

Surreal inserts are used sparingly, when psychological reality breaches ordinary social form. They are there to sharpen stakes, not to replace them.
